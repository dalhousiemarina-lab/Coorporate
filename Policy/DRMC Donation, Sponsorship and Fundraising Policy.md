## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Sponsorship Policy is to establish guidelines for ethical, transparent, and effective practices regarding the acceptance, management, and acknowledgment of sponsorships at Dalhousie Regional Marina (DRM).

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all directors, officers, employees, volunteers, contractors, and representatives involved in the solicitation or acceptance of financial support on behalf of Dalhousie Regional Marina.

---

## 3. Definitions

| **Term** | **Definition** |
|----------|----------------|
| **Fundraising (The "Action")** | Any activity or organized effort intended to solicit present or future financial support, in-kind gifts, or the sale of goods and services to raise funds for DRM. This includes research, planning, execution, and donor stewardship, whether conducted by DRM representatives or third parties on its behalf. Fundraising is the entire effort organized by a nonprofit to secure resources. It includes the planning, the staff time, the software used to send emails, and the events themselves. <br><br>**Note:** Fundraising explicitly includes Gaming Activities (raffles, 50/50s, etc.) where proceeds are directed toward the Marina's mission.<br><br>**Example:** Organizing a "Community Fun Run" or an "Annual Raffle" is a fundraising activity. |
| **Sponsorship (The "Business Deal")** | A mutually beneficial business arrangement where an individual or corporation provides financial support or products/services in exchange for specified promotional benefits (advertising). A sponsorship occurs when a business provides money or goods to a nonprofit in exchange for advertising or promotion.<br><br>**Tax Benefit:** The business cannot get a charitable tax receipt. Instead, they deduct the cost as a Marketing/Advertising Expense.<br><br>**Why businesses do it:** They want their logo on your jerseys, their banner at your gala, or "Presented by [Company Name]" in your radio ads.<br><br>**CRA Warning:** If a charity issues a tax receipt for a sponsorship, they are at risk of an audit. The CRA views this as the charity "selling" advertising space rather than receiving a gift. |
| **Donation (The "Gift")** | A voluntary transfer of property (cash or goods) made without expectation of return or material benefit (consideration). While DRM accepts such support, as a non-charitable NPO, these contributions do not qualify for official charitable tax receipts. A donation is a voluntary transfer of property where the donor expects nothing in return.<br><br>**Tax Benefit:** Because it is a gift, a registered charity can issue an Official Tax Receipt, which the donor uses to reduce their personal or corporate income tax.<br><br>**Rule of Thumb:** If you put a donor's name on a small "wall of thanks" or in a newsletter, it is still a donation. |
| **Gift-In-Kind** | A contribution of tangible goods or professional services rather than cash. |
| **Third-Party Fundraising & Cause Marketing** | Initiatives organized by external parties (e.g., a business donating a portion of sales) to benefit DRM. |
| **Gaming Activities** | Any fundraising involving chance, including 50/50 draws, raffles, or lotteries. All such activities must strictly adhere to the New Brunswick Gaming Control Act and federal Competition Act requirements. |

## 5. Acceptance of Support

### 5.1 Non-Profit Status

DRM is a registered **Non-Profit Organization (NPO)** in New Brunswick. It is **not a registered charity** and cannot issue official tax receipts.

### 5.2 Restriction on Donations

⚠️ **IMPORTANT RESTRICTION** ⚠️

DRM **cannot accept general philanthropic donations**. All support must be structured as a **Sponsorship** with an exchange of promotional value.

### 5.3 Restriction on Fundraising

⛔ **CURRENT RESTRICTION** ⛔

DRM **cannot support fundraising** as of the creation of this document. During the 2026 season, we will review the methodology to support it.

### 5.4 Approval Thresholds

| **Sponsorship Value** | **Approval Required** |
|-----------------------|----------------------|
| Up to $50 | Treasurer |
| $51 - $500 | Board of Directors |
| Over $500 or restricted conditions | Board of Directors (unanimous consent recommended) |

### 5.5 Authority to Bind

Only the **Treasurer** or a **designated Board Officer** has the legal authority to sign sponsorship agreements.

## 6. Recognition and Business Exchanges

### 6.1 Consistent Application

All sponsors within the same tier shall receive the same benefits, as outlined in the DRM Sponsorship Tier Schedule (maintained by the Treasurer).

| **Tier** | **Contribution Level** | **Benefits** |
|----------|------------------------|--------------|
| Bronze | $50 - $100 | Name on website sponsor page |
| Silver | $101 - $250 | Bronze benefits + logo in newsletter |
| Gold | $251 - $500 | Silver benefits + banner at marina events |
| Platinum | $501+ | Gold benefits + "Presented by" recognition for one event |

### 6.2 Business Deductibility

As DRM provides advertising services, contributions may be deductible by the sponsor as a **business expense** (advertising/marketing). DRM will provide a receipt for services rendered, **not** a charitable tax receipt.

---

## 7. Intellectual Property

### 7.1 Use of Marina Logo

Any use of the DRM name or logo by a third party must be approved in writing by the Board.

### 7.2 Logo Usage Guidelines

When approved, third parties must:
- Use only official DRM logo files provided by the marina
- Not modify or alter the logo
- Submit all materials featuring the logo for Board review prior to publication
- Cease use of the logo upon termination of the sponsorship agreement

---

## 8. Accounting Governance

### 8.1 Deposit Protocol

All contributions must be deposited into the official bank account within **five (5) business days** of receipt.

### 8.2 Petty Cash

Small cash amounts for workshop expenses must be reconciled by the Treasurer **twice per month**.

### 8.3 Tracking (SAGE)

Every sponsorship must be assigned a unique tracking number in SAGE using the format:

DRM-202x-xxx

Example: DRM-2026-001 (first sponsorship of 2026)


### 8.4 Audit Trail

DRM shall maintain all financial records and evidence of impact for **seven (7) years** in accordance with CRA requirements.

### 8.5 Gift-In-Kind Valuation

| **Value** | **Requirement** |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Under $500 | Reasonable estimate by recipient |
| Over $500 | Commercial invoice or independent appraisal required |

## 9. Legal Compliance (Digital & Gaming)

### 9.1 Anti-Spam (CASL)

DRM must:
- Obtain consent before sending electronic messages
- Identify themselves clearly in all messages
- Provide a functional "unsubscribe" link in all commercial electronic messages
- Honour unsubscribe requests within 10 business days

### 9.2 Gaming as Fundraising

DRM recognizes that licensed gaming activities are a vital fundraising method. All revenue generated through these activities (less prizes and approved expenses) is used solely to support the Marina's social welfare and community improvement objectives.

### 9.3 Gaming Licenses

⚠️ **CRITICAL REQUIREMENT** ⚠️

Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd. **does not own or hold a permanent gambling or gaming license**. A specific license must be applied for and obtained through the **NB Gaming, Liquor and Security Licensing Branch** for **every individual event** involving gaming activities.

| **License Type** | **Requirement** | **Fee** | **Application Deadline** |
|------------------|-----------------|---------|--------------------------|
| **Class A License** | Total prizes > $500 | $50 | 30 days prior to event |
| **Class B License** | Total prizes ≤ $500 | $20 | 15 days prior to event |

### 9.4 Minimum Age

In accordance with New Brunswick law, **no person under the age of 19** may purchase a ticket or participate in any gaming activity.

### 9.5 Competition Act Disclosure

All promotional materials must clearly disclose:
- The number and approximate value of prizes
- The geographic area to which they relate
- Any fact that materially affects the odds/chances of winning
- No purchase necessary rules (if applicable)
- Skill testing question requirements (for contests of chance)

### 9.6 Gaming Reporting

Gaming reports must be filed with provincial authorities within **30 days** post-event, including:
- Total tickets sold
- Total revenue
- Prize payouts
- Expenses
- Net proceeds

## 10. Social Media and Digital Campaigns

### 10.1 Content Standards

All digital content must be:
- Truthful and accurate
- Clearly identified as originating from DRM
- Include a clear call to action
- Compliant with CASL requirements
- Accessible to persons with disabilities (where practicable)

### 10.2 Peer-to-Peer Campaigns

Supporters may create digital pages for DRM, provided they explicitly state:

> **"Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd. is a registered Non-Profit Organization in New Brunswick. We are not a registered charity and cannot issue tax receipts. Your support helps us maintain and improve our community marina and campground facilities."**

### 10.3 Social Media Monitoring

The Treasurer or designate shall monitor all DRM-affiliated social media campaigns to ensure:
- Compliance with this policy
- Accurate representation of DRM
- Appropriate responses to public inquiries
- No unauthorized representations

---

## 11. Corporate Partnerships and Cause Marketing

### 11.1 Contracts

All "Cause Marketing" arrangements (e.g., a business donating a portion of sales to DRM) must be documented with a **written agreement** signed by both parties.

### 11.2 Agreement Elements

Each cause marketing agreement shall include:
- Duration of the campaign
- Percentage or fixed amount to be donated
- How and when funds will be transferred
- How the promotion will be advertised
- Termination clauses
- Intellectual property usage rights
- Reporting requirements

### 11.3 Due Diligence

The Board will screen potential partners to ensure:
- Alignment with the Marina's mission and values
- No reputational risks
- Compliance with applicable laws
- Financial stability
- No conflicts of interest

---

## 12. Ethics and Privacy

### 12.1 Coordination of Outreach

All outreach to potential sponsors must be coordinated through the **Treasurer** to prevent:
- Duplicate solicitations
- Donor fatigue
- Conflicting messaging
- Unauthorized representations

### 12.2 Privacy Protection

DRM will:
- **Not** sell or trade supporter lists
- Collect only necessary information
- Store supporter data securely
- Provide access to supporters' own data upon request
- Delete data upon supporter request (subject to record retention requirements)

### 12.3 Conflict of Interest

Any Board member, officer, or volunteer with a personal interest in a potential sponsorship must:
- Disclose the interest in writing
- Recuse themselves from related discussions and decisions
- Document their recusal in meeting minutes

---

## 13. Use of Funds and Accountability

### 13.1 Use of Funds

Funds raised must be used **exclusively** for DRM's stated non-profit purposes:
- Social welfare
- Civic improvement
- Recreation
- Marina and campground operations and improvements

### 13.2 Restricted Funds

Any funds raised for specific projects (e.g., "Dock Expansion Fund", "Campground Improvement Fund") must be used for that intended purpose.

⚠️ **WARNING**: Misuse of funds can jeopardize DRM's tax-exempt status as an NPO.

### 13.3 Transparency

DRM will provide clear information to supporters regarding how much of the sponsorship revenue goes directly to marina projects versus operational or administrative costs.

### 13.4 Reporting to Supporters

Upon request, DRM will provide sponsors with:
- Confirmation of funds received
- Acknowledgment of promotional benefits provided
- Summary of how funds were used (for significant contributions)

---

## 14. Reporting and CRA Compliance

### 14.1 Record Keeping Requirements

DRM is required to keep accurate financial records of all sponsorship and fundraising activities, including:
- All receipts and disbursements
- Contracts and agreements
- Banking records
- Gaming licenses and reports
- Sponsor correspondence

### 14.2 Annual Filings

| **Filing** | **Requirement** | **Due Date** |
|------------|-----------------|--------------|
| T2 Corporation Income Tax Return | All NPOs | 6 months after year-end |
| T1044 Non-Profit Organization Information Return | If assets > $200,000 or revenue > $50,000 | Within 6 months of year-end |

### 14.3 Audit/Review

Records must be maintained to facilitate potential audits or financial reviews to ensure continued accountability to members and stakeholders.

### 14.4 Document Retention

All records related to sponsorships and fundraising shall be retained for a minimum of **seven (7) years** as required by CRA.

## 19. Violations and Enforcement

### 19.1 Unauthorized Solicitations

Any individual found to be soliciting funds on behalf of DRM without authorization shall:

- Be directed to cease immediately
- Have their actions reported to the Board
- Be subject to removal from volunteer positions
- Be subject to legal action if misrepresentation occurred

### 19.2 Misuse of Funds

Any misuse of sponsorship or gaming funds shall result in:

- Immediate reporting to the Board
- Possible reporting to law enforcement
- Restitution required
- Removal from any financial responsibilities
- Termination of membership or employment

### 19.3 Reporting Violations

Suspected violations should be reported to:

| **Contact** | **Method** |
|-------------|------------|
| Treasurer | treasurer@dalmarina.ca |
| Commodore | commodore@dalmarina.ca |
| Board of Directors | board@dalmarina.ca |
