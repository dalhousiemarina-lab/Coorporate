## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Procurement Policy is to establish a transparent, fair, and consistent process for acquiring goods and services on behalf of Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd. (DRMC). This policy ensures that all procurement activities deliver value for money, comply with legal and ethical standards, avoid conflicts of interest, and protect the Corporation's financial resources. It complements the spending authority limits set out in Bylaw Article XII.7 and the project approval process defined in Article X.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all purchases of goods and services made by DRMC, including:

- Operational supplies and materials.
- Equipment and tools.
- Contracted services (e.g., repairs, maintenance, professional services).
- Capital project expenditures (as defined in the Project Proposal Template).
- Any other acquisition of goods or services using Corporation funds.

This policy does not apply to:

- Routine reimbursements to volunteers or Executive members for approved out‑of‑pocket expenses (covered by the Expense Reimbursement Policy).
- Member fees, dues, or other revenue transactions.
- Emergency purchases as defined in Section 8.

---

## 3. Definitions

- **Procurement**: The process of acquiring goods or services, including needs identification, vendor selection, contracting, and payment.
- **Goods**: Tangible items purchased by the Corporation (e.g., lumber, hardware, office supplies).
- **Services**: Work performed by a third party (e.g., electrical repairs, accounting, dock construction).
- **Vendor**: A person or company supplying goods or services.
- **Sole Source Procurement**: Purchasing from a single vendor without competitive bidding, justified by unique circumstances.
- **Conflict of Interest**: A situation where a person involved in procurement has a personal or financial interest that could influence their decision.
- **Executive**: The Board of Directors of DRMC.
- **Treasurer**: The elected officer responsible for financial oversight.

---

## 4. Procurement Principles

All procurement activities shall be guided by the following principles:

- **Value for Money**: Purchases shall represent the best overall value, considering price, quality, suitability, and long‑term costs (not simply the lowest price).
- **Transparency**: The procurement process shall be open and documented, with clear criteria for vendor selection.
- **Fairness**: All potential vendors shall be treated equitably, with no undue preference or discrimination.
- **Accountability**: Those involved in procurement are responsible for adhering to this policy and maintaining proper records.
- **Conflict of Interest Avoidance**: Any actual or perceived conflict of interest shall be disclosed and managed appropriately.

---

## 5. Procurement Thresholds and Approval Levels

The following table defines the procurement thresholds and corresponding approval requirements. All amounts are in Canadian dollars.

| Estimated Value | Procurement Method | Approval Required | Documentation Required |
|-----------------|--------------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **$0 – $250** | Discretionary purchase | Squad Coordinator or Executive member (oral approval sufficient) | Receipt or invoice for payment |
| **$251 – $1,000** | Informal quotes (at least 2 verbal or written quotes encouraged) | Executive member (e.g., Treasurer, Harbour Master) | Brief memo or email record of quotes and selection rationale |
| **$1,001 – $2,500** | Written quotes from at least 3 vendors | Executive (simple majority vote) | Written quotes, selection memo, Executive meeting minutes |
| **$2,501 – $5,000** | Written quotes from at least 3 vendors | Executive (majority vote) plus notification to membership at next update meeting | Full documentation in project file or procurement log |
| **Over $5,000** | Formal competitive process (RFP or written quotes from at least 3 vendors) | Membership vote (if part of a project requiring approval per Bylaw XII.7) or Executive vote if within approved project budget | Complete procurement file, including quotes, evaluation criteria, and approval documentation |

**Notes**:
- For emergency purchases (see Section 8), these thresholds may be temporarily waived, but retroactive approval must be sought.
- For multi‑phase projects, the total project cost determines the approval level, not individual phase purchases.
- If a purchase is part of a previously member‑approved project budget, Executive approval within that budget is sufficient.

## 6. Vendor Selection Process

### 6.1 Competitive Quotes

For purchases exceeding $1,000, competitive quotes shall be obtained as follows:

1. **Identify Potential Vendors**: Seek at least three qualified vendors. Consider recommendations from members, online research, or previous experience.
2. **Provide Clear Specifications**: Prepare a clear description of the goods or services required, including quantities, delivery timelines, and any technical requirements.
3. **Request Quotes**: Contact vendors and request written quotes by a specified deadline.
4. **Evaluate Quotes**: Assess quotes based on pre‑defined criteria (e.g., price, experience, availability, warranty). Document the evaluation.
5. **Select Vendor**: Choose the vendor offering the best overall value. If the lowest price is not selected, document the reasons.
6. **Notify Vendors**: Inform all vendors of the outcome (optional but encouraged for good relations).

### 6.2 Formal Requests for Proposals (RFP)

For purchases exceeding $5,000 or for complex services, a formal RFP process may be used. The RFP shall include:

- Detailed scope of work.
- Evaluation criteria and weighting.
- Submission deadline.
- Terms and conditions.
- Conflict of interest declaration.

The evaluation shall be conducted by at least two individuals, and the results documented.

---

## 7. Sole Source Procurement

In limited circumstances, competitive bidding may be waived, and a single vendor selected directly. Sole source procurement is permitted only when:

- The required goods or services are available from only one vendor (e.g., proprietary parts, specialized expertise).
- An emergency threatens safety or property (see Section 8).
- A vendor has unique qualifications or experience essential to the project.
- The purchase is a follow‑on to a previous contract where continuity is critical.

Any sole source procurement must be justified in writing and approved by the Executive (for purchases over $1,000) or the Treasurer (for purchases $1,000 and under). The justification shall explain why competition was not feasible and confirm that the price is fair and reasonable.

---

## 8. Emergency Procurement

In the event of an immediate threat to safety, security, or property (e.g., storm damage, equipment failure), the Harbour Master, Campground Manager, or an Executive member may authorize emergency purchases without following the full competitive process. The following rules apply:

- The purchase shall be limited to what is necessary to address the immediate emergency.
- Oral approval from the Treasurer or Commodore should be obtained if possible.
- As soon as practicable (within 7 days), a written report shall be submitted to the Executive explaining the emergency, the actions taken, and the costs incurred.
- Retroactive approval shall be sought at the next Executive meeting.

---

## 9. Conflict of Interest

Any individual involved in procurement who has a personal or financial interest in a potential vendor must disclose that interest **immediately** and **in writing** to the Executive. Disclosure shall include:

- The nature of the relationship (e.g., family member, business partner, ownership interest).
- The vendor involved.
- The value of the proposed transaction.

Upon disclosure, the individual shall **recuse themselves** from all discussions, evaluations, and decisions related to that procurement. The Executive shall determine whether the interest is significant enough to warrant additional safeguards (e.g., obtaining an independent quote).

Failure to disclose a conflict of interest may result in disciplinary action, including removal from the Executive or membership termination.

Vendors shall also be asked to disclose any known conflicts of interest as part of the quote or proposal process.

---

## 10. Documentation and Record Keeping

All procurement activities must be documented to ensure transparency and auditability. The following records shall be maintained by the Treasurer or Secretary in the Document Repository:

- Quotes and proposals received.
- Evaluation notes and selection rationale.
- Purchase orders or contracts.
- Invoices and proof of payment.
- Any conflict of interest disclosures.
- Emergency procurement reports.

For purchases over $1,000, a **Procurement Summary Sheet** (Appendix A) shall be completed and filed.

Records shall be retained in accordance with the Document Retention Policy (typically 7 years for financial records).

---

## 11. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| **Squad Coordinators** | Identify needs; obtain quotes within delegated authority; ensure purchases align with approved budgets. |
| **Treasurer** | Oversee compliance with this policy; maintain procurement records; advise on thresholds and approval levels; process payments. |
| **Executive** | Approve purchases exceeding $1,000; review emergency procurements; resolve conflicts of interest; ensure member votes are obtained for large projects. |
| **Membership** | Vote on projects exceeding Executive spending authority per Bylaw XII.7. |

---

## 12. Compliance and Non‑Compliance

All individuals involved in procurement are expected to comply with this policy. Instances of non‑compliance (e.g., splitting purchases to avoid thresholds, failing to disclose conflicts) shall be reported to the Executive and may result in:

- Requirement to personally cover unauthorized expenditures.
- Disciplinary action, up to and including removal from position or membership termination.

---

## 13. Policy Review and Amendments

This policy shall be reviewed **annually** by the Executive. Amendments require a **two‑thirds (2/3) majority vote** of the Executive. Members shall be notified of significant changes.

---

## 14. Effective Date

This policy takes effect on **March 1, 2026**.
