## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Expense Reimbursement Policy is to establish a clear and strict framework regarding the use of personal funds for Corporation expenses. Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd. (DRMC) operates on a **no‑reimbursement basis** to ensure financial discipline, prevent unauthorized spending, and protect the Corporation's resources. This policy reinforces that all expenses must be pre‑approved and paid directly by the Corporation.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all individuals associated with DRMC, including:

- Executive members (Directors)
- Squad Coordinators and volunteers
- Members
- Contractors (unless otherwise specified in their contract)

It covers any out‑of‑pocket expenses incurred in connection with Corporation activities, including but not limited to supplies, materials, travel, meals, equipment, and services.

---

## 3. Core Principle: No Reimbursement

**DRMC does not reimburse any out‑of‑pocket expenses.**

Individuals who use personal funds for Corporation‑related purchases do so at their own risk and will not be reimbursed, regardless of the circumstances, unless an exception is expressly granted in writing **before** the expense is incurred.

This principle applies even if the expense was incurred in good faith or for an emergency. The only acceptable way to make purchases on behalf of the Corporation is through the procurement process described in Section 4.

---

## 4. How Expenses Must Be Handled

### 4.1 Pre‑Approved Direct Payment

All expenses must be approved in advance and paid directly by the Corporation using corporate funds. The process is:

1. **Identify Need**: Determine the goods or services required.
2. **Follow Procurement Policy**: Obtain necessary quotes and approvals per the Procurement Policy (thresholds, competitive quotes, etc.).
3. **Corporation Pays**: Once approved, the Treasurer or designated person arranges payment directly to the vendor (e.g., corporate credit card, cheque, electronic transfer). No personal funds should be used.

### 4.2 Corporate Credit Card

Where practical, the Corporation may maintain a corporate credit card for approved purchases. Use of the card is restricted to individuals authorized by the Executive. All credit card transactions must be supported by receipts and reconciled monthly by the Treasurer.

### 4.3 Direct Billing

Vendors may be directed to invoice the Corporation directly. Invoices must reference the approved purchase and be submitted to the Treasurer for payment.

---

## 5. Emergency Exception (Strictly Limited)

In the event of a genuine emergency that poses an immediate threat to safety, security, or property, and when it is impossible to obtain pre‑approval or use corporate payment methods, an individual **may** use personal funds. However:

- The individual must notify an Executive member **immediately** (by phone or text) and explain the situation.
- Retroactive approval must be sought at the next Executive meeting.
- Reimbursement is **not guaranteed**. The Executive will review the circumstances and may, at its sole discretion, authorize reimbursement if:
  - The expense was absolutely necessary.
  - The amount was reasonable.
  - No practical alternative existed.
- If reimbursement is denied, the individual bears the full cost.

This exception is intended for rare, genuine emergencies only. Routine purchases do not qualify.

---

## 6. Prohibited Expenses

The following expenses are never reimbursable and must never be incurred on behalf of the Corporation:

- Personal meals or refreshments (unless part of an approved event budget and paid directly by the Corporation).
- Travel expenses (mileage, fuel, parking, accommodation) unless specifically approved in advance as part of a budget and paid directly.
- Gifts or donations.
- Any expense not directly related to Corporation business.
- Any expense incurred without prior written approval.

---

## 7. Consequences of Unauthorized Spending

If an individual makes an unauthorized purchase using personal funds and seeks reimbursement:

- The request will be denied.
- The individual may be required to return any goods purchased with Corporation funds if they are in the individual's possession.
- Repeated violations may result in disciplinary action, including removal from volunteer positions, suspension of privileges, or membership termination.

---

## 8. Volunteer and Executive Expectations

All volunteers and Executive members are expected to:

- Understand and adhere to this policy.
- Never assume that personal expenses will be reimbursed.
- Always seek pre‑approval and use corporate payment methods.
- Report any suspected misuse of funds to the Treasurer or Commodore.

---

## 9. Policy Review and Amendments

This policy shall be reviewed **annually** by the Executive. Amendments require a **two‑thirds (2/3) majority vote** of the Executive. Members shall be notified of significant changes.

---

## 10. Effective Date

This policy takes effect on **March 1, 2026**.
