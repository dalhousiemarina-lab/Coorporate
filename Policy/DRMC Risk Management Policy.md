# Risk Management Policy

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Risk Management Policy is to establish a structured and proactive approach to identifying, assessing, mitigating, monitoring, and reporting risks that could affect **[Organization Name]** ’s ability to achieve its mission, objectives, and strategic goals. This policy promotes informed decision-making, protects the organization’s assets, reputation, stakeholders, and ensures compliance with applicable laws and regulations.

Effective risk management enables the organization to minimize potential negative impacts while maximizing opportunities.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all directors, officers, employees, volunteers, contractors, and any other individuals acting on behalf of **[Organization Name]** (collectively referred to as **"Personnel"** ). It covers all organizational activities, operations, programs, and decisions.

---

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Risk** | The effect of uncertainty on objectives, which can be positive (opportunities) or negative (threats). |
| **Risk Management** | Coordinated activities to direct and control the organization with regard to risk. |
| **Risk Assessment** | The process of identifying, analyzing, and evaluating risks. |
| **Risk Appetite** | The level and type of risk the organization is willing to accept. |
| **Risk Owner** | The individual or department responsible for managing a specific risk. |
| **Residual Risk** | The risk remaining after mitigation measures have been applied. |

---

## 4. Risk Management Framework

**[Organization Name]** adopts a risk management framework based on recognized standards (e.g., ISO 31000 or COSO). The framework includes the following key processes:

- **Risk Identification**
- **Risk Analysis and Evaluation**
- **Risk Treatment/Mitigation**
- **Monitoring and Review**
- **Communication and Consultation**

---

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **Board of Directors/Governing Body** | Oversees the risk management framework, approves the policy, sets risk appetite, and reviews high-level risks periodically. |
| **Executive Leadership (e.g., Executive Director/CEO)** | Implements the policy, ensures integration into operations, and reports significant risks to the Board. |
| **Risk Management Committee (if applicable)** | Coordinates risk management activities, reviews assessments, and recommends actions. |
| **Risk Owners/Managers** | Identify, assess, mitigate, and monitor risks within their areas of responsibility. |
| **All Personnel** | Report potential risks promptly and comply with risk management procedures. |

---

## 6. Risk Categories

Risks may include, but are not limited to:

- **Strategic** – Changes in funding, market, or mission alignment
- **Operational** – Program delivery, IT systems, supply chain
- **Financial** – Fraud, budgeting, investments
- **Compliance/Legal** – Regulatory changes, contracts
- **Reputational** – Public perception, media
- **Health and Safety** – Workplace incidents, volunteer safety
- **Information Security/Cyber** – Data breaches
- **Human Resources** – Staffing shortages, misconduct

---

## 7. Risk Management Process

### 7.1 Risk Identification

Personnel must proactively identify risks through regular reviews, audits, incident reports, brainstorming sessions, or external consultations.

### 7.2 Risk Assessment

- **Likelihood:** Probability of occurrence (Rare, Unlikely, Possible, Likely, Almost Certain)
- **Impact/Consequence:** Potential effect (Insignificant, Minor, Moderate, Major, Catastrophic)

Risks are prioritized using a risk matrix to determine overall risk level (Low, Medium, High, Extreme).

### 7.3 Risk Treatment

For each significant risk:

- **Avoid:** Eliminate the risk activity
- **Mitigate:** Implement controls to reduce likelihood or impact
- **Transfer:** Shift risk (e.g., through insurance or contracts)
- **Accept:** Retain the risk if within appetite and no feasible treatment

Document treatment plans, assign Risk Owners, and set timelines.

### 7.4 Monitoring and Review

- Risks are monitored ongoing through key risk indicators (KRIs) and periodic reviews
- The risk register is updated at least annually or following significant events
- Internal audits or external reviews may assess effectiveness

### 7.5 Reporting and Communication

- Significant risks are reported to leadership and the Board as needed
- Annual risk report presented to the Board
- Risks are communicated appropriately to stakeholders
