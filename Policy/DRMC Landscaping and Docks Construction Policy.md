## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Landscaping and Deck Construction Policy is to establish a clear framework for the approval, design, and construction of landscaping improvements and deck structures on property owned or leased by Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd. (DRMC). This policy ensures that all modifications:

- Comply with applicable municipal bylaws and building codes.
- Protect the integrity of the Corporation's infrastructure and utilities.
- Maintain a cohesive and aesthetically pleasing environment.
- Do not create safety hazards or interfere with marina operations.
- Preserve access for maintenance, emergency services, and adjacent members.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All members, tenants, and occupants of DRMC property, including seasonal campers, moorage members, and leaseholders.
- Any landscaping, hardscaping, or deck construction activities undertaken on DRMC premises.
- Both permanent and temporary structures, including decks, patios, fences, retaining walls, garden sheds, and planted gardens.

---

## 3. Definitions

- **Landscaping**: The modification of outdoor areas through planting, grading, hardscaping, or the addition of decorative elements.
- **Deck**: An elevated, open platform constructed of wood, composite, or other materials, typically attached to a camper, building, or freestanding.
- **Hardscaping**: Non-plant elements such as patios, walkways, retaining walls, gravel areas, and stonework.
- **Approved Materials**: Materials that meet DRMC specifications for durability, fire resistance, and aesthetic consistency.
- **Utilities**: Underground and above-ground services including water, sewer, electrical, gas, and telecommunications lines.
- **Setback**: The minimum distance a structure must be located from property lines, adjacent structures, or utility easements.
- **Allocated Spot**: The designated area assigned to a member, as defined in their moorage or camping agreement, including site boundaries and any common area setbacks.
- **Administrative Approval**: Approval granted by the Campground Manager or Harbour Master for projects that are contained entirely within the member's allocated spot and comply with all policy requirements.
- **Member Approval**: Approval granted by the Executive for projects that extend outside the member's allocated spot or involve shared spaces, requiring additional review and member consultation.

---

## 4. General Principles

All landscaping and deck projects must adhere to the following principles:

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Safety** | Projects shall not create trip hazards, fire risks, or obstruct emergency access. |
| **Utility Protection** | No excavation or construction shall occur over or near utility lines without prior approval and location verification. |
| **Access** | All projects must maintain clear access for maintenance vehicles, emergency services, and adjacent members. |
| **Aesthetics** | Projects should complement the natural coastal environment and maintain a consistent visual standard. |
| **Drainage** | Landscaping shall not alter natural drainage patterns or direct water toward adjacent sites or common areas. |
| **Compliance** | All projects must comply with applicable municipal, provincial, and federal regulations. |

## 5. Approval Process

### 5.1 When Approval Is Required

Approval is required for the following activities:

- Construction of any deck, porch, or raised platform.
- Installation of fences, retaining walls, or privacy screens.
- Installation of patios, walkways, or gravel areas exceeding 50 square feet.
- Planting of trees, large shrubs, or any vegetation that may impact sightlines or utilities.
- Any excavation or grading.
- Installation of garden sheds, gazebos, or outbuildings.

### 5.2 When Approval Is Not Required

Approval is not required for:

- Routine lawn maintenance and weeding.
- Planting of small annual flowers or non-invasive ground cover (under 10 square feet).
- Placement of portable planters, chairs, or decorative items that do not obstruct access.

### 5.3 Approval Levels

Approval is categorized based on whether the proposed work is contained within the member's allocated spot:

| Approval Level | Authority | Applicable When | Process |
|----------------|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| **Administrative Approval** | Campground Manager or Harbour Master | The entire project is contained within the member's allocated spot and complies with all policy requirements. | Application reviewed and approved by the Campground Manager or Harbour Master. Decision provided within 14 days. |
| **Member Approval** | Executive | The project extends outside the member's allocated spot, encroaches on common areas, or affects adjacent members. | Application reviewed by the Campground Manager or Harbour Master, then forwarded to Executive with recommendation. Adjacent members may be consulted. Executive decision provided within 30 days. |

### 5.4 Application Process Steps

| Step | Action | Responsible Party |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Submit Application**: Complete the Landscaping and Deck Construction Application Form (Appendix A) and submit to the Campground Manager or Harbour Master, depending on location. | Member |
| 2 | **Utility Locate**: If excavation is required, the member must arrange for utility location services (e.g., NB Power, Bell Aliant) at their own cost and provide confirmation. | Member |
| 3 | **Preliminary Review**: The Campground Manager or Harbour Master reviews the application to determine if the project is within the allocated spot. | Campground Manager / Harbour Master |
| 4a | **Administrative Approval Path**: If the project is entirely within the allocated spot and complies with policy, the Campground Manager or Harbour Master approves and notifies the member. | Campground Manager / Harbour Master |
| 4b | **Member Approval Path**: If the project extends outside the allocated spot, the application is forwarded to Executive with a recommendation. Executive reviews, may consult adjacent members, and issues a decision. | Executive |
| 5 | **Site Inspection**: If required, a site visit is conducted to mark utility locations, setbacks, and boundaries. | Campground Manager / Harbour Master |
| 6 | **Construction**: Work proceeds in accordance with approved plans. | Member |
| 7 | **Final Inspection**: Upon completion, a final inspection is conducted to ensure compliance. | Campground Manager / Harbour Master |

**Timeline**:
- Administrative Approval: Decision within **14 calendar days** of receiving a complete application.
- Member Approval: Decision within **30 calendar days** of receiving a complete application.

## 6. Landscaping Guidelines

### 6.1 Permitted Landscaping

| Element | Guidance |
|---------|----------|
| **Native Plants** | Encouraged. Native coastal species are preferred for low maintenance and environmental compatibility. |
| **Lawn and Ground Cover** | Low-growing grasses and ground covers are permitted. |
| **Flower Beds** | Small flower beds (under 50 sq. ft.) are permitted. Beds must be edged to prevent encroachment. |
| **Gravel or Mulch** | Permitted for pathways and defined areas. Gravel must be contained with edging to prevent migration. |
| **Decorative Rocks** | Permitted when used as accents or borders. Large boulders require approval. |
| **Water Features** | Small, self-contained features (e.g., birdbaths) are permitted. No permanent ponds or water features without approval. |

### 6.2 Not Permitted Landscaping

| Element | Rationale |
|---------|----------|
| **Invasive Species** | Plants such as Japanese knotweed, purple loosestrife, and goutweed are prohibited due to their aggressive spread and damage to infrastructure. |
| **Vegetable Gardens** | Not permitted in common areas or within setbacks. May be considered in designated areas on a case-by-case basis. |
| **Deep Root Planting Over Utilities** | No trees or large shrubs shall be planted within 5 feet of underground utilities to prevent root intrusion and damage. |
| **Artificial Turf** | Permitted only with prior approval due to drainage and fire risk concerns. |
| **Excessive Hardscaping** | Hardscaping shall not exceed 30% of the site area to maintain permeability. |
| **Retaining Walls Over 2 Feet** | Retaining walls over 2 feet in height require engineering review and municipal permits. |
| **Encroachment** | Landscaping shall not extend beyond assigned site boundaries or into common areas without Member Approval. |

## 7. Deck Construction Guidelines

### 7.1 Permitted Deck Features

| Element | Guidance |
|---------|----------|
| **Materials** | Pressure-treated wood, cedar, composite decking, or aluminum. Materials must be durable and weather-resistant. |
| **Height** | Decks shall not exceed **30 inches** above grade without railing and engineering review. |
| **Railings** | Railings are required for decks over 24 inches above grade. Railings must be structurally sound and not obstruct sightlines. |
| **Setbacks** | Decks must maintain a **3-foot setback** from adjacent sites and a **5-foot setback** from utility poles, fire hydrants, and common areas. |
| **Footings** | Decks must be supported by proper footings (concrete piers or blocks) to prevent settling and frost heave. |
| **Fasteners** | Use galvanized or stainless steel fasteners to prevent corrosion. |
| **Stairs** | Stairs must have uniform riser heights and slip-resistant treads. Handrails required for stairs with 3 or more risers. |

### 7.2 Not Permitted Deck Features

| Element | Rationale |
|---------|----------|
| **Enclosed or Roofed Decks** | Full enclosures or permanent roofs are not permitted without additional approval and may create code compliance issues. |
| **Decks Over Utility Access Points** | Decks shall not be constructed over utility boxes, cleanouts, or access hatches. |
| **Unstable Foundations** | Decks resting directly on bare ground, concrete blocks without proper footing, or pallets are prohibited. |
| **Cantilevered Structures** | Decks extending beyond the camper footprint without additional support are prohibited. |
| **Non-Permitted Electrical** | Electrical outlets, lighting, or heating installed on decks must be installed by a licensed electrician and approved separately. |

## 8. Utility Protection

Protection of underground and above-ground utilities is a critical requirement:

- **Utility Locates**: Members must contact **NB Power**, **Bell Aliant**, and any other relevant utility providers **at least 72 hours** before any excavation. Confirmation numbers must be provided to DRMC.
- **Marked Lines**: Utility lines marked by locate services must be respected. No excavation within **2 feet** of marked lines.
- **Above-Ground Utilities**: No structures, decks, or landscaping shall obstruct access to utility poles, pedestals, hydrants, or meter boxes.
- **Damage**: Members are responsible for the full cost of repairing any utility damage caused by their activities.

---

## 9. Maintenance and Removal

### 9.1 Ongoing Maintenance

- Members are responsible for the ongoing maintenance of all approved landscaping and decks.
- Dead or hazardous trees must be removed promptly.
- Decks must be maintained to prevent rot, splintering, or structural failure.
- Vegetation shall not encroach onto adjacent sites or common areas.

### 9.2 Removal Requirements

- If a member vacates their site, all approved decks, patios, and landscaping improvements must be **removed** unless otherwise agreed with DRMC.
- Removal must be completed within **30 days** of vacating.
- The site must be restored to a clean, graded condition.
- Any removal costs incurred by DRMC will be charged to the member.

---

## 10. Enforcement and Penalties

| Violation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Unapproved Work** | Stop-work order issued. Member must apply retroactively. Work may be required to be removed. |
| **Non-Compliance with Approved Plans** | Member must correct deficiencies within 14 days. Failure may result in removal at member's expense. |
| **Utility Damage** | Member responsible for all repair costs. Future approval privileges may be suspended. |
| **Encroachment** | Member must remove encroaching structures or vegetation within 14 days. |
| **Repeated Violations** | May result in suspension of approval privileges or termination of moorage/camping agreement. |

---

## 11. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| **Campground Manager** | Review and approve applications for campground sites that are within allocated spots (Administrative Approval); forward applications requiring Member Approval to Executive; conduct site inspections; enforce policy. |
| **Harbour Master** | Review and approve applications for moorage areas that are within allocated spots (Administrative Approval); forward applications requiring Member Approval to Executive; conduct site inspections; enforce policy. |
| **Executive** | Review and decide on applications that extend outside allocated spots (Member Approval); hear appeals of approval decisions; approve policy amendments. |
| **Member** | Submit applications; comply with all requirements; maintain approved improvements. |

---

## 12. Appeals

A member may appeal an approval decision or enforcement action by submitting a written appeal to the Executive within **14 days** of the decision. The Executive shall review the appeal and issue a final decision within **30 days**.

---

## 13. Policy Review and Amendments

This policy shall be reviewed **every three years** or as needed based on operational requirements. Amendments require a **two‑thirds (2/3) majority vote** of the Executive. Members shall be notified of significant changes.

---

## 14. Effective Date

This policy takes effect on **March 1, 2026**.
