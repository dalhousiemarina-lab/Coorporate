# DRMC Conflict of Interest Policy

**Version:** 1.0
**Approved by:** DRMC Board of Directors
**Date of Approval:** March 13, 2026
**Effective Date:** March 13, 2026
**Next Review Date:** March 2027

## 1.0 Purpose

The purpose of this Conflict of Interest Policy is to ensure that all decisions made on behalf of the Dalhousie Regional Marina Club (DRMC) are made in the best interests of the Corporation, free from the influence of personal or private interests. This policy protects the integrity of DRMC's decision-making processes and maintains public trust by establishing clear standards for identifying, disclosing, and managing conflicts of interest.

## 2.0 Scope

This policy applies to all individuals who act on behalf of DRMC, including:

- Directors and Officers of the Board
- Committee members
- Volunteers in leadership or decision-making roles
- Staff (if any)
- Any member participating in a decision-making capacity (e.g., voting on projects or activities)

## 3.0 Policy Definitions

- **Conflict of Interest:** A situation in which an individual's personal, financial, or professional interests (or those of a family member or close associate) conflict with their duty to act in the best interests of DRMC. Conflicts may be actual, potential, or perceived.
- **Family Member:** Spouse, partner, parent, child, sibling, or any relative living in the same household.
- **Close Associate:** A business partner, close personal friend, or any person with whom the individual has a significant relationship that could influence their objectivity.
- **Material Interest:** Any financial or non-financial interest that could reasonably be expected to influence a person's judgment or actions, including but not limited to:
    - Ownership or investment in a business
    - Employment or consultancy position
    - Receipt of compensation, gifts, or favors
    - Serving on another organization's board or committee
- **Pecuniary Interest:** A financial interest that could result in personal gain or loss, directly or indirectly.

## 4.0 General Principles

1.  **Duty of Loyalty:** All individuals covered by this policy have a duty to act honestly and in good faith, placing the interests of DRMC above their personal interests.
2.  **Transparency:** All potential conflicts must be disclosed promptly and fully.
3.  **Abstention:** Individuals with a conflict shall not participate in discussions, decisions, or votes on the matter giving rise to the conflict.
4.  **Public Confidence:** DRMC will manage conflicts in a manner that maintains public confidence in the integrity of the organization.

## 5.0 Disclosure Obligations

### 5.1 Initial Disclosure
Upon assuming a role covered by this policy, each individual shall complete and sign a **Conflict of Interest Disclosure Statement** (see Section 11.0) declaring any known actual, potential, or perceived conflicts.

### 5.2 Ongoing Disclosure
Individuals have a continuing obligation to disclose any new or changing circumstances that could give rise to a conflict of interest. Disclosure must be made as soon as the individual becomes aware of the potential conflict.

### 5.3 Annual Renewal
All directors and officers shall complete an updated Disclosure Statement annually, at the first Board meeting of the fiscal year.

### 5.4 Method of Disclosure
Disclosures shall be made in writing to the Board Secretary, or orally at a Board or committee meeting (and subsequently recorded in the minutes).

## 6.0 Identification and Management of Conflicts

When a conflict of interest is disclosed or identified, the following process shall be followed:

1.  **Recusal:** The individual with the conflict shall immediately excuse themselves from any discussion or deliberation on the matter.
2.  **Determination:** The remaining disinterested members of the Board or committee shall determine whether a conflict exists and, if so, how it should be managed.
3.  **Management Options:** Depending on the nature and severity of the conflict, management may include:
    - **Recusal Only:** The individual abstains from voting but may provide factual information if requested.
    - **No Participation:** The individual is excluded from all discussions and decisions related to the matter.
    - **Divestment:** The individual may be required to divest themselves of the conflicting interest (e.g., selling shares) to eliminate the conflict.
    - **Other Measures:** As determined by the Board on a case-by-case basis.
4.  **Documentation:** All disclosures, determinations, and management actions shall be recorded in the minutes of the meeting.

## 7.0 Specific Prohibitions and Guidelines

### 7.1 Gifts and Hospitality
No individual covered by this policy shall accept gifts, hospitality, or benefits from any person or organization that does business with, or seeks to do business with, DRMC, where such acceptance could reasonably be seen to influence their decisions. Nominal gifts of token value (e.g., promotional items under $50) are permitted but must be disclosed.

### 7.2 Contracts with DRMC
No director or officer shall enter into a contract with DRMC for the provision of goods or services without:
- Full disclosure of their interest.
- Recusal from all discussions and decisions regarding the contract.
- Approval by the disinterested members of the Board, who must determine that the contract is fair and reasonable to DRMC and represents the best value available.

### 7.3 Use of DRMC Assets
DRMC assets, including equipment, facilities, and intellectual property, shall be used only for DRMC purposes and not for personal benefit or gain, unless specifically authorized by the Board.

### 7.4 Confidential Information
Individuals shall not use confidential information obtained through their role at DRMC for personal gain or to benefit a third party.

### 7.5 Outside Activities
Individuals shall not engage in outside activities, including business ventures or volunteer roles with other organizations, that compete with DRMC or that could reasonably create a perception of divided loyalty.

## 8.0 Confidentiality

All discussions and disclosures regarding conflicts of interest shall be treated as confidential, except as required to be documented in meeting minutes or as necessary for legal or audit purposes.

## 9.0 Compliance and Consequences of Non-Compliance

Failure to disclose a conflict of interest, or failure to comply with this policy, may result in:
- Removal from the Board, committee, or volunteer position.
- Invalidation of any decision made where the conflict was not properly managed.
- Other disciplinary action as determined by the Board.
- In cases of deliberate concealment or personal gain, potential legal liability.

## 10.0 Annual Review and Acknowledgment

This policy will be reviewed annually by the Board. All individuals covered by this policy shall sign an acknowledgment form annually, confirming they have read, understood, and agree to comply with the policy.
