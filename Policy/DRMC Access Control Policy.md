## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish clear, consistent rules for the issuance, use, and recovery of all access devices—including keys, fobs, gate codes, and electronic credentials—used to secure the facilities of the Dalhousie Regional Marina Club (DRMC).

While the Bylaws and Code of Conduct establish expectations for member behavior, they do not provide specific operational rules for physical security. This policy fills that gap, ensuring that:

- Access is granted only to eligible individuals.
- Security devices are properly tracked and recovered.
- The risk of unauthorized entry, theft, or vandalism is minimized.
- The club's liability is reduced through clear accountability.
- Our infrastructure investments (gates, cameras, fencing) are protected.

This policy is essential given the complex land situation at DRMC, where some facilities (including the new campground and boat storage areas) are located on land leased from the Port of Dalhousie and subject to specific access restrictions.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All members of the DRMC (Voting Units and associate members).
- All volunteers and Squad members.
- Contractors and service providers requiring temporary access.
- Any person issued a key, fob, code, or other access device by the DRMC.

It covers access to all DRMC‑controlled facilities, including:

- The main gate (parking and trailer access).
- Dock gates and security barriers.
- The clubhouse and storage buildings.
- The campground area (including the new transient‑only zone).
- Boat storage areas.
- Any future facilities with restricted access.

---

## 3. Definitions

- **Access Device:** Any physical or electronic mechanism used to gain entry to a restricted area. This includes keys, key fobs, RFID cards, keypad codes, and remote openers.
- **Fob:** A radio‑frequency identification (RFID) device that grants access when presented to a reader.
- **Gate Code:** A numeric code entered into a keypad to open a gate.
- **Eligible Member:** A member in good standing (dues paid, agreements signed) who has been granted access privileges based on their membership type.
- **Authorized Issuer:** The Harbour Master or their designate, who is responsible for managing access devices.
- **Recovery:** The process of retrieving an access device from a member whose privileges have expired or been revoked.

## 5. Issuance Procedure

### 5.1 New Members
Upon acceptance of a moorage or camping agreement and payment of all fees:

1. The Harbour Master shall verify the member's eligibility and determine the appropriate access level.
2. The member shall be issued the required access devices (fobs, keys, codes) upon signing an **Access Device Acknowledgment Form** (see Section 10).
3. A refundable deposit of **$50 per fob/key** shall be collected at the time of issuance. This deposit will be returned when the device is returned in good working condition.
4. Gate codes shall be provided in writing and are to be kept confidential.

### 5.2 Existing Members (Replacement Devices)
If a member loses a fob or key or requires an additional device:

1. The member shall submit a request to the Harbour Master.
2. A replacement fee of **$25 per fob/key** (non‑refundable) shall be paid before a new device is issued. This covers the club's cost of replacement.
3. The lost device shall be deactivated immediately (if technically possible).

### 5.3 Volunteers and Squad Members
Volunteers requiring access for maintenance or projects:

1. The relevant Squad Lead shall request access in writing to the Harbour Master, specifying the duration and areas needed.
2. Access devices shall be issued on a temporary basis and must be returned immediately upon completion of the work.
3. No deposit is required for temporary volunteer access, but the volunteer assumes responsibility for the device while in their possession.

### 5.4 Contractors
Contractors working on site:

1. The Executive or Harbour Master shall authorize temporary access.
2. Contractors shall be issued a temporary fob or code valid only for the duration of their contract.
3. Contractors must be accompanied by a member or volunteer when accessing sensitive areas (fuel dock, electrical rooms) unless explicitly authorized otherwise.
4. The contractor shall sign a liability waiver if required by the club's insurer.

### 5.5 Gate Codes
- Gate codes shall be changed:
  - At the start of each season.
  - Immediately after a member with access privileges is terminated or resigns.
  - If a code is suspected of being compromised.
- Codes shall be provided to members in writing and must not be shared with non‑members.

## 6. Member Responsibilities

Members issued access devices are responsible for:

- **Safekeeping:** Keeping devices secure and not lending them to anyone, including family members, guests, or other members. Each adult member of a Voting Unit may have their own device upon request.
- **Reporting Loss:** Immediately notifying the Harbour Master if a fob, key, or code is lost or stolen. Delayed reporting may result in liability for unauthorized access.
- **Reporting Suspicious Activity:** Notifying the Harbour Master or Executive if they observe unauthorized individuals attempting to gain access.
- **Return upon Departure:** Returning all access devices to the Harbour Master when their membership ends, or when they vacate their slip or site.
- **Code Confidentiality:** Not sharing gate codes with anyone. Codes are for the personal use of the member only.

---

## 7. Prohibited Conduct

The following actions are strictly prohibited and may result in disciplinary action, including revocation of access privileges, fines, or termination of membership:

- Loaning or transferring an access device to a non‑member.
- Allowing a non‑member to enter a secured area using your device.
- Duplicating keys or fobs without authorization.
- Sharing gate codes with non‑members.
- Tampering with gates, locks, or access control systems.
- Using access devices to enter areas beyond your authorization (e.g., a transient camper entering the seasonal campground).
- Failing to report a lost or stolen device promptly.

---

## 8. Recovery and Return

### 8.1 End of Membership
When a membership ends for any reason (resignation, non‑payment, termination):

1. The Harbour Master shall notify the member in writing that all access devices must be returned within **7 days**.
2. Upon return of the devices in good condition, the member's deposit (if any) shall be refunded.
3. If devices are not returned within 7 days, the club shall:
   - Deduct the replacement cost (up to the full deposit amount or actual cost) from any refund owed to the member.
   - Deactivate the devices if technically possible.
   - Change any affected codes.

### 8.2 Temporary Suspension
If a member's privileges are temporarily suspended (e.g., for non‑payment of a late fee):

1. The Harbour Master shall deactivate the member's fobs and change any codes known to the member.
2. Access shall be restored immediately upon resolution of the issue.

### 8.3 Lost Devices
If a member reports a lost device:

1. The Harbour Master shall deactivate the device immediately (if possible).
2. The member shall pay the replacement fee before a new device is issued.
3. If the lost device is later found, it may be reactivated, but no refund of the replacement fee will be issued.

---

## 9. Auditing and Record Keeping

The Harbour Master shall maintain a secure **Access Control Log** containing:

- Inventory of all access devices (fobs, keys) by serial number.
- Issuance records showing:
  - Member name and Voting Unit ID.
  - Date of issuance.
  - Device type and serial number.
  - Deposit collected (if any).
  - Acknowledgment form signed.
- Return records showing:
  - Date of return.
  - Condition of device.
  - Deposit refunded (if applicable).
- Lost or stolen device reports.
- Gate code change history.

This log shall be reviewed quarterly by the Executive to ensure compliance and identify any issues.

## 11. Enforcement and Penalties

Violations of this policy shall be addressed as follows:

| Violation | First Offense | Second Offense |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Loaning fob/key to non‑member | Written warning | $50 fine and possible suspension |
| Failure to return devices upon departure | Deduction from deposit | Referral to collections / legal action |
| Sharing gate code with non‑member | Code change and warning | $100 fine |
| Tampering with access hardware | Immediate suspension, possible termination | Termination of membership |

All penalties are at the discretion of the Executive and may be escalated depending on the severity of the violation.

---

## 12. Emergency Situations

In the event of an emergency (fire, medical, security threat), access control systems may be overridden by:

- First responders (who may cut locks or force gates).
- The Harbour Master or Executive, who may grant temporary access to emergency personnel.

Any damage caused during an emergency response shall be documented and reported to the insurer.

## 13. Policy Review and Amendments

This policy shall be reviewed annually by the Executive and the Harbour Master to ensure it remains effective and aligned with the club's security needs. Amendments may be made by the Executive as needed, with changes communicated to members via the newsletter.
