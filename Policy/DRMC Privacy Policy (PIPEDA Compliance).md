## 1. Purpose

Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd. (DRMC) is committed to protecting the privacy of its members, volunteers, contractors, and other individuals whose personal information we collect. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your personal information in compliance with the **Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA)** and any applicable provincial privacy laws.

This policy applies to all personal information handled by DRMC, whether in electronic or paper format.

---

## 2. Definitions

- **Personal Information**: Information about an identifiable individual, including but not limited to name, address, telephone number, email address, vessel or RV details, payment information, and correspondence with DRMC. It does not include business contact information when used solely for business purposes.

- **Member**: An individual who holds a membership with DRMC as defined in the Bylaws.

- **Volunteer**: An individual who provides unpaid services to DRMC.

- **Contractor**: An individual or entity providing goods or services to DRMC under contract.

- **Executive**: The Board of Directors of DRMC.

- **PIPEDA**: The Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (Canada).

---

## 3. What Personal Information We Collect

DRMC collects personal information that is reasonably necessary for the operation of the marina and campground, and for the administration of membership and volunteer programs. This may include:

- **Contact Information**: Name, mailing address, email address, phone number.
- **Membership Details**: Membership type, assigned slip or campsite, vessel or RV information (length, beam, draft, registration number, make/model), insurance policy details.
- **Payment Information**: Credit card or banking details (processed securely; we do not retain full payment information beyond transaction records).
- **Volunteer Records**: Volunteer hours, skills, emergency contact information.
- **Correspondence**: Records of communications with DRMC (emails, letters, forms).
- **Photographs/Videos**: Occasionally taken at events for promotional purposes (with consent).

We collect information directly from you through membership applications, agreements, event registrations, volunteer sign-ups, and other interactions. In some cases, we may collect information from third parties with your consent (e.g., insurance verification).

---

## 4. How We Use Personal Information

DRMC uses personal information for the following purposes:

- To establish and manage membership, including billing and communications.
- To assign and manage slips, campsites, and facility access.
- To verify insurance coverage and meet legal requirements.
- To coordinate volunteer activities and track volunteer hours (including for the bonus vote).
- To communicate with members about club news, events, meetings, and important operational updates.
- To process payments and refunds.
- To respond to inquiries and provide customer service.
- To comply with legal and regulatory obligations (e.g., government filings, law enforcement requests).
- To improve our services and plan for future needs.
- To administer surveys, contests, or promotions (with consent).
- To document club activities through photographs/videos for archival and promotional use (with consent).

We will not use your personal information for any other purpose without your consent, unless required or permitted by law.

---

## 5. Consent

By providing your personal information to DRMC, you consent to its collection, use, and disclosure as described in this policy. In some cases, we may seek explicit consent (e.g., for photographs). You may withdraw your consent at any time, subject to legal or contractual restrictions, by contacting our Privacy Officer (see Section 11). Withdrawal of consent may affect our ability to provide certain services (e.g., membership).

---

## 6. Disclosure of Personal Information

DRMC does not sell, rent, or trade personal information. We may disclose personal information only in the following circumstances:

- **To volunteers and contractors** who need the information to perform their duties (e.g., Harbour Master accessing member lists for dock assignments).
- **To service providers** who assist us with operations (e.g., payment processors, IT support), under contractual obligations to protect the information.
- **To legal or regulatory authorities** when required by law (e.g., court order, government audit).
- **To another organization** in the event of a merger, amalgamation, or transfer of assets (with member notification).
- **With your explicit consent** for other purposes.

Access to member lists is restricted to Executive members and authorized volunteers on a need‑to‑know basis. All individuals with access are bound by confidentiality obligations.

---

## 7. Security Safeguards

DRMC takes reasonable measures to protect personal information against loss, theft, unauthorized access, disclosure, copying, use, or modification. Safeguards include:

- **Physical security**: Locked filing cabinets, secure office premises.
- **Technological security**: Password‑protected computers, encrypted digital storage, secure online payment processing.
- **Organizational security**: Access controls, confidentiality agreements for volunteers and contractors, regular training on privacy practices.
- **Data disposal**: Secure shredding of paper records and secure deletion of electronic files when no longer needed.

Despite these measures, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure. DRMC cannot guarantee absolute security but will promptly address any suspected breach in accordance with PIPEDA breach reporting requirements.

---

## 8. Access and Correction Rights

You have the right to:

- **Access** your personal information held by DRMC, subject to limited exceptions (e.g., information protected by solicitor‑client privilege).
- **Request correction** of any inaccuracies or omissions.

To request access or correction, please contact our Privacy Officer in writing (see Section 11). We will respond within 30 days, or notify you if an extension is needed. A minimal fee may apply for access requests (you will be advised in advance).

---

## 9. Retention of Personal Information

DRMC retains personal information only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law. Typical retention periods:

- **Membership records**: Duration of membership + 1 year (for transition purposes), unless longer retention is required for legal or audit purposes.
- **Financial records**: 7 years (as required by Canada Revenue Agency).
- **Volunteer records**: 3 years after last activity.
- **Photographs/videos**: Indefinitely for archival purposes, with consent; consent may be withdrawn at any time.

When information is no longer required, it is securely destroyed or anonymized.

---

## 10. Changes to This Policy

DRMC may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in legal requirements or our operations. The current version will always be available in the corporate document repository and on our website (if applicable). Significant changes will be communicated to members via email or notice at least 30 days before they take effect.
