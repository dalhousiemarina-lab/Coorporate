# Financial Management Policy

**Organization Name:** [Organization Name]  
**Effective Date:** December 17, 2025  
**Version:** 1.0

---

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Financial Management Policy is to establish a framework for sound financial stewardship at [Organization Name]. This policy ensures that financial resources are managed responsibly, transparently, and efficiently to support the organization's mission, maintain fiscal integrity, comply with legal and regulatory requirements, and sustain long-term viability.

Effective financial management protects assets, promotes accountability, facilitates informed decision-making, and builds trust with donors, funders, stakeholders, and the public.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all directors, officers, employees, volunteers, contractors, and any individuals authorized to handle financial matters on behalf of [Organization Name]. It covers all financial activities, including budgeting, revenue, expenditures, assets, reporting, and audits.

---

## 3. Principles
[Organization Name] adheres to the following financial management principles:

- **Accuracy and Integrity:** All financial records must be complete, accurate, and timely.
- **Transparency:** Financial information will be disclosed appropriately to stakeholders.
- **Accountability:** Clear roles and segregation of duties to prevent fraud or errors.
- **Compliance:** Adherence to all applicable laws, regulations, and donor restrictions.
- **Prudence:** Conservative approaches to budgeting, reserves, and investments.
- **Efficiency:** Resources used effectively to maximize mission impact.

---

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors / Treasurer** | Oversees financial policy, approves budgets, reviews financial reports, ensures compliance, and appoints auditors. |
| **Executive Director / CEO** | Implements the policy, manages day-to-day finances, prepares budgets and reports, and recommends financial strategies. |
| **Finance Committee** | Assists the Board in monitoring finances, reviewing reports, and recommending policies. |
| **Finance Staff / Manager** | Maintains records, processes transactions, prepares reports, and ensures internal controls. |
| **All Personnel** | Follow procedures for expenses, reimbursements, and reporting; report concerns promptly. |

---

## 5. Budgeting
- An annual operating budget will be prepared by the Executive Director and Finance Staff and approved by the Board prior to the start of the fiscal year.
- Budgets must align with strategic plans and include revenue projections, expense categories, and contingency reserves.
- Mid-year budget reviews will be conducted, with amendments requiring Board approval if significant (e.g., exceeding 10% of the total budget).
- Program/project budgets must comply with donor restrictions.

---

## 6. Revenue Management
- All revenue (donations, grants, fees, etc.) must be recorded promptly and deposited into designated accounts within 24-48 hours of receipt.
- Restricted funds will be tracked separately in the general ledger and used only for intended purposes.
- Fundraising activities must comply with ethical standards and legal requirements.
- Gift acceptance: The organization may decline gifts that conflict with its mission or impose undue burdens (e.g., environmental liabilities, complex administrative costs).

---

## 7. Expenditure Management
- All expenditures must be authorized, supported by documentation, and aligned with the approved budget.
- **Segregation of duties:** No single person controls all aspects of a transaction (e.g., approval, payment, reconciliation).
- **Purchasing:** Competitive bidding (minimum of three quotes) is required for purchases over $5,000; contracts reviewed by appropriate personnel and legal counsel if necessary.
- **Expense reimbursements:** Require prior approval for expenses over $100, itemized receipts, and must be submitted within 30 days of incurring the expense.
- **Credit card use:** Limited to authorized personnel; original receipts required; statements reconciled monthly by an individual who is not a cardholder.

---

## 8. Internal Controls
- Bank reconciliations performed monthly by a staff member who does not have check-writing authority.
- Dual signatures required for checks over $2,500.
- Access to financial systems restricted by role (read-only, user, admin).
- Regular reviews of transactions for unusual activity by the Finance Committee.
- Conflict of Interest Policy applies to all financial decisions; interested parties must recuse themselves from approvals.

---

## 9. Financial Reporting
- Monthly financial reports (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow) prepared and reviewed by leadership and the Finance Committee/Board.
- Annual financial statements prepared in accordance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) or applicable standards.
- Reports provided to funders/donors as required by grant agreements.
- Key performance indicators (e.g., days of cash on hand, program expense ratio, fundraising efficiency) monitored quarterly.

---

## 10. Reserves and Investments
- **Operating Reserves:** Maintain operating reserves equivalent to 3-6 months of operating expenses to ensure stability during unexpected cash flow disruptions.
- **Investments:** Investments managed prudently, prioritizing safety, liquidity, and return; guided by a separate Investment Policy if applicable.
- **Endowment or Restricted Funds:** Invested per donor intent, with earnings tracked accordingly.

---

## 11. Auditing and Compliance
- Annual independent audit (or review for smaller organizations) conducted by a qualified external auditor selected by the Board.
- Tax filings (e.g., IRS Form 990) prepared and submitted timely; made available to the public as required.
- Compliance with grant agreements, contracts, and regulations monitored by the finance team.
- **Whistleblower protection:** Personnel encouraged to report suspected fraud or irregularities confidentially without fear of retaliation.

---

## 12. Asset Management
- Fixed assets (capitalized at $1,000 or more) inventoried annually; depreciation recorded as applicable.
- Cash handled securely; petty cash limited to $500 and reconciled monthly by an independent individual.
- Insurance coverage maintained for assets, liability, cyber liability, and directors/officers.

---

## 13. Risk Management Integration
Financial risks (e.g., fraud, cash flow shortages, cyber theft) are addressed through the organization's Risk Management Policy and reviewed annually by the Finance Committee.

---

## 14. Training
Relevant personnel (staff, board members, and volunteers with financial roles) will receive training on financial procedures upon onboarding and annually thereafter to ensure understanding of internal controls and compliance.

---

## 15. Review and Amendments
This policy will be reviewed annually or as needed by the Finance Committee. Amendments require Board approval. All changes will be documented and communicated to key personnel.

---
