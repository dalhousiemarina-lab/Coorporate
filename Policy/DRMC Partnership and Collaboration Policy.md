# Partnership and Collaboration Policy

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Partnership and Collaboration Policy is to provide a clear framework for establishing, managing, and evaluating partnerships and collaborations with external organizations, businesses, government entities, academic institutions, and individuals. These relationships are essential to advancing **[Organization Name]** 's mission by leveraging shared resources, expertise, and networks.

This policy ensures that all partnerships are aligned with the organization's values, strategic objectives, and ethical standards; promote mutual benefit; minimize risks; and maintain transparency and accountability.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all directors, officers, employees, volunteers, and representatives acting on behalf of **[Organization Name]** when initiating, negotiating, formalizing, implementing, or terminating partnerships or collaborations. It covers formal agreements (e.g., Memoranda of Understanding, contracts) and informal collaborative arrangements.

---

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Partnership/Collaboration** | Any arrangement where **[Organization Name]** works with an external entity to achieve shared goals, including joint programs, resource sharing, co-branding, sponsorships, or advocacy initiatives. |
| **Partner** | An external organization, business, government body, academic institution, or individual entering into a collaborative relationship with **[Organization Name]** . |
| **Formal Partnership** | A partnership documented through a written agreement (e.g., MOU, contract, letter of agreement). |
| **Informal Collaboration** | Short-term or ad-hoc cooperation without a binding written agreement. |

---

## 4. Principles

All partnerships and collaborations must adhere to the following principles:

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Alignment** | Consistent with **[Organization Name]** 's mission, vision, values, and strategic plan. |
| **Mutual Benefit** | Provide clear value to both parties. |
| **Transparency** | Open communication and disclosure of terms. |
| **Ethical Standards** | No conflicts of interest, undue influence, or compromise of independence. |
| **Risk Management** | Identification and mitigation of potential risks (financial, reputational, legal, operational). |
| **Equity and Inclusion** | Respect for diversity, fairness, and inclusive practices. |
| **Accountability** | Clear roles, responsibilities, and performance measures. |

---

## 5. Types of Partnerships

Partnerships may include, but are not limited to:

- **Programmatic** – Joint delivery of services or projects
- **Funding/Sponsorship** – Financial or in-kind support
- **Advocacy/Policy** – Shared campaigning or lobbying
- **Knowledge Sharing/Research** – Co-creation of resources or studies
- **Corporate** – Employee volunteering, cause-related marketing

---

## 6. Approval Process

### 6.1 Initiation
Any staff or volunteer identifying a potential partnership must discuss it with their supervisor and submit a preliminary proposal to **[designated role/committee, e.g., Executive Director or Partnerships Committee]** .

### 6.2 Due Diligence
Conduct a thorough assessment of the potential partner, including:
- Reputation, values alignment, and track record
- Financial stability (if applicable)
- Legal status and compliance history
- Potential conflicts of interest
- References and past collaborations

### 6.3 Risk Assessment
Evaluate risks using the organization's Risk Management Policy framework.

### 6.4 Approval

| Partnership Type | Approval Authority |
|------------------|--------------------|
| **Informal collaborations** | Approved by **[e.g., Department Head or Executive Director]** |
| **Formal partnerships** | Require review by **[e.g., Partnerships Committee]** and final approval by the Executive Director or Board (for high-value or high-risk agreements) |
| **High-value agreements** | Agreements exceeding **[e.g., $10,000]** or involving co-branding require Board approval |

### 6.5 Documentation
All formal partnerships must be documented in writing, specifying:
- Objectives
- Roles and responsibilities
- Resources
- Timelines
- Financial arrangements
- Intellectual property
- Confidentiality
- Termination clauses
- Dispute resolution

---

## 7. Management and Monitoring

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Designated Contact** | Each partnership will have a primary staff liaison responsible for coordination. |
| **Regular Review** | Partnerships will be monitored through periodic progress reports, meetings, and evaluations (at least annually). |
| **Performance Metrics** | Establish measurable outcomes where possible. |
| **Reporting** | Significant partnerships will be reported to leadership and the Board as needed. |

---

## 8. Conflicts of Interest and Ethical Considerations

- All potential conflicts must be disclosed and managed per the **Conflict of Interest Policy**.
- Partnerships with entities involved in activities contrary to **[Organization Name]** 's values (e.g., tobacco, weapons, human rights violations) are **prohibited**.
- No partnership may imply endorsement of a partner's products, services, or political positions without explicit approval.

---

## 9. Branding and Communications

- Use of **[Organization Name]** 's logo, name, or branding by partners requires **prior written approval**.
- Joint communications must be mutually agreed upon.
- Partners may not speak on behalf of **[Organization Name]** without authorization.

---

## 10. Financial Arrangements

- All financial contributions or obligations must comply with the **Financial Management Policy**.
- Restricted funds will be tracked and used solely for agreed purposes.
- Cost-sharing or revenue-sharing arrangements must be clearly documented.

---

## 11. Termination

Partnerships may be terminated by:
- Mutual agreement
- Expiration of term
- For cause (e.g., breach of agreement, misalignment, reputational risk)

Termination procedures, including notice periods and winding-down obligations, must be followed as outlined in agreements.

---

## 12. Integration with Other Policies

This policy complements:

- Conflict of Interest Policy
- Risk Management Policy
- Financial Management Policy
- Volunteer Policy (for partnerships involving volunteers)

---

## 13. Review and Amendments

This policy will be reviewed **annually** or as needed by **[e.g., the Board or Partnerships Committee]** . Amendments require Board approval.
