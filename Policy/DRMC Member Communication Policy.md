## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish clear, consistent, and professional standards for all official communications between the Dalhousie Regional Marina Club (DRMC) and its members, as well as with the broader public. Clear communication is essential to our pillars of **Transparency** and **Teamwork**.

While the Bylaws mandate specific notices for meetings and votes, this policy addresses day‑to‑day communications, ensuring that all members receive timely, accurate information and that the club's public presence remains professional and engaging.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All official communications sent by the Executive, the Communication Squad, or any Squad on behalf of the DRMC.
- The newsletter (primary communication channel).
- Social media accounts (Facebook and any future platforms).
- Official notice boards at the marina.
- The member portal and website.
- Emergency or urgent notifications.

This policy does **not** apply to informal conversations among members, personal social media posts, or communications sent by members using their personal accounts.

---

## 3. Communication Channels and Their Purpose

The DRMC uses multiple channels to reach members and the public. Each channel has a specific purpose.

| Channel | Primary Audience | Purpose |
|---------|------------------|---------|
| **Newsletter (via Sendmails.io)** | All members and subscribers | Primary channel for all official updates, announcements, financial reports, project proposals, and calls for feedback/voting. |
| **Facebook Page** | Public, prospective members, visitors | Public‑facing window to showcase the marina, share photos/videos, announce public events, and attract new members. |
| **Notice Boards (Physical)** | Members on-site | Post urgent notices, safety alerts, and reminders for those who may not check email regularly. |
| **Member Portal (Future)** | Members | Central repository for documents, invoices, and individual account information. |
| **Executive Meetings & General Meetings** | Members | Formal decision‑making and detailed discussions as required by the Bylaws. |

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

### 4.1 The Communication Squad
The Communication Squad (established October 2025) is responsible for:

- Drafting, editing, and distributing the official newsletter.
- Maintaining and growing the mailing list.
- Managing the official Facebook page and responding to public inquiries.
- Ensuring all communications are clear, professional, and aligned with the club's six pillars.
- Coordinating with other Squads to highlight their work and achievements.
- Maintaining a content calendar.

### 4.2 The Executive
- Approve all major announcements, especially those related to finances, Bylaw changes, or significant projects.
- Ensure that any communication requiring a member vote or feedback includes clear instructions and deadlines.
- Delegate urgent communications to the Communication Squad as needed.

### 4.3 All Squads
- Coordinate with the Communication Squad when planning projects or events that require member awareness.
- Provide accurate and timely information about their activities for inclusion in the newsletter.

### 4.4 Members
- Keep their contact information (especially email address) up to date.
- Read official communications promptly to stay informed.
- Provide feedback through the designated channels (e.g., `feedback@dalhousiemarina.ca`).

## 5. Newsletter Standards and Schedule

The newsletter, sent via **Sendmails.io**, is the club's primary official communication channel. All members and interested parties are encouraged to subscribe.

### 5.1 Frequency Schedule

The newsletter frequency follows the natural rhythm of the marina's operating season:

| Period | Frequency | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Off‑Season (November – March)** | Monthly (first week of each month) | Keep members informed of strategic planning, Bylaw development, financial updates, and winter projects without overwhelming inboxes. |
| **Pre‑Season (April – May)** | Weekly | As the season opening approaches, members need timely information about invoices, spring setup, project votes, and upcoming events. |
| **Peak Season (June – September)** | Every two weeks | Balance the need for updates with members' desire to enjoy their time on the water. Urgent matters may warrant additional special editions. |
| **Post‑Season (October)** | Weekly (as needed) | Wrap‑up information, winterization updates, and calls for feedback on the past season. |

### 5.2 Newsletter Content Standards
Each newsletter should aim to include:

- A clear, friendly opening.
- Updates from the Executive (financial summaries, project status, strategic decisions).
- Calls to action (votes, feedback requests, volunteer opportunities) with clear deadlines.
- Squad highlights (what the FinOps, FunSquad, SaveTheMarina, etc., are working on).
- Upcoming dates and deadlines.
- A closing note and contact information.

### 5.3 Tone and Voice
- **Professional but warm:** Reflect the friendly, community‑oriented nature of the marina.
- **Clear and concise:** Avoid jargon and overly complex language.
- **Bilingual:** All official communications shall be sent in both English and French, reflecting the bilingual nature of our community.
- **Factual and transparent:** Present information honestly, even when the news is difficult (e.g., the playground removal).

### 5.4 Sendmails.io Best Practices
- All official newsletters must be sent from an authorized DRMC account (e.g., `communication@dalhousiemarina.ca`).
- Maintain a clean mailing list by regularly removing hard bounces and honoring unsubscribe requests promptly.
- Use the platform's analytics to track open rates and engagement, using this data to improve future communications.

## 6. Social Media Standards

The DRMC Facebook page (`facebook.com/dalhousieregionalmarinacampground`) is the club's public face.

### 6.1 Content
- Photos and videos of the marina, campground, and community events.
- Public updates and news (e.g., season opening dates, public event announcements).
- Highlights of volunteer work and projects.
- Links to the newsletter for more detailed information.

### 6.2 Management
- Only designated members of the Communication Squad shall have posting access.
- Comments and messages shall be monitored regularly and responded to in a timely, professional manner.
- Negative comments shall be addressed constructively; personal attacks or spam may be deleted.

### 6.3 Relationship to Newsletter
- Social media is **not** a substitute for the newsletter. Official decisions, financial reports, and member votes will always be communicated first and foremost via the newsletter.
- Social media posts may summarize or link to newsletter content to drive traffic and engagement.

## 7. Urgent and Emergency Communications

In the event of an urgent situation requiring immediate member awareness (e.g., a major safety hazard, unplanned facility closure, environmental incident), the following protocol shall be followed:

1.  **Immediate Assessment:** The Harbour Master or Executive member on scene shall assess the urgency and determine the key message.
2.  **Rapid Notification:**
    - Send an urgent email via Sendmails.io marked with "[URGENT]" in the subject line.
    - Post a notice on the physical notice boards at the marina.
    - If necessary, post a brief update on Facebook (for public awareness).
3.  **Follow‑Up:** A detailed update shall be sent via the next regular newsletter or sooner if the situation requires.

## 8. Member Feedback and Inquiries

The DRMC values member feedback. The following channels are available:

- **General Feedback:** `feedback@dalhousiemarina.ca` – for ideas, suggestions, and general comments.
- **Billing Inquiries:** `billing@dalhousiemarina.ca` – for questions about invoices or payments.
- **Incident Reporting:** `incident@dalhousiemarina.ca` – for formal incident reports (see Incident Reporting Procedure).
- **Newsletter Sign‑Up/Unsubscribe:** Via the link in every newsletter footer or by visiting [insert sign‑up link].

All inquiries shall be acknowledged within **5 business days**. Complex issues may take longer to resolve, but the member shall be informed of the expected timeline.

---

## 9. Mailing List Management

- The mailing list is maintained in **Sendmails.io**.
- Anyone may subscribe to the list, not just current members. This ensures the wider community (visitors, former members, friends of the marina) can stay informed.
- Members are responsible for ensuring their email address is current.
- The list shall be used **only** for official DRMC communications. It will never be sold, shared, or used for third‑party solicitations.
- Every newsletter must include a clear and easy **unsubscribe** link, in compliance with Canada's Anti‑Spam Legislation (CASL).

---

## 10. Archiving and Record Keeping

- All newsletters shall be archived in the DRMC's official document repository (Google Workspace) for future reference.
- Major announcements and communications regarding financial decisions, Bylaw changes, or project votes shall be retained permanently.
- The Communication Squad shall maintain an editorial calendar and archive of past communications.

---

## 11. Compliance with Canada's Anti‑Spam Legislation (CASL)

The DRMC is committed to complying with CASL. Accordingly:

- All commercial electronic messages (e.g., fundraising appeals) will only be sent to individuals who have provided express consent.
- Newsletters containing primarily informational or governance‑related content rely on implied consent for current members but will always include an unsubscribe option.
- Consent records (opt‑ins) shall be maintained in Sendmails.io.
- All unsubscribe requests shall be processed promptly and automatically.

---

## 12. Policy Review and Amendments

This policy shall be reviewed annually by the Executive and the Communication Squad to ensure it remains effective and aligned with the club's needs. Amendments may be made by the Executive as needed, with changes communicated to members via the newsletter.
