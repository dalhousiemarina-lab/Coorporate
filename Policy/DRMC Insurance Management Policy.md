## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Insurance Management Policy is to establish a clear framework for the procurement, maintenance, and management of insurance coverage for Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd. (DRMC). This policy ensures that the Corporation maintains adequate insurance to protect its assets, volunteers, members, and Executive from financial loss, while complying with legal and contractual obligations. It also fulfills the requirement set out in Bylaw Article XVIII.3 to maintain Directors' and Officers' Liability Insurance and provides guidance for managing residual risk.

---

## 2. Definitions

- **Insurance Broker**: A professional advisor who arranges insurance coverage on behalf of the Corporation.
- **Insurer**: The insurance company providing coverage.
- **Policy**: The insurance contract between the Corporation and the insurer.
- **Premium**: The annual cost of insurance coverage.
- **Deductible**: The amount the Corporation must pay out‑of‑pocket before insurance coverage applies.
- **Limit**: The maximum amount the insurer will pay for a covered claim.
- **Actual Cash Value (ACV)**: Replacement cost minus depreciation.
- **Replacement Cost**: The cost to replace or repair an asset at current prices, without deduction for depreciation.
- **D&O**: Directors' and Officers' Liability Insurance.
- **WorkSafeNB**: The New Brunswick workers' compensation authority.
- **Coverage B**: Environmental cleanup costs coverage (part of pollution liability insurance).
- **ACV Cliff**: The point at which an asset's insurance valuation transitions from replacement cost to actual cash value, typically after a specified number of years.

---

## 3. Insurance Philosophy

DRMC maintains insurance coverage based on the following principles:

- **Adequate Protection**: Coverage limits shall be sufficient to protect the Corporation's assets and shield volunteers and directors from personal liability, considering current legal and economic realities.
- **Risk Transfer**: Insurance is used to transfer risks that the Corporation cannot reasonably self‑insure.
- **Cost Effectiveness**: Premiums shall be managed through competitive bidding, risk mitigation, and strategic deductible levels.
- **Compliance**: All coverage shall meet or exceed legal requirements, including those imposed by the *Canada Not‑for‑profit Corporations Act*, provincial regulations, and contractual obligations (e.g., lease agreements).
- **Transparency**: Members shall be informed of significant insurance changes and the rationale behind coverage decisions.

## 5. Insurance Procurement Process

### 5.1 Annual Review

The Executive shall review all insurance policies annually, at least 60 days before renewal. The review shall assess:

- Adequacy of coverage limits based on current legal requirements and asset valuations.
- Competitiveness of premiums (obtain at least two quotes every three years, unless circumstances warrant more frequent bidding).
- Claims history and its impact on future premiums.
- Changes in operations or assets that may require coverage adjustments.
- Any new legal or regulatory requirements.

### 5.2 Broker Relationship

The Corporation shall maintain a relationship with a qualified insurance broker who:

- Holds appropriate licenses and has experience with marina and non‑profit insurance.
- Provides annual policy reviews and market comparisons.
- Assists with claims management.
- Advises on risk mitigation strategies.

### 5.3 Policy Documentation

All current policies, certificates of insurance, and related correspondence shall be maintained in the Document Repository by the Secretary. The Treasurer shall ensure that policies are accessible to Executive members.

## 6. Member Insurance Requirements

To ensure the Corporation's liability coverage remains valid, all members must comply with the following:

### 6.1 Vessel Insurance

- Every vessel with a moorage agreement must maintain **commercial general liability insurance** with a minimum limit of **$1,000,000**.
- Proof of insurance must be provided **before the vessel is launched** each season (Code of Conduct Section 2.02).
- Policies must name Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd. as an **additional insured** where possible.
- Failure to provide proof will result in suspension of docking privileges.

### 6.2 Camper Insurance

- Every seasonal camper must maintain liability insurance on their RV/trailer with a minimum limit of **$1,000,000**.
- Proof of insurance must be provided before occupancy each season.

### 6.3 Member Responsibility

Members are responsible for ensuring their own insurance is adequate for their needs (e.g., contents, boat damage). The Corporation's insurance does not cover member property.

## 7. Volunteer Coverage

### 7.1 Current Exposure

The 2026 insurance audit confirmed that volunteers are **not automatically covered** by WorkSafeNB or the Corporation's liability insurance for injuries sustained while volunteering. This exposes the marina to civil lawsuits.

### 7.2 Volunteer Coverage Solution

The Executive shall pursue formalizing **elective WorkSafeNB coverage** for members who assist with maintenance and other volunteer activities. This coverage:

- Provides medical and wage benefits to injured volunteers.
- Generally prevents the volunteer from suing the marina for negligence.
- Shall be offered to volunteers in designated roles (e.g., grounds maintenance, dock work, event setup).

Until WorkSafeNB coverage is in place, all volunteers must sign a **Volunteer Waiver and Assumption of Risk** form (to be developed) acknowledging the risks and releasing the Corporation from liability.

### 7.3 Volunteer Supervision

All volunteer activities shall be supervised by a Squad Coordinator or Executive member who ensures:

- Tasks are appropriate to the volunteer's skill level.
- Safety equipment is provided and used.
- Incidents are reported immediately.

## 8. Risk Management and Mitigation

To minimize the likelihood of claims and maintain insurability, the Corporation shall implement the following risk management practices:

### 8.1 Mandatory Documentation

The insurer requires:

- **Fully signed moorage agreements** for every vessel (renewed annually).
- **Documented inspection logs** for all docks, buildings, and equipment. Inspections shall be conducted monthly during the operating season and after major storms. Logs shall be maintained by the Harbour Master and Campground Manager.

Failure to maintain these records can void coverage entirely.

### 8.2 The 2029 "ACV Cliff"

In 2029, the docks will reach their **15‑year mark**, at which point the insurance valuation typically transitions from **Replacement Cost** to **Actual Cash Value (ACV)** , potentially reducing payouts for storm losses.

- **Impact**: Without intervention, post-2029 payouts for storm losses could cover significantly less than the full cost to rebuild.
- **Mitigation**: The Corporation shall immediately work with the Insurance Broker to explore options with the insurer to extend the replacement cost valuation beyond the standard 15-year mark. 
Continuous investment has been made by the Corporation to maintain the docks in pristine condition, and this proactive maintenance record will be used as leverage in negotiations to push the "ACV Cliff" deadline further into the future.

### 8.3 Claims Management

In the event of an incident that may lead to a claim:

1. **Notify the Treasurer and Commodore immediately**.
2. **Document** the incident thoroughly (photos, witness statements, Incident Report Form).
3. **Do not admit liability** or make promises to claimants.
4. **Contact the insurance broker** for guidance before communicating with third parties.
5. **Cooperate fully** with the insurer's investigation.

All claims shall be tracked by the Treasurer, and a claims register shall be maintained.

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|

| **Commodore** | Oversee insurance strategy; sign policies; communicate major changes to members. |

| **Campground Manager** | Maintain campground inspection logs; ensure member insurance compliance (campers); report incidents. |

## 10. Reporting to Members

The Treasurer shall provide an **insurance update** at least annually, typically at the Spring General Meeting or AGM. The update shall include:

- Summary of current coverages and limits.
- Premium costs and any significant changes.
- Claims history (without compromising confidentiality).
- Explanation of any recommended changes.
- Update on the infrastructure replacement fund progress.

---

## 11. Policy Review and Amendments

This policy shall be reviewed **annually** by the Executive, typically in conjunction with the insurance renewal process. Amendments require a **two‑thirds (2/3) majority vote** of the Executive. Members shall be notified of significant changes.
