## 1.0 Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish clear guidelines for the use of specific sites at Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd. (DRMC) 
that are affected by underground services installed on Port of Dalhousie property without prior consent. 
This policy ensures compliance with legal obligations, mitigates operational risks, maintains insurance coverage, 
and protects the environment in accordance with the NB Clean Environment Act.

---

## 2.0 Scope

This policy applies to:

- All seasonal and long-term slip holders at the marina
- All seasonal and long-term campground site holders
- All transient visitors seeking short-term accommodations
- DRMC staff and volunteers responsible for site assignments and reservations

---

## 3.0 Definitions

| **Term** | **Definition** |
|----------|----------------|
| **Affected Sites** | Campground Sites 101–115 (located along the railroad siding) and any marina slips supported by the underground services in question |
| **Transient Use** | Short-term occupancy with a maximum stay of 30 consecutive nights, renewable once per season |
| **Seasonal Booking** | A reservation for an entire operating season (typically May–October) |
| **Long-Term Booking** | A reservation extending beyond one operating season, including multi-year commitments |
| **Port of Dalhousie** | The authority governing the waterfront property where underground services were installed without prior consent |
| **Ecorock Pozzolan Project** | A development project requiring relocation of the underground services in question |
| **NB Clean Environment Act** | Provincial legislation governing environmental protection and contamination prevention |
| **Duty to Defend** | Insurance policy provision requiring the insured to take all reasonable steps to prevent losses; failure to do so may void coverage |

---

## 4.0 Background

On May 30, 2025, DRMC entered into an Agreement with the Port of Dalhousie regarding underground services that were installed on Port property without prior consent. Under this agreement:

- DRMC is required to relocate the affected underground services at its own expense to accommodate the Ecorock Pozzolan Project
- The Port may issue a 30-day notice requiring immediate relocation of these services
- Failure to comply could result in legal action and environmental violations

### 4.1 Insurance Review Finding

Upon further review with DRMC's insurance provider, a critical finding was identified:

> **⚠️ IMPORTANT INSURANCE REQUIREMENT ⚠️**
>
> If an environmental problem is raised, DRMC is **NOT INSURED** unless they can provide proof of doing everything they can to avoid the incident.
>
> This means:
> - Insurance coverage may be voided if DRMC fails to take reasonable preventive measures
> - The "duty to defend" requires proactive risk mitigation
> - Continued seasonal use of affected sites could be construed as negligence
> - DRMC must demonstrate **due diligence** in preventing environmental incidents

### 4.2 Risk Mitigation Rationale

To mitigate the risk of:
- Disrupting seasonal members mid-season
- Losing insurance coverage in the event of an environmental incident
- Facing legal liability for damages
- Violating NB Clean Environment Act requirements

DRMC has implemented this policy restricting affected sites to transient use only. 
This action demonstrates to insurers, regulators, and the Port that DRMC is taking all reasonable steps to prevent environmental incidents.

---

## 5.0 Policy Statement

### 5.1 Transient-Only Restriction

All sites supported by the underground services in question are hereby restricted to **TRANSIENT (short-term) use only**. This means:

- ❌ **No seasonal bookings** will be accepted or renewed on these sites
- ❌ **No long-term bookings** will be accepted or renewed on these sites
- ✅ **Transient use only** with first-come, first-served availability
- ✅ **Maximum stay:** 30 consecutive nights
- ✅ **Renewal option:** One renewal per season (maximum 60 nights total)

### 5.2 Insurance Compliance Statement

This restriction is necessary to maintain insurance coverage. By limiting exposure duration on affected sites, DRMC demonstrates:

- **Due diligence** in preventing environmental incidents
- **Proactive risk mitigation** as required by insurance policies
- **Reasonable steps** to avoid circumstances that could lead to claims
- **Compliance** with the duty to defend provisions in DRMC's liability coverage

Failure to implement these restrictions could result in:
- Denial of insurance claims
- Personal liability for Executive members
- Uninsured environmental cleanup costs
- Regulatory penalties

### 5.3 Booking Priority

Affected sites shall be available on a **first-come, first-served basis** for:

- Transient boaters seeking temporary moorage
- Transient campers seeking temporary campsites
- Walk-in visitors during operating hours
- Online reservations through the DRMC booking system

### 5.4 Seasonal and Long-Term Bookings

| **Booking Type** | **Status** | **Action Required** |
|------------------|------------|---------------------|
| Existing Seasonal Slip/Site in Affected Zone | Cancelled effective immediately | Contact office for transition options |
| Existing Long-Term Booking (2026+) | Automatically cancelled | Priority for non-affected sites available |
| Waiting List for Affected Sites | Suspended | No new additions to waiting list |

## 7.0 Insurance Implications

### 7.1 Duty to Defend Explained

Most commercial general liability and environmental impairment liability policies contain a **"duty to defend"** provision. This requires the insured to:

> *"Take all reasonable steps to prevent losses and mitigate circumstances that could lead to claims."*

If an insured fails to take reasonable preventive measures and a loss occurs, the insurer may:
- Deny coverage entirely
- Reserve rights to deny coverage
- Pursue subrogation against the insured
- Cancel the policy

### 7.2 What This Means for DRMC

| **Scenario** | **Insurance Outcome** |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Environmental incident occurs on affected sites while occupied by seasonal member, and DRMC took no preventive action | **Coverage likely DENIED** – DRMC failed duty to defend |
| Environmental incident occurs on affected sites while occupied by seasonal member, but DRMC had policy restricting use and notified members | **Coverage MAY APPLY** – DRMC demonstrated due diligence |
| Environmental incident occurs on affected sites during transient use, with all reasonable precautions taken | **Coverage SHOULD APPLY** – DRMC fulfilled duty to defend |
| No environmental incident occurs | Policy remains in force |

### 7.3 Documentation Requirements

To maintain insurance coverage, DRMC must document:

- ✅ This policy and its effective date
- ✅ Notification to all affected members
- ✅ Transition meetings conducted
- ✅ Refund/relocation forms completed
- ✅ Regular inspections of affected sites
- ✅ Spill prevention measures implemented
- ✅ Training records for staff on this policy

All documentation shall be maintained in the Corporate Document Repository for a minimum of 7 years.

## 8.0 Transition Options for Affected Site Holders

### 8.1 Available Options

Existing seasonal and long-term site holders affected by this policy may choose from the following options:

| **Option** | **Description** | **How to Access** |
|------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Relocation** | Move to a non-affected seasonal site (limited availability) | Contact office for site availability |
| **Transient Credits** | Convert remaining seasonal fees to transient use credits (prorated refund) | Complete Refund/Relocation Form |
| **Full Refund** | Receive full refund for current season if no alternative available | Complete Refund/Relocation Form |
| **Priority Waitlist** | Priority placement on waitlist for non-affected sites in future seasons | Contact office to be added |

### 8.2 Refund Calculation

Refunds shall be calculated on a **prorated basis** as follows:

Refund Amount = (Total Seasonal Fee) × (Remaining Days in Season) ÷ (Total Season Days)


Transient credits shall be calculated at the equivalent daily transient rate.

### 8.3 Transition Meetings

Affected site holders are encouraged to schedule a **one-on-one transition meeting** by calling the marina office at (506) 684-3346 to discuss:

- Insurance implications
- Available relocation options
- Refund calculations
- Future booking possibilities

## 9.0 Procedures

### 9.1 For Staff: Assigning Affected Sites

1. Verify site status in reservation system (marked as "TRANSIENT ONLY")
2. Confirm maximum stay does not exceed 30 consecutive nights
3. Track stays to ensure renewal limit (one per season) is not exceeded
4. Document all transient reservations in the booking system
5. Report any unauthorized seasonal use to the Commodore immediately

### 9.2 For Staff: Handling Cancellations

1. Notify affected site holders in writing (email and posted notice)
2. Explain insurance implications for non-compliance
3. Provide Refund/Relocation Form upon request
4. Process refunds within 14 business days of approved form
5. Update reservation system to reflect cancelled bookings
6. Maintain record of all cancelled bookings for insurance audit purposes

### 9.3 For Staff: Insurance Documentation

Maintain the following records for insurance purposes:

- Copy of this policy and all amendments
- List of all affected members and their chosen options
- Signed acknowledgment forms
- Refund/relocation forms
- Meeting notes from transition meetings
- Email communications regarding the policy

### 9.4 For Members: Transition Process

1. Review this policy and identify if your site is affected
2. Contact office at (506) 684-3346 to schedule transition meeting
3. Choose your preferred option (relocation, credits, or refund)
4. Complete and submit Refund/Relocation Form
5. Make arrangements for any equipment or property removal if relocating
6. Sign acknowledgment form confirming understanding of insurance implications

## 11.0 Compliance and Enforcement

### 11.1 Unauthorized Seasonal Use

Any attempt to use affected sites for seasonal or long-term purposes after the effective date of this policy shall result in:

- Immediate removal from the site
- Forfeiture of any fees paid
- Suspension of marina privileges
- Possible termination of membership per DRMC Bylaws
- **Potential personal liability for environmental incidents** due to insurance exclusion

### 11.2 Overstay Penalties

Transient users exceeding the 30-night maximum or second renewal limitation shall be charged:

- Double the daily rate for each night over the limit
- Subject to immediate removal if space is needed for compliant users

### 11.3 Reporting Violations

Members and staff should report any suspected violations to:

| **Contact** | **Method** |
|-------------|------------|
| Marina Office | (506) 684-3346 |
| Harbour Master | In person at marina office |
| Commodore | commodore@dalmarina.ca |

## 12.0 Environmental Compliance

This policy supports DRMC's commitment to environmental protection by:

- Limiting exposure duration on sites with uncertain service reliability
- Reducing risk of fuel spills or contamination in sensitive areas
- Ensuring compliance with NB Clean Environment Act requirements
- Allowing rapid evacuation of sites if relocation is mandated
- **Maintaining insurance coverage for environmental incidents**

All users of affected sites shall comply with:

- DRMC Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure (SPCC) Plan
- DRMC Emergency Response Plan
- NB Clean Environment Act provisions
- Any additional environmental requirements posted at the facility

## 13.0 Liability and Indemnification

### 13.1 DRMC Liability Limitation

DRMC, its Executive, volunteers, and staff shall not be held liable for:

- Any losses incurred due to cancellation of seasonal or long-term bookings
- Costs associated with relocation of vessels, campers, or personal property
- Business interruptions resulting from compliance with Port requirements
- Any environmental damages arising from failures of the underground services in question
- **Any insurance coverage denials resulting from member non-compliance with this policy**

### 13.2 Member Acknowledgment

By accepting a transient booking on affected sites, users acknowledge:

- They have been informed of the insurance implications
- They understand the risks associated with using affected sites
- They agree to indemnify and hold harmless DRMC from any claims arising from their use of the site
- They accept that their own insurance may be required for losses

### 13.3 Indemnification Agreement

All users of affected sites shall sign the following acknowledgment:

I acknowledge that I have been informed that:
1. The site I am using is served by underground services installed without Port consent
2. DRMC may receive 30-day notice to relocate these services
3. DRMC's insurance coverage requires demonstration of due diligence
4. My use of this site is at my own risk
5. I release DRMC from any liability arising from environmental incidents
