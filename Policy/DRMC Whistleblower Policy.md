# Whistleblower Policy

**Organization Name:** [Organization Name]  
**Effective Date:** [Date]  
**Version:** 1.0

---

## 1. Purpose

[Organization Name] is committed to maintaining the highest standards of integrity, transparency, and accountability in all aspects of its operations. This Whistleblower Policy is designed to encourage and enable individuals to report suspected misconduct, illegal activities, or violations of organizational policies without fear of retaliation.

The purpose of this policy is to:
- Provide a clear, confidential mechanism for reporting concerns.
- Protect individuals who report concerns in good faith.
- Ensure that all reports are investigated promptly and thoroughly.
- Reinforce the organization's commitment to ethical conduct and legal compliance.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all:
- Directors, officers, and board members
- Employees (full-time, part-time, and temporary)
- Volunteers
- Interns
- Contractors and consultants
- Vendors and partners conducting business with [Organization Name]

Any individual covered by this scope may report concerns under this policy.

---

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Whistleblower** | Any individual who reports a suspected violation or concern under this policy. |
| **Retaliation** | Any adverse action taken against a whistleblower in response to a good faith report, including termination, demotion, harassment, discrimination, or any other form of intimidation. |
| **Good Faith** | A report made with honest intent and reasonable belief that the information is true, even if later investigation reveals no violation. |
| **Misconduct** | Any activity that violates law, regulations, organizational policy, or ethical standards, including fraud, theft, corruption, abuse of power, safety violations, or gross mismanagement. |

---

## 4. Reporting Protected Under This Policy

This policy protects reports regarding, but not limited to:
- Fraud, theft, or embezzlement of organizational assets
- Accounting irregularities, financial misstatements, or audit violations
- Violations of federal, state, or local laws or regulations
- Gross mismanagement or waste of resources
- Abuse of authority or unethical conduct
- Health and safety violations
- Discrimination, harassment, or retaliation
- Violations of grant or donor restrictions
- Circumvention of internal controls
- Any other conduct that undermines the integrity of the organization

## 6. Confidentiality

[Organization Name] will protect the confidentiality of whistleblowers to the fullest extent possible, consistent with conducting a thorough investigation and complying with legal requirements.

- Information will be shared only with individuals who have a legitimate need to know.
- The identity of the whistleblower will not be disclosed without consent unless required by law or necessary to enforce this policy.
- Investigators and recipients of reports are obligated to maintain strict confidentiality.

---

## 7. Non-Retaliation

[Organization Name] strictly prohibits retaliation against any individual who reports a concern in good faith or participates in an investigation.

**Prohibited retaliatory actions include:**
- Termination or demotion
- Unfavorable performance evaluations
- Harassment or intimidation
- Exclusion from meetings or opportunities
- Threats or coercion
- Any adverse change in terms or conditions of employment or volunteer status

Any individual who engages in retaliation will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination or removal from the organization.

---

## 8. Investigation Process

All reports will be handled in a timely, fair, and impartial manner.

| Step | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1. Receipt** | Reports are received by the designated individual(s) and logged with a unique identifier. |
| **2. Preliminary Review** | Initial assessment to determine credibility and whether further investigation is warranted. |
| **3. Assignment** | Investigations are assigned to the appropriate party (e.g., Board Chair, Audit Committee, external investigator) based on the nature of the report. |
| **4. Investigation** | Fact-finding through interviews, document review, and other methods; conducted objectively and confidentially. |
| **5. Findings & Recommendations** | A written summary is prepared with findings and recommended actions. |
| **6. Resolution** | Corrective actions, policy updates, or referrals to external authorities are implemented as appropriate. |
| **7. Closure & Feedback** | Whistleblowers (if identifiable) receive acknowledgment of receipt and, to the extent possible, notification of resolution. |

---

## 9. Good Faith Requirement

This policy is intended for legitimate concerns made in good faith. Individuals who knowingly make false or malicious reports will be subject to disciplinary action.

A good faith report is one where the individual reasonably believes the information is true. Mistakes or unsubstantiated allegations made without malice do not constitute bad faith.

---

## 10. Rights and Protections

Whistleblowers retain the right to:
- Report concerns to external regulatory or law enforcement agencies.
- Seek legal advice regarding their rights.
- Be free from retaliation as described in Section 7.

Nothing in this policy prohibits individuals from reporting potential violations to appropriate government agencies, including the Internal Revenue Service (IRS), state attorneys general, or law enforcement.

---

## 11. Responsibilities of Management

All managers and supervisors are responsible for:
- Communicating this policy to those under their supervision.
- Ensuring that reports are promptly directed to appropriate channels.
- Refraining from any form of retaliation.
- Cooperating fully with investigations.

Managers who fail to uphold these responsibilities may face disciplinary action.

---

## 12. Recordkeeping

[Organization Name] will maintain confidential records of all reports received, investigations conducted, and outcomes reached. Records will be retained in accordance with the organization's document retention policy.

---

## 13. Training and Awareness

- All personnel will receive information about this policy during onboarding.
- Periodic training will be provided to reinforce understanding of reporting mechanisms, confidentiality, and non-retaliation protections.
- The policy will be prominently displayed on internal communication platforms and made available to all stakeholders.

---

## 14. Review and Amendments

This policy will be reviewed annually by the Board of Directors or designated committee to ensure effectiveness and compliance with applicable laws. Amendments require Board approval.
