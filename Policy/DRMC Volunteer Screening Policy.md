# DRMC Volunteer Screening Policy

**Document Version:** 1.0 (Draft) – March 2026  
**Status:** Work in Progress – Not yet approved by the Executive  
**Effective Date:** [To be determined upon approval]  
**Review Cycle:** Annually (or as needed)

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a consistent, fair, and legally sound process for screening volunteers who take on roles within the Dalhousie Regional Marina Club (DRMC). As identified in our February 2026 financial review, our audit confirmed that volunteers are not automatically covered by WorkSafeNB or our liability insurance in a way that fully protects the club. This policy addresses that gap by ensuring that:

- Volunteers in sensitive roles are appropriately screened.
- The club's liability is reduced through documented due diligence.
- Vulnerable individuals (children, seniors, etc.) who may participate in club activities are protected.
- Member trust in our volunteer leadership is maintained.

This policy is grounded in our pillars of **Accountability**, **Transparency**, and **Teamwork**, and is designed to be practical for a volunteer‑run organization while meeting legal and insurance requirements.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All individuals applying for or serving in volunteer positions that involve:
  - Handling club funds or financial records.
  - Supervising or working with minors or vulnerable persons.
  - Access to sensitive member data (personal information, financial records).
  - Operating heavy equipment or performing high‑risk maintenance.
  - Representing the club in an official capacity with external organizations.
  - Unsupervised access to club facilities (keys, fobs, codes).
- All members of the Executive (Directors).
- All Squad Leads and any volunteer in a role designated as "screened" under Section 5.

This policy does **not** apply to:

- Members participating in general membership meetings or open events.
- Volunteers assisting with one‑time, low‑risk activities under direct supervision (e.g., helping at a BBQ, picking up litter).
- Guests or visitors.

---

## 3. Definitions

- **Screened Role:** A volunteer position identified as requiring a background check due to the nature of the responsibilities.
- **Vulnerable Person:** An individual who, due to age, disability, or other circumstances, may be at greater risk of harm. This includes children under 18, seniors, and persons with physical or cognitive disabilities.
- **Criminal Record Check (CRC):** A check of Canadian criminal records, typically including the Canadian Police Information Centre (CPIC) database. May also be referred to as a "Police Records Check."
- **Vulnerable Sector Check (VSC):** A more detailed check that includes a search of the Pardoned Sex Offender database. Required for roles involving unsupervised access to vulnerable persons.
- **Screening:** The process of assessing a volunteer's suitability for a role, which may include a criminal record check, reference checks, and an interview.
- **Eligible Volunteer:** An individual who has successfully completed the screening process for their role.

---

## 4. Guiding Principles

- **Proportionality:** The level of screening shall be proportional to the risk associated with the role. Not all volunteers require a full criminal record check.
- **Fairness:** All volunteers in the same role category shall be subject to the same screening requirements. Screening results shall be handled confidentially and fairly.
- **Confidentiality:** All information obtained through screening is strictly confidential and shall only be shared with those who have a legitimate need to know (e.g., the Executive for hiring decisions, insurers if required).
- **Inclusion:** A criminal record does not automatically disqualify a volunteer. The nature of the offense, its relevance to the role, and the time elapsed shall be considered.
- **Compliance:** The screening process shall comply with all applicable laws, including privacy legislation (PIPEDA) and human rights legislation, which prohibits discrimination based on a pardoned or unrelated criminal record.

---

## 5. Risk Levels and Screening Requirements

Volunteer roles are classified into three risk levels. The Executive, in consultation with Squad Leads, shall determine the appropriate level for each role.

| Risk Level | Description | Examples | Screening Required |
|------------|-------------|----------|---------------------|
| **Low** | Minimal risk; volunteers work under direct supervision; no access to sensitive data, funds, or vulnerable persons. | One‑time event helpers, general meeting attendees, social event participants. | None. General orientation and sign‑in only. |
| **Medium** | Moderate risk; volunteers work with limited supervision; may have access to club facilities, equipment, or member contact information. | Dock Squad members (routine maintenance), Maintenance Squad members, Communication Squad members (non‑sensitive data). | Basic Criminal Record Check (every 3 years). Reference checks. Signed confidentiality agreement. |
| **High** | Significant risk; unsupervised access to vulnerable persons, club funds, sensitive data, or heavy equipment. Roles of trust and authority. | Executive members (Directors), Treasurer, FinOps Squad members, SaveTheMarina Squad members handling grants, Squad Leads, volunteers working with children/youth, volunteers with master keys/fobs. | Vulnerable Sector Check (if role involves vulnerable persons) or Criminal Record Check. Reference checks. Interview. Signed confidentiality and conflict of interest forms. |

## 6. Screening Process

### 6.1 Step 1: Role Identification
When a volunteer expresses interest in a role, or when the Executive or a Squad Lead identifies a need to fill a position, the screening requirements for that role shall be reviewed and confirmed.

### 6.2 Step 2: Application and Consent
The volunteer shall complete:
- A **Volunteer Application Form** (name, contact, relevant experience, references).
- A **Consent for Criminal Record Check Form**, authorizing the DRMC to request a background check.
- A **Confidentiality Agreement** (for all Medium and High‑risk roles).
- A **Conflict of Interest Declaration** (for High‑risk roles).

### 6.3 Step 3: Reference Checks
For Medium and High‑risk roles, the Executive or designate shall contact at least two references provided by the applicant. Reference checks shall focus on the applicant's suitability for the role, reliability, and alignment with the club's values.

### 6.4 Step 4: Criminal Record Check (as required)
- The club shall provide the volunteer with information on how to obtain a Criminal Record Check through the local police or an authorized service provider.
- The cost of the check shall be:
  - **Reimbursed by the club** for High‑risk volunteer roles (including Executive members), recognizing that these roles are essential to club operations and require a higher level of screening.
  - **Paid by the volunteer** for Medium‑risk roles, unless the volunteer requests reimbursement and the Executive approves it based on the club's budget.
- The check must be dated within the last **6 months** at the time of submission.
- Results shall be submitted directly to the volunteer, who shall then provide a copy to a designated, confidential contact (e.g., the Commodore or a specific Executive member). The club shall not request that results be sent directly to the club without the volunteer's knowledge.

### 6.5 Step 5: Review and Decision
A small screening committee (minimum two Executive members) shall review all application materials, including the criminal record check results (if any). The committee shall consider:

- The relevance of any criminal record to the role.
- The time elapsed since the offense.
- The applicant's explanation and references.
- The risk to the club and its members.

The committee may:
- **Accept** the volunteer.
- **Accept with conditions** (e.g., supervision, restricted duties).
- **Reject** the application (with written explanation).

The decision shall be communicated to the applicant in a timely and respectful manner.

### 6.6 Step 6: Onboarding
Successful applicants shall receive:
- A copy of this policy.
- Role‑specific training and orientation.
- Issuance of any access devices (per the Access Control Policy).
- A welcome from their Squad Lead.

## 7. Confidentiality and Record Keeping

- All screening documents (application forms, consent forms, criminal record check results) are **strictly confidential**.
- Documents shall be stored in a secure, access‑restricted digital folder (Google Workspace, with access limited to the Executive and the designated screening committee).
- Criminal record check results shall not be kept longer than necessary. A record that a check was completed and the date of completion may be retained, but the actual results should be securely destroyed once the hiring decision is made, unless retention is required for legal or insurance purposes (in which case they must be kept in a sealed, confidential file).
- No information from the screening process shall be shared with other members, squad members, or the general membership.

---

## 8. Frequency of Screening

- **Executive members (Directors):** A criminal record check shall be required upon initial election and every **5 years** thereafter, or more frequently if required by the club's insurer.
- **High‑risk roles:** A criminal record check shall be required upon appointment and every **3 years** thereafter.
- **Medium‑risk roles:** A criminal record check shall be required upon appointment and every **3 years** thereafter.
- All volunteers shall promptly notify the Executive if they are charged with or convicted of a criminal offense during their volunteer tenure that could affect their suitability for the role.

---

## 9. Volunteers with Criminal Records

A criminal record does not automatically disqualify an individual from volunteering. The screening committee shall consider:

- The nature and seriousness of the offense.
- The relationship of the offense to the duties of the volunteer role.
- The length of time since the offense occurred.
- Evidence of rehabilitation.
- The number of offenses.

If an applicant is rejected based on a criminal record, they shall be provided with a written explanation and an opportunity to discuss the decision.

---

## 10. Volunteers Working with Vulnerable Persons

Any volunteer whose role involves **unsupervised access to vulnerable persons** (including children under 18, seniors, or persons with disabilities) must undergo a **Vulnerable Sector Check (VSC)** . This is a legal requirement in Canada and is non‑negotiable.

Examples of such roles:
- Organizing or supervising youth programs (e.g., sailing lessons, junior activities).
- Visiting or providing services to isolated seniors.
- Leading activities that involve one‑on‑one interaction with vulnerable individuals.

The club shall cover the cost of the VSC for these essential roles.

---

## 11. Volunteers from Outside Canada

For volunteers who have lived outside Canada within the last five years, the Executive may require a criminal record check or equivalent from the country or countries of residence. The volunteer is responsible for obtaining and covering the cost of such checks, unless otherwise agreed.

---

## 12. Consequences of Non‑Compliance

- Volunteers who refuse to undergo required screening shall not be placed in the role for which screening is required.
- Volunteers who falsify information on their application or consent forms may have their volunteer status revoked immediately.
- Volunteers who fail to disclose a criminal charge or conviction that occurs during their tenure may be subject to removal from their role.

---

## 13. Transition for Existing Volunteers

Volunteers already serving in Medium or High‑risk roles as of the effective date of this policy shall be given a reasonable transition period (e.g., 6 months) to complete the screening process. During this period, they may continue in their roles unless the Executive determines that immediate screening is necessary due to specific risk concerns.

---

## 14. Policy Review and Amendments

This policy shall be reviewed annually by the Executive to ensure it remains effective, legally compliant, and aligned with the club's needs. Amendments may be made by the Executive as needed, with changes communicated to volunteers and members.
