# DRMC Bylaws 2026 – Complete Plain-Language Guide

**Effective Date:** February 28, 2026

This document pairs each section of the official bylaws with a simple explanation. The original legal text is shown first, followed by a **Descriptive Note** that translates it into everyday language for all members to understand.

---

## ARTICLE I: Name

**Source:**
The official name of the corporation shall be "Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd" (referred to as "the Corporation" throughout these bylaws).

> **Descriptive Note:** This is just our legal name. We are officially called **"Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd."** In the rulebook, we refer to ourselves as "the Corporation."

## ARTICLE II: Objects

**Source:**
The Corporation is established with the primary objective of operating and maintaining a marina and campground for the enjoyment of its members, their families, and their guests. Its aims include providing boaters with secure facilities for boats and equipment, and ensuring campers have access to well-maintained sites and amenities. The Corporation fosters community-building activities, upholds compliance with Federal, Provincial, and Municipal regulations, and encourages a strong community spirit through volunteer squads, social events, and collaborative decision-making. Additionally, the Corporation is responsible for seeking and managing funds for facility maintenance and improvement, and for securing all necessary permits, leases, and licenses required for operations.

> **Descriptive Note:** This explains why we exist. Our purpose is to run a marina and campground for members, keep everything safe and well-maintained, build community through events and volunteering, follow all government rules, raise money for improvements, and get all the necessary permits and licenses.

## ARTICLE IV: Membership & Voting Rights

### IV.1 The Voting Unit

**Source:**
Membership governance is conducted through Voting Units, defined as a single family unit using the same premises (slip or campsite). Each Voting Unit is entitled to a maximum of **two (2) votes** in total, allocated as follows:

- **One (1) base vote** per Voting Unit based on membership class.
- **One (1) additional bonus vote** if the Unit meets the volunteer requirements in Section IV.2.

No Voting Unit shall cast more than two votes on any single motion.

> **Descriptive Note:** The basic voting group is a "Voting Unit"—usually a family renting one boat slip or one campsite. Each Unit can have up to two votes total: one automatic vote for being a member, and one extra "bonus vote" earned by volunteering. No Unit gets more than two votes on any single issue.

### IV.2 Bonus Vote for Volunteers

**Source:**
To promote active participation, a Voting Unit may earn one (1) additional vote (a "bonus vote") if at least one adult member of the Unit completes 20 or more hours of verified volunteer work in the preceding 12-month period. Volunteer hours are tracked in a public Volunteer Registry. Volunteer hours registered in the Volunteer Registry expire 12 months after they are recorded.

> **Descriptive Note:** To encourage helping out, a Unit gets one extra vote if an adult does 20 or more verified volunteer hours in the past year. Hours are tracked in a public list and expire after 12 months.

### IV.3 Voter Eligibility

**Source:**
Only the designated Primary or Secondary Member of a Voting Unit in good standing may exercise the Unit's vote(s). If the Unit is entitled to a bonus vote, it is exercised by the same designated voter.

> **Descriptive Note:** Only the one or two people the family names as its voters can cast the Unit's votes. The Unit must be "in good standing" (fees paid, no active suspensions).

### IV.4 Voting Unit Access Types

**Source:**
Membership access is classified into the following categories, which do not affect voting rights:

- Berth Holder: Holds a seasonal boat slip.
- Seasonal Camper: Holds a seasonal campsite.
- Dual Member: Holds both a boat slip and a campsite.
- Associate Member: Social member with no voting rights and no boat slip or campsite.
- Overnight Guest: Short-term visitor with no voting rights.

> **Descriptive Note:** These are categories describing how you use the marina. They do not affect your voting rights. Associate Members and Overnight Guests cannot vote.

### IV.5 Unit Classes and Voting Rights

**Source:**
The Corporation shall have **Class A Voting Unit**, divided into the following subcategories. Each subcategory is entitled to **one (1) base vote**:

1.  **Berth Holder**
    Definition: An individual who holds a berth agreement with the Corporation.
    Base Voting Rights: **One (1) vote**.

2.  **Seasonal Camper**
    Definition: An individual who holds a seasonal camping agreement with the Corporation.
    Base Voting Rights: **One (1) vote**.

3.  **Dual Member**
    Definition: An individual who holds both a seasonal camping agreement and a berth agreement with the Corporation.
    Base Voting Rights: **One (1) vote**.

4.  **Certified Volunteer**
    Definition: An individual recognized by the Corporation as a certified volunteer under its established criteria.
    Base Voting Rights: **One (1) vote**.

**Bonus Vote:** Any Voting Unit described in subcategories 1 through 4 above may earn **one (1) additional bonus vote** upon completion of 20 or more verified volunteer hours as set forth in Section IV.2.

> **Descriptive Note:** This lists who gets the one "base vote": Berth Holders, Seasonal Campers, Dual Members, and Certified Volunteers. All of these can also earn the bonus volunteer vote.

### IV.6 Eligibility and Verification

**Source:**
The Corporation establishes and maintains procedures for verifying eligibility within each subcategory and confirming completion of required volunteer hours for the bonus vote.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Executive will have rules to make sure people are in the right category and that volunteer hours are real.

### IV.7 Designating Voters

**Source:**
Each Voting Unit must notify the Secretary in writing of its designated Primary Voter. A Secondary Voter may also be named, who may vote only in the absence of the Primary Voter. Only the designated Primary or Secondary Voter may cast the Unit's vote(s), and only one individual may vote on behalf of the Unit at any given meeting or vote.

> **Descriptive Note:** Each Unit must tell the Secretary in writing who their main voter is. They can also name a backup voter, who can only vote if the main person can't.

### IV.8 Voting Procedure

**Source:**
For any vote, the total number of votes cast is the sum of all base votes and bonus votes from participating Voting Units. No Unit may cast more than two votes total.

> **Descriptive Note:** The total votes in any election are the sum of all base votes and earned bonus votes from the Units that participate. Remember, no Unit gets more than two.

### IV.9 Volunteer Hour Tracking & Verification

**Source:**
The Corporation maintains a public Volunteer Registry to monitor each Unit's progress toward the 20-hour goal. Volunteered and verified volunteer hours must be documented either by the Squad Coordinator or an Executive member. Any disputes regarding volunteer hours are resolved by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** The public Volunteer Registry tracks hours, logged by a Squad Coordinator or Executive member. The Executive settles any disputes.

### IV.10 Resignation of Membership

**Source:**
A member may resign from the Corporation at any time by delivering a written notice of resignation to the Secretary. The resignation shall be effective upon receipt by the Secretary or on a later date specified in the notice. Upon resignation, the member forfeits all rights and privileges of membership, and no refund of dues or fees shall be provided unless otherwise determined by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** To quit, you must tell the Secretary in writing. You give up all rights and usually don't get a refund.

### IV.11 Code of Conduct Agreement

**Source:**
All members must agree to abide by the current Code of Conduct as a condition of membership and to access Corporation facilities.

> **Descriptive Note:** By being a member, you automatically agree to follow the separate Code of Conduct.

## ARTICLE V: Suspension of Membership

### V.1 Termination of Membership

**Source:**
Membership may be terminated for repeated or severe violations following the suspension process outlined in this Article. The affected member shall have the right to appeal the termination decision to the membership at the next General Meeting, where a majority vote of Voting Units present shall decide the appeal. Termination shall be effective immediately upon the Executive's decision, subject to appeal.

> **Descriptive Note:** Membership can be ended for serious or repeated rule-breaking. The person can appeal the decision at the next General Meeting, where a majority vote of other Voting Units decides the final outcome.

### V.2 Grounds for Suspension

**Source:**
The Executive may suspend a membership for the following reasons:

- Failure to pay fees after due notice
- Violation of Corporation rules or bylaws, including the Code of Conduct
- Conduct deemed detrimental to the Corporation, its members, or its operations

> **Descriptive Note:** You can be suspended for not paying fees after being reminded, breaking rules, or behavior harmful to the club.

### V.3 Process and Appeal

**Source:**
Prior to any expulsion, the member shall receive written notice of the allegations and be given an opportunity to be heard by the active members at a meeting.

> **Descriptive Note:** Before being kicked out, the member gets a written notice explaining the problem and a chance to explain their side at a meeting.

### V.4 Expulsion Decision

**Source:**
Following the hearing, a petition for expulsion may be circulated among the active members. The member shall be expelled if the petition receives the signatures of at least 51% of the active members of the Corporation.

> **Descriptive Note:** After the hearing, a petition to expel the member can be circulated. They are expelled if at least 51% of all active members sign it.

### V.5 Notification

**Source:**
The Executive shall promptly notify the suspended member in writing.

> **Descriptive Note:** The member gets a written notice of the decision.

### V.6 Fee Forfeiture

**Source:**
Suspended members forfeit their membership fee and are not entitled to a refund.

> **Descriptive Note:** If suspended, you lose your membership fee. No refund.

### V.7 Loss of Membership for Non-Use

**Source:**
A membership may be terminated if the member does not utilize their assigned slip or campsite for a full season, as follows:

**V.7.1 Definition of Non-Use**
- Non-use means the member's assigned slip or campsite remains vacant and unoccupied for the entire operational season (May 1 to October 31)
- Occasional, sporadic, or de minimis use (e.g., less than 7 total days occupied) may be deemed non-use at the Executive's discretion

> **Descriptive Note:** You can lose your spot for not using it. Non-use means your slip or campsite is empty the whole season (May 1 to Oct 31). Using it very little (like less than 7 days) might also count.

**V.7.2 Notification Requirement**
- Members anticipating non-use for an upcoming season must notify the Secretary in writing by April 1st
- Members who fail to notify and do not use their space forfeit their membership rights as outlined below

> **Descriptive Note:** If you know you won't use your spot next season, you must tell the Secretary in writing by April 1st.

**V.7.3 Consequences of Non-Use**
- After one season of non-use, the member's assigned slip or campsite shall be forfeited and offered to the waiting list
- The member's Voting Unit status shall be converted to Associate (Non-Voting) Member status for the following season
- After two consecutive seasons of non-use, membership shall be terminated entirely, and the member must reapply as a new member if they wish to return

> **Descriptive Note:** After one season of non-use, you lose your specific spot, and your voting membership becomes a non-voting "Associate" membership. After two straight seasons, your membership is ended, and you must reapply as a new member.

**V.7.4 Exceptions**
The Executive may grant exceptions to this policy for:
- Medical circumstances (with supporting documentation)
- Military service or extended work assignments abroad
- Major repairs to vessel or RV (with proof and timeline)
- Other exceptional circumstances as determined by the Executive

Requests for exceptions must be submitted in writing prior to the season.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Executive can make exceptions for things like medical issues, military service, or major repairs, if you ask in writing before the season starts.

**V.7.5 Fee Forfeiture**
Members whose membership is terminated under this section forfeit all fees paid and are not entitled to any refund.

> **Descriptive Note:** If your membership ends for non-use, you lose your fees. No refund.

### V-A.4 Enforcement

**Source:**
Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in suspension of membership under Article V.

> **Descriptive Note:** Breaking the Code of Conduct can lead to suspension under the rules in Article V.

## ARTICLE VI: Membership Fees and Pricing

### VI.1 Annual Fee Setting Process

**Source:**
Fees are recommended by the Executive and approved by the general membership at the Annual General Meeting. The Executive must present a detailed fee schedule for the upcoming year, including justifications for any proposed increases.

Fee calculations shall be based on the following methodologies:

- **Berth Holders:** Annual fee = vessel length overall (LOA) in feet × rate per foot established annually by the membership.
- **Seasonal Campers:** Annual fee = fixed rate per campsite established annually by the membership.
- **Dual Members:** Annual fee = (boat LOA × rate per foot) + fixed campsite rate.
- **Associate Members:** Annual fee = fixed social membership rate established annually by the membership.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Executive suggests fees for the next year, but the general membership must approve them at the Annual General Meeting (AGM). They have to show how the fees are calculated. Berth fees are based on boat length, campers pay a flat rate, dual members pay both, and associate members pay a flat social rate.

### VI.2 Annual Price Increase Cap

**Source:**
Annual fee increases are capped at 4% above the previous year's fees unless the Executive provides detailed justification and the increase is specifically approved by a majority vote at the September AGM. AGM notice must include the proposed fee schedule and rationale for exceeding the cap.

> **Descriptive Note:** Fees usually can't go up by more than 4% from the previous year. If a bigger increase is needed, the Executive must explain why, and members must approve it by a majority vote at the AGM.

### VI.3 Emergency Fee Adjustment

**Source:**
In extraordinary circumstances, the Executive may convene a Special Membership Meeting to consider fee adjustments outside the normal cycle. Emergency fee adjustments require approval by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of Voting Units present.

> **Descriptive Note:** In an emergency, the Executive can call a Special Meeting to discuss an urgent fee change. This needs approval from two-thirds of the Voting Units present.

### VI.4 Membership Year, Invoicing, and Payment

**Source:**
- **Membership Year:** All membership rights expire on April 30th each year.
- **Invoicing:** Invoices for the upcoming membership year shall be issued on **April 1st**.
- **Payment Due Date:** All fees are due by **May 1st**. Fees unpaid after May 1st shall be subject to a penalty charge as determined by the Executive, and the assigned slip or campsite may be forfeited and offered to the waiting list.
- **Late Fees:** The Executive may establish a late fee schedule, which shall be communicated to members with the invoice.

> **Descriptive Note:** Membership runs from May 1 to April 30. Bills are sent out on April 1st and are due by May 1st. Late payments may result in a penalty, and you could lose your spot to someone on the waiting list.

### VI.5 Fee Structure Transparency

**Source:**
The Executive shall provide a detailed breakdown of how membership fees are allocated across operational areas. This breakdown will be included in Spring General Meeting materials and available to members upon request.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Executive will show members how their fees are spent, with a breakdown included in Spring Meeting materials or available upon request.

### VI.6 Membership Rights Transfer

**Source:**
Membership rights are not transferable except in the event of a member's death (to a surviving spouse) or as provided for Unit transfers in Article VII.

> **Descriptive Note:** You can't transfer your membership to someone else, except to a surviving spouse if a member dies, or as allowed under the transfer rules in Article VII.

### VI.7 Lifetime Memberships

**Source:**
**VI.7.1 Grandfathering of Existing Lifetime Memberships**
All individuals who held a valid lifetime membership as of the 2025 season are hereby grandfathered and shall retain their lifetime membership status under the terms set forth in this section.

> **Descriptive Note:** People who already had a lifetime membership before 2025 keep it under the rules in this section.

**VI.7.2 Rights and Privileges**
Lifetime members shall enjoy the following rights:
- Exemption from annual membership fees (including any base fees or social dues).
- Full voting rights as a Voting Unit, subject to the two-vote maximum in Article IV.
- Access to all Corporation facilities and participation in activities, subject to applicable usage fees (e.g., boat slip fees based on vessel length, campsite fees at the seasonal fixed rate, etc.) and compliance with all rules and regulations.

> **Descriptive Note:** Lifetime members don't pay annual fees. They have full voting rights (up to two votes per Unit) and access to facilities, but they still pay usage fees like slip or campsite rental and must follow all rules.

**VI.7.3 Non-Transferability**
Lifetime memberships are personal to the individual member and are not transferable to any other person, including family members, except as provided in Article VII for transfers upon death to a surviving spouse who was part of the same Voting Unit.

> **Descriptive Note:** A lifetime membership belongs to that specific person only. You cannot give it or sell it to someone else, except to a surviving spouse as allowed in Article VII.

**VI.7.4 Revocation**
A lifetime membership may be revoked only in accordance with Article V (Suspension of Membership) for cause, or if the member resigns in writing. Upon revocation or resignation, all rights cease and no refund of any kind shall be provided.

> **Descriptive Note:** A lifetime membership can only be taken away for serious rule-breaking (as in Article V) or if the member quits in writing. No refunds are given.

**VI.7.5 Record Keeping**
The Secretary shall maintain a register of all lifetime members, which shall be included in the corporate records.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Secretary keeps an official list of all lifetime members.

### VI.8 New Memberships and Initiation Fee

**Source:**
**VI.8.1 Availability of New Memberships**
New memberships shall be granted only when an assigned slip (for Berth Holders) or campsite (for Seasonal Campers) becomes available. The Corporation shall maintain a waiting list of applicants in the order of application received, and vacancies shall be offered to the next qualified applicant on the waiting list.

> **Descriptive Note:** New memberships are only available when a slip or campsite opens up. We keep a waiting list, and spots are offered to the next person on the list.

**VI.8.2 One-Time Initiation Fee**
All new members admitted to the Corporation shall pay a one-time, non-refundable initiation fee in addition to the applicable annual seasonal fees. The amount of the initiation fee shall be established annually by the Executive and presented to the membership for information at the Annual General Meeting.

> **Descriptive Note:** New members pay a one-time, non-refundable initiation fee on top of their regular annual fees. The Executive sets the amount each year and tells members about it at the AGM.

**VI.8.3 Exceptions**
Transfers of membership rights under Article VII are not subject to the initiation fee. The initiation fee does not apply to Associate Members unless otherwise determined by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** People who get a membership through a transfer (Article VII) don't pay the initiation fee. Associate Members usually don't pay it either, unless the Executive decides otherwise.

## ARTICLE VII: Transfer of Membership Rights

### VII.1 Eligibility for Transfer

**Source:**
Voting Units may request to transfer membership rights under the following circumstances:

- Sale of the primary asset associated with the membership (boat or camping equipment)
- Relocation of the primary residence
- Other significant life circumstances as approved by the Executive

**Lifetime memberships are not transferable** except as provided in VI.7.4.

> **Descriptive Note:** You can ask to transfer your membership if you sell your boat or RV, move away, or have another major life event approved by the Executive. Lifetime memberships cannot be transferred, except to a surviving spouse.

### VII.2 Transfer Process

**Source:**
The current Voting Unit must submit a written request to the Secretary, stating the reason for transfer, providing contact information for the proposed new Unit, and including written consent from all Unit members. The proposed new Unit must meet all eligibility requirements, and the Executive must approve the transfer by majority vote.

> **Descriptive Note:** To transfer, the current member must write to the Secretary explaining why, give contact info for the new person, and get consent from everyone in their Unit. The new person must qualify for membership, and the Executive must approve the transfer by majority vote.

### VII.3 Conditions of Transfer

**Source:**
The transfer includes all rights and responsibilities associated with the membership category. The new Unit assumes membership for the remainder of the current year. No new membership fee is required for transfers within the same year, and the transfer does not result in an additional membership slot.

> **Descriptive Note:** The new member takes over all rights and responsibilities for the rest of the current year. No extra membership fee is charged for that year, and this doesn't create a new membership spot—it's just filling the existing one.

### VII.4 Limitations

**Source:**
Only one transfer per membership is permitted each year. Transfers may not be used to circumvent waiting lists or membership caps. The Corporation may charge an administrative fee for processing transfers.

> **Descriptive Note:** Only one transfer per membership is allowed each year. Transfers can't be used to jump the waiting list. The club may charge a small fee to cover administrative costs.

### VII.5 Effective Date

**Source:**
The transfer is effective upon Executive approval and completion of all required documentation.

> **Descriptive Note:** The transfer takes effect as soon as the Executive approves it and all the paperwork is done.

## ARTICLE VIII: Membership Meetings

### VIII.1 Annual General Meeting

**Source:**
The Annual General Meeting (AGM) shall take place near season closing of each year.
The order of business at the AGM shall include the following:
- Call to Order and Approval of Previous Minutes: Opening of the meeting and approval of minutes from the previous meeting.
- Activity Report: Presentation of the year's activities by the Commodore (or presiding officer).
- Financial Report: Presentation of the financial report by the Treasurer.
- Squad Reports: Reports from Squad committees, as applicable.
- Project Report previous season: Presentation and review of project status and outcomes for projects completed or underway during the current year.
- Project Plan Next season: Presentation of proposed project plans and capital initiatives for the upcoming year (in accordance with Article X).
- Pricing for the Next Season: Presentation and approval of the membership fee schedule for the upcoming year.
- Election: Election of Executive members (in accordance with Article XI).
- Governance Amendments: Decision on any proposed amendments to the By-Laws or Corporate documents, if applicable.
- Other Business: Any other matter brought before the meeting in accordance with the agenda.

The meeting format may be in person, web conference, or hybrid, as determined by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** The AGM happens near the end of the season (September). It's our most important meeting where we approve the minutes from last time, hear reports on activities and finances, review past projects and vote on future ones, approve next year's fees, elect new leaders, vote on bylaw changes, and discuss any other business. The meeting can be in person, online, or both.

### VIII.2 Spring General Meeting

**Source:**
A Spring General Meeting shall be held in April of each year, with the format determined by the Executive.
The order of business at the Spring General Meeting shall include the following:
- Call to Order and Approval of Previous Minutes: Opening of the meeting and approval of minutes from the previous meeting.
- Project Voting: Presentation and vote on any proposed projects requiring membership approval (in accordance with Articles IX and X).
- Budget Presentation: Presentation and approval of the annual operating budget for the upcoming fiscal year by the Treasurer.
- Special Committees Plan: Presentation of plans and objectives by special committees for the upcoming season.
- Seasons What's new: Any changes to the upcoming season (Insurance, Hours, Services).
- Governance Voting: Discussion and voting on any proposed amendments to the By-Laws or Corporate documents, if applicable.
- Other Business: Any other matter brought before the meeting in accordance with the agenda.

Minutes from this meeting will announce voting results.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Spring Meeting is in April. We vote on projects, approve the annual budget, hear plans from special committees, learn about any changes for the upcoming season, and vote on any bylaw changes. Results are announced in the minutes.

### VIII.3 Seasonal Update Meetings

**Source:**
Two Seasonal Update Meetings are held during the operational season to ensure transparent communication and ongoing member engagement:

- June: First Seasonal Update Meeting
- August: Second Seasonal Update Meeting

Agendas include project updates, financial summaries, operational reports, squad updates, and open member forums. These meetings are informational and consultative; no binding votes or bylaw amendments are held.

> **Descriptive Note:** We have two informal meetings during the season (June and August) just to share updates and get member feedback. These are for communication only—no voting happens here.

### VIII.4 Decision Making

**Source:**
Decisions at membership meetings are determined by majority vote of total votes cast by Voting Units present in person or participating via web conference, unless stated otherwise. Each Voting Unit's vote(s) shall be counted in accordance with Article IV.

> **Descriptive Note:** Most decisions are made by a simple majority vote of the Units present (either in person or online). Each Unit's votes are counted as described in Article IV (base vote + bonus vote).

### VIII.5 Special Meetings

**Source:**
A Special Membership Meeting may be called by the Executive or by written request of at least 15 Voting Units. Notice must be circulated at least fifteen (15) days prior to the meeting and must include the purpose and web conference instructions if applicable.

> **Descriptive Note:** A special meeting can be called by the Executive or by a petition signed by at least 15 Voting Units. Notice must be given 15 days in advance, explaining the purpose and how to join online if applicable.

### VIII.6 Notice of Meetings

**Source:**
Notice of the Annual General Meeting or any Special Meeting shall be given to all members not less than twenty-one (21) days nor more than sixty (60) days before the meeting date, via email, postal mail, or other electronic means as recorded in the membership register. Notice for Seasonal Update Meetings or the Spring General Meeting shall be given at least fourteen (14) days in advance.

> **Descriptive Note:** For the AGM and Special Meetings, we give 21 to 60 days' notice by email or mail. For Spring and Seasonal Update Meetings, we give at least 14 days' notice.

### VIII.7 Quorum

**Source:**
One-third (1/3) of eligible Voting Units constitutes a quorum for any General or Special Membership Meeting. No quorum is required for Seasonal Update Meetings.

> **Descriptive Note:** For votes at the AGM, Spring Meeting, or a Special Meeting to be valid, at least one-third of all Voting Units must be present (either in person or online). No quorum is needed for the informal Seasonal Update Meetings.

### VIII.8 Notice & Web Conference Details

**Source:**
Written notice of all General Membership Meetings must be provided at least twenty-one (21) days in advance for the Annual and Spring Meetings, and at least fourteen (14) days in advance for the Seasonal Update Meetings.

> **Descriptive Note:** This repeats the notice requirements: 21 days for AGM and Spring Meeting, 14 days for Seasonal Updates.

### VIII.9 Participation via Web Conference

**Source:**
Members participating by web conference are considered present for all purposes. The Corporation shall ensure reasonable opportunity for participation via web conference technology.

> **Descriptive Note:** If you join a meeting online, you're considered "present" just like being there in person. The club will make sure online participation works reasonably well.

### VIII.10 Action Without a Meeting

**Source:**
Actions required or permitted at a membership meeting may be conducted without an in-person meeting if the Corporation delivers an electronic or paper ballot to every Voting Unit entitled to vote, provided quorum and approval thresholds are met.

> **Descriptive Note:** Sometimes we can vote without holding a meeting, by sending out electronic or paper ballots to all Voting Units. The same rules for quorum and approval still apply.

### VIII.11 Summary of Meeting Powers

**Source:**
| Meeting Type | When Held | Main Purpose | Binding Votes Held? |
|--------------|-----------|--------------|----------------------|
| Annual General (AGM) | September | Set fees, decision to launch election, executive nomination, nominated presentation 10min | YES |
| Spring General | April | Approve annual budget, rules and projects | YES |
| Seasonal Update | June & August | Share updates and get member feedback | NO |
| Special Meeting | As Needed | Address a single, urgent issue | YES |
| Online Project Vote | As Needed | Vote on a specific, pre-defined project | YES |

> **Descriptive Note:** This table summarizes the different types of meetings: AGM (September) sets fees and holds elections; Spring Meeting (April) approves budget and projects; Seasonal Updates (June/August) are for information only; Special Meetings handle urgent issues; Online Project Votes allow voting on specific projects outside of meetings.

## ARTICLE IX: Online Project Voting

### IX.1 Purpose and Scope

**Source:**
The Corporation may conduct online votes for specific, well-defined projects to facilitate member participation and efficient decision-making. A "Project" refers to a discrete capital expenditure, improvement initiative, or significant operational change, fully defined and costed using the official Project Proposal Template specified in Article X.

> **Descriptive Note:** We can vote online on specific projects to make it easier for members to participate. A "Project" is a major expense or improvement that is fully planned and priced using the official form described in Article X.

### IX.2 Initiation of a Project Vote

**Source:**
A Project Vote may be initiated by a majority vote of the Executive or by a petition signed by at least 15% of Voting Units submitted to the Secretary.

> **Descriptive Note:** An online project vote can be started either by a majority vote of the Executive or by a petition signed by at least 15% of Voting Units and given to the Secretary.

### IX.3 Project Definition and Information Package

**Source:**
Before voting, the Executive must prepare and distribute a comprehensive Project Information Package using the official Project Proposal Template.

> **Descriptive Note:** Before any online vote, the Executive must prepare and share a complete information package about the project, using the official template.

### IX.4 Notice and Voting Period

**Source:**
The Project Information Package shall be distributed to all Voting Units at least fourteen (14) days before the online vote closes. The voting period must remain open for at least seven (7) consecutive days.

> **Descriptive Note:** The project information must be sent to all Voting Units at least 14 days before the online vote closes. The voting period itself must be open for at least 7 days.

### IX.5 Voting Platform

**Source:**
Votes are cast via a secure, third-party online voting platform, which ensures Unit authentication, vote secrecy, and result integrity. The system must apply the correct number of votes per Voting Unit (base vote plus any bonus vote), verified by the Volunteer Registry.

> **Descriptive Note:** We use a secure online voting system that makes sure each Unit can only vote once, keeps votes secret, and counts the votes correctly. The system automatically applies the right number of votes (base + bonus) for each Unit.

### IX.6 Quorum and Passing Threshold

**Source:**
At least 25% of eligible Voting Units must cast votes for validity. A motion passes with a simple majority, unless it involves borrowing money, in which case a two-thirds (2/3) majority is required.

> **Descriptive Note:** For an online vote to count, at least 25% of all Voting Units must participate. Most projects pass with a simple majority (more than 50% of votes cast). If the project involves borrowing money, it needs a two-thirds majority.

### IX.7 Certification and Results

**Source:**
The Secretary and Treasurer, or an independent third party appointed by the Executive, certify the results. Results must be announced to the membership within seven (7) days of the vote closing, including the number of Units voting and the total vote count. Certified results from successful online Project Votes carry the same force as resolutions passed at a General Membership Meeting.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Secretary and Treasurer (or an outside person) verify the results. Results are announced to all members within 7 days, including how many Units voted and the total vote count. Approved projects are just as official as if they were voted on at a regular meeting.

## ARTICLE X: Project Definition and Management

### X.1 Purpose

**Source:**
This Article provides clarity and transparency in spending authority and membership voting requirements by defining what constitutes a "Project" and establishing a standardized proposal process.

> **Descriptive Note:** This section is here to make things clear and fair. It defines what counts as a "Project" and sets up a standard way to propose one, so everyone understands how spending decisions are made.

### X.2 Definition of a Project

**Source:**
A "Project" is any discrete initiative involving capital expenditure or significant changes to Corporation property or operations, with a defined scope, timeline, and budget. Projects may be executed in one or multiple phases.

> **Descriptive Note:** A "Project" is any specific job that costs money or makes big changes to the marina or campground. It has a clear goal, timeline, and budget. Projects can be done all at once or in stages.

### X.3 Project Classification

**Source:**
- Maintenance Project: Involves repair, replacement, or restoration of existing property to its original condition or intended function.
- Capital/Improvement Project: Involves new construction, expansion, enhancement, or major modification that adds new functionality, capacity, or features.

> **Descriptive Note:** There are two types of projects: **Maintenance** (fixing or replacing something that already exists) and **Capital/Improvement** (building something new or making a big upgrade that adds new features).

### X.4 Project Phasing

**Source:**
- Single-Phase Project: Unified scope executed continuously from start to finish.
- Multi-Phase Project: Divided into sequential phases, each with its own scope, budget, and timeline, possibly dependent on completion of previous phases and requiring separate approval.

> **Descriptive Note:** A project can be done in one go (single-phase) or broken into stages (multi-phase). Each stage has its own plan and budget, and might need its own approval.

### X.5 What is Not a Project

**Source:**
Routine operations and maintenance activities, such as annual opening/closing, lawn care, minor repairs, and purchasing consumable supplies, do not require a project proposal or membership vote if within approved budget and Executive authority.

> **Descriptive Note:** Regular day-to-day stuff—like opening and closing for the season, mowing the lawn, small repairs, and buying supplies—doesn't count as a "Project." These things don't need a special proposal or member vote, as long as they're in the budget.

### X.6 Mandatory Project Proposal Process

**Source:**
Any initiative meeting the definition of a Project must be documented using the official Project Proposal Template, which is required for both Executive-approved projects and those requiring membership vote.

> **Descriptive Note:** If something meets the definition of a Project, it must be written up using the official Project Proposal Template. This applies whether the Executive is approving it or it needs a membership vote.

### X.7 Template Compliance

**Source:**
The Project Information Package for online Project Voting must use the official Project Proposal Template. Incomplete templates may be rejected until all required information is provided.

> **Descriptive Note:** Any project that goes to an online vote must use the official template. If the form isn't filled out completely, it can be sent back until all the information is there.

### X.8 Template Maintenance and Updates

**Source:**
The official Project Proposal Template is maintained as a separate document in the corporate repository. Updates require a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the Executive, and substantive changes must be circulated to members for comment at least 30 days prior to adoption.

> **Descriptive Note:** The official project form is kept in our document archive. Changing it requires a two-thirds vote of the Executive. Big changes must be shared with members for comments 30 days before being approved.

## ARTICLE XI: Election Cycle

### XI.1 Nomination Window (August 1 – August 21)

**Source:**
Nominations for Executive positions shall be accepted via a secure digital form. Any voting member in good standing may nominate themselves or another member (with the nominee's written consent). The Election Committee shall verify the eligibility of all candidates.

> **Descriptive Note:** From August 1 to 21, you can nominate someone (or yourself) for a leadership position using an online form. The person being nominated must agree to it. An Election Committee checks that all candidates are eligible.

### XI.2 Campaign Period (August 22 – September AGM)

**Source:**
Candidates shall have the opportunity to present themselves to the membership through a hybrid approach:

- **Digital:** Candidate bios, photographs, and mission statements shall be posted to the member portal for review by all Voting Units.
- **In-Person:** Candidates may engage with members at the Marina during the August Seasonal Update Meeting, which shall include a dedicated forum for candidate introductions and questions.

> **Descriptive Note:** From August 22 until the AGM in September, candidates can introduce themselves to members. Their info (bio, photo, goals) will be posted online. They can also meet members in person at the August update meeting.

### XI.3 Voting – In Person Only at September AGM

**Source:**
Actual voting for Executive positions shall occur strictly in person at the September Annual General Meeting.

- Proxy voting is permitted as set forth in Article VIII.7.
- Voting shall be by secret ballot. The Election Committee shall oversee the voting process, distribute ballots, and ensure the integrity of the count.
- Only designated Primary or Secondary Members of Voting Units in good standing may cast ballots. Each Voting Unit is entitled to cast its base vote and, if applicable, its bonus vote, up to a maximum of two votes.
- Results shall be announced at the AGM and subsequently published to the membership.

> **Descriptive Note:** Voting for leaders happens in person at the September AGM only. You can vote by proxy if you can't attend (see Article VIII.7). Votes are secret. The Election Committee runs the vote and counts the ballots. Only designated voters can vote, and each Unit can cast up to two votes (base + bonus). Results are announced at the meeting and shared afterward.

### XI.4 Election Committee

**Source:**
At the Spring General Meeting, the Executive shall appoint an Election Committee consisting of at least three members from different Voting Units who are not standing for election in the current cycle. The Committee shall:

- Manage the nomination process,
- Verify candidate eligibility,
- Oversee the campaign period,
- Administer the in-person voting at the AGM,
- Certify the results, and
- Resolve any disputes or challenges.

> **Descriptive Note:** At the Spring Meeting, the Executive appoints an Election Committee of at least three people from different families who are not running for office. This committee handles the whole election: managing nominations, checking candidates, overseeing the campaign, running the vote, certifying results, and settling any disputes.

### XI.5 Assumption of Office

**Source:**
Newly elected Directors shall shadow the outgoing board during the transition period. Official signing authority and duties shall transfer on **November 15th**.

> **Descriptive Note:** New leaders will learn from the outgoing leaders during a transition period. They officially take over their duties and signing authority on November 15th.

### XI.6 Term of Office

**Source:**
Elected Executive members shall serve a two-year term, beginning on November 15th following their election and ending on November 14th of the second year thereafter.

> **Descriptive Note:** Leaders serve a two-year term. Their term starts on November 15th after they're elected and ends on November 14th two years later.

### XI.7 Contested Elections and Recounts

**Source:**
Any challenge to the election process or results must be submitted in writing to the Election Committee within forty-eight (48) hours of the announcement of results. The Committee may conduct a recount if warranted. The Committee's decision on any challenge shall be final.

> **Descriptive Note:** If someone thinks there was a problem with the election, they must tell the Election Committee in writing within 48 hours after results are announced. The Committee can do a recount if needed. Their decision is final.

### XI.8 Vacancies

**Source:**
If a vacancy occurs on the Executive, the remaining Executive members may appoint a qualified member to fill the vacancy for the remainder of the term, as provided in Article XII.4.

> **Descriptive Note:** If a leader steps down mid-term, the remaining Executive members can appoint a qualified person to finish that term. (See Article XII.4.)

## ARTICLE XII: Executive (Board of Directors)

### XII.1 Composition

**Source:**
The Executive shall consist of no fewer than three (3) and no more than seven (7) members, elected by the Voting Units as provided in these By-Laws. This range aligns with the provisions set forth in the Corporation's Articles of Incorporation (Charter).

> **Descriptive Note:** Our leadership team (the Executive) has between 3 and 7 people, elected by the members. This matches what's in our founding charter.

### XII.2 Officers

**Source:**
**Required Executive positions** include: Commodore (Chair), Treasurer, and Harbour Master (Port Captain).

**Optional Executive positions** may include: Vice-Commodore, Secretary, Campground Manager, and Director of Entertainment & Fundraising, as determined by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** We must always have a Commodore (the chair), a Treasurer, and a Harbour Master. Other roles like Vice-Commodore, Secretary, Campground Manager, and Entertainment Director are optional—the Executive can decide whether to have them.

### XII.3 Election and Term of Office

**Source:**
The term of office for each Executive member shall be two (2) years following their election and ending on the second year election of the new Executive. No Executive member may serve more than four (4) consecutive terms without a one-year break.

> **Descriptive Note:** Leaders serve two-year terms. No one can serve more than four terms in a row (that's 8 years) without taking at least one year off.

### XII.4 Vacancies

**Source:**
The Executive may appoint a qualified member to fill any vacancy for the remainder of the term.

> **Descriptive Note:** If a leader steps down mid-term, the remaining leaders can appoint someone qualified to finish that term.

### XII.5 Disqualification of Directors

**Source:**
A person shall be disqualified from serving as a Director if they:

(a) are less than eighteen (18) years of age;
(b) have been declared incapable by a court;
(c) are an undischarged bankrupt; or
(d) have been convicted of an offence involving fraud or dishonesty.

Any Director becoming disqualified shall immediately resign or be removed per Article XIV.

> **Descriptive Note:** You cannot be a leader if you're under 18, have been declared mentally incapable by a court, are an undischarged bankrupt, or have been convicted of fraud or dishonesty. If a leader becomes disqualified, they must resign immediately or be removed.

### XII.6 Executive Meetings & Quorum

**Source:**
Executive meetings are held at least six times per year. A majority of filled Executive positions constitutes a quorum.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Executive meets at least six times a year. For a meeting to be official, more than half of the current leaders must be present.

### XII.7 Spending Authority

**Source:**
The Executive may spend on day-to-day operations without a membership vote. For Maintenance Projects, up to \$2,500 per project is permitted without prior approval. For Capital/Improvement Projects, up to \$1,000 per project is allowed without prior approval. Projects exceeding these thresholds, or multiple projects exceeding \$2,000 in a year, require a majority vote of Voting Units.

Borrowing money requires a two-thirds majority vote of Voting Units. The Corporation must maintain transparency by keeping a list of all projects using the official Project Proposal Template and making it available to members upon request.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Executive can spend money on daily operations without a member vote. For maintenance projects, they can spend up to $2,500 per project without asking members. For new construction or major upgrades, they can spend up to $1,000 per project. Anything above these limits, or if multiple projects add up to more than $2,000 in a year, needs a member vote. Borrowing money always needs a two-thirds member vote. We keep a list of all projects using the official form, available to members who ask.

### XII.8 Banking and Borrowing

**Source:**
Banking documents must be signed by any two of the Commodore, Treasurer, or Secretary. Borrowing money requires a special resolution (two-thirds majority) of Voting Units.

> **Descriptive Note:** For bank transactions, two of these three people must sign: Commodore, Treasurer, or Secretary. Borrowing money always needs a two-thirds vote of the members.

### XII.9 Committees

**Source:**
The Executive may appoint special committees with specific mandates, which report directly to the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Executive can create special committees for specific tasks. These committees report back to the Executive.

- **Director of Entertainment & Fundraising:** Plans and promotes social events, implements fundraising strategies, works with volunteers, manages sponsorships and grants, and coordinates the entertainment and fundraising budget with the Treasurer.

> **Descriptive Note:** Here's what each leader does:
> - **Commodore:** Runs meetings, is part of all committees automatically, and makes sure everyone follows the Code of Conduct.
> - **Vice-Commodore:** Takes over if the Commodore can't do the job.
> - **Secretary:** Takes meeting minutes, sends out notices, keeps official records and the corporate seal, and maintains the member list and document archive.
> - **Treasurer:** Manages money, keeps accurate accounts, deposits funds, gives financial reports at the AGM, and maintains financial records.
> - **Harbour Master:** Looks after the marina facilities and safety.
> - **Campground Manager:** Looks after the campground facilities, safety, and operations.
> - **Director of Entertainment & Fundraising:** Plans social events, runs fundraising, works with volunteers, manages sponsorships and grants, and coordinates the entertainment budget with the Treasurer.

## ARTICLE XIX-A: Public Accountant

### XIX-A.1 Appointment

**Source:**
At each annual meeting of members, the members shall appoint a public accountant to hold office until the close of the next annual meeting. If an appointment is not made, the incumbent public accountant continues in office until a successor is appointed.

> **Descriptive Note:** At every AGM, members vote to appoint an outside accountant. This person serves until the next AGM. If no new appointment is made, the current accountant stays on.

### XIX-A.2 Qualifications

**Source:**
To be eligible as public accountant, a person must:
- Be a member in good standing of an institute or association of accountants incorporated under an Act of a province
- Be independent of the Corporation, its affiliates, and its directors and officers
- Meet any other qualifications required by law

> **Descriptive Note:** The accountant must be a qualified professional, must be independent from the club and its leaders, and must meet all legal requirements.

### XIX-A.3 Independence

**Source:**
Independence is a question of fact. A person is deemed not independent if they or their business partner:
- Is a business partner, director, officer, or employee of the Corporation or any of its affiliates
- Beneficially owns or controls a material interest in the debt obligations of the Corporation or any of its affiliates
- Has been a receiver, liquidator, or trustee in bankruptcy of the Corporation within two years before their proposed appointment

> **Descriptive Note:** The accountant must be truly independent. They cannot be a business partner, leader, or employee of the club. They cannot have a significant financial interest in the club's debts. They cannot have been involved in any bankruptcy or receivership of the club in the past two years.

### XIX-A.4 Removal and Resignation

**Source:**
- The members may by ordinary resolution at a special meeting remove a public accountant from office.
- A public accountant who becomes disqualified shall resign immediately after becoming aware of the disqualification.
- A resignation becomes effective when a written resignation is sent to the Corporation or at the time specified in the resignation, whichever is later.

> **Descriptive Note:** Members can vote to remove the accountant at a special meeting. If the accountant becomes disqualified, they must resign immediately. Their resignation takes effect when they send a written notice or at a later date they specify.

### XIX-A.5 Right to Attend Meetings

**Source:**
The public accountant is entitled to attend meetings of members at the expense of the Corporation and be heard on matters relating to their duties.

> **Descriptive Note:** The accountant can attend member meetings at the club's expense and can speak on matters related to their work.

### XIX-A.6 Duty to Attend

**Source:**
If a director or member gives written notice at least ten (10) days before a meeting of members to the public accountant, the public accountant shall attend the meeting at the expense of the Corporation and answer questions relating to their duties.

> **Descriptive Note:** If a leader or member gives the accountant at least 10 days' notice, the accountant must come to the meeting (at club expense) and answer questions about their work.

### XIX-A.7 Notice of Errors

**Source:**
If the public accountant or a director or officer becomes aware of an error or misstatement in a financial statement on which the public accountant has reported, and if in their opinion the error or misstatement is material, they shall inform each director accordingly. The directors shall then prepare and issue revised financial statements or otherwise inform the members.

> **Descriptive Note:** If the accountant or any leader discovers a significant error in financial statements that the accountant had already reported on, they must tell all the leaders. The leaders must then correct the statements and inform the members.

## ARTICLE XXIV: Dissolution

### XXIV.1 Application of Law

**Source:**
The dissolution of the Corporation shall be governed by Part 14 of the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act*.

> **Descriptive Note:** If we ever close down the club, we follow the rules in the federal Not-for-profit Corporations Act.

### XXIV.2 Distribution of Remaining Property

If the Corporation is dissolved or wound up, any remaining assets after paying all liabilities shall be transferred to one or more qualified non-profit organizations in Canada with purposes similar to those of the Corporation. This provision is unalterable.

If the Corporation is a registered charity or a soliciting corporation, any property remaining on liquidation after the discharge of any liabilities shall be distributed to one or more qualified donees within the meaning of subsection 248(1) of the *Income Tax Act*.

The dissolution shall be carried out in accordance with the procedures set out in the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act*, including the sending of statements of intent to dissolve, articles of dissolution, and obtaining certificates of dissolution from the Director.

> **Descriptive Note:** We follow all the legal steps to close down properly, including filing the right paperwork with the government.

## ARTICLE XXV: By-Law Amendments

**Source:**
- Any proposal to amend, repeal, or create a new By-Law must be submitted to the Executive in writing at least thirty (30) days prior to any General Membership Meeting.
- Notice of all proposed amendments shall be distributed to Voting Units at least fifteen (15) days prior to the meeting.
- Amending or repealing any By-Law requires approval by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of votes of Voting Units present and eligible to vote, provided quorum is met.
- If an amendment affects the rights of a specific class or group of members in a manner different from other members, the members of that class or group are entitled to vote separately as a class or group, and the amendment must be approved by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of that class or group.
- A copy of any by-law, amendment, or repeal shall be sent to the Director under the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act* within the prescribed period after adoption.

> **Descriptive Note:** Here's how we change these rules:
> - Any proposed change must be given to the Executive in writing at least 30 days before a member meeting.
> - All members must be told about proposed changes at least 15 days before the meeting.
> - Changing a bylaw needs a two-thirds vote of the Voting Units present at the meeting, as long as enough members are there (quorum).
> - If a change only affects a specific group of members (like only campers), that group gets to vote separately on it, and it needs two-thirds approval from that group too.
> - After a change is approved, the Secretary must send a copy to the government within the required time.

## ARTICLE XXVII: Definitions

**Source:**
For the purposes of these Bylaws, the following definitions apply:

- **"Public Accountant"** means the public accountant appointed for the Corporation under Article XIX-A.
- **"Special Resolution"** means a resolution passed by a majority of not less than two-thirds of the votes cast on that resolution.
- **"Ordinary Resolution"** means a resolution passed by a majority of the votes cast on that resolution.
- **"Voting Unit"** means a single family unit using the same premises, entitled to one base vote and potentially one bonus vote, up to a maximum of two votes total.

> **Descriptive Note:** Here's what some terms mean:
> - **"Act"**: The federal law that governs not-for-profit corporations in Canada.
> - **"Director"**: The government official appointed to oversee not-for-profit corporations.
> - **"Public Accountant"**: The outside accountant we appoint to review our finances.
> - **"Special Resolution"**: A vote that needs at least two-thirds approval.
> - **"Ordinary Resolution"**: A vote that needs more than half approval.
> - **"Voting Unit"**: A family renting one slip or campsite, with one base vote and possibly one bonus vote.
