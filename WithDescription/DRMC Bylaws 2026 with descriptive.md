# DRMC Bylaws 2026 – Expanded Plain-Language Guide

**Document Version:** 3.3
**Effective Date:** February 27, 2026

This document pairs each section of the official Bylaws with a detailed, easy-to-understand explanation. The original legal text is shown first, followed by a **Descriptive Note** that translates it into everyday language for all members to understand.

The Bylaws are our detailed operating manual. While the Charter is our constitution (setting our fundamental purpose and structure), the Bylaws govern how we run things day-to-day—how meetings work, how voting happens, what leaders do, how fees are set, and much more.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is our legal identity. This name appears on all our legal documents, contracts, and bank accounts.

## ARTICLE II: Objects

**Source:**
The Corporation is established with the primary objective of operating and maintaining a marina and campground for the enjoyment of its members, their families, and their guests. Its aims include providing boaters with secure facilities for boats and equipment, and ensuring campers have access to well-maintained sites and amenities. The Corporation fosters community-building activities, upholds compliance with Federal, Provincial, and Municipal regulations, and encourages a strong community spirit through volunteer squads, social events, and collaborative decision-making. Additionally, the Corporation is responsible for seeking and managing funds for facility maintenance and improvement, and for securing all necessary permits, leases, and licenses required for operations.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is our mission statement—it explains why we exist. Let's break it down:
>
> *   **Run a marina and campground for members:** Our core business is providing these facilities for the people who join. Everything we do serves our members and their guests.
>
> *   **Provide secure facilities for boats:** We give boaters a safe place to keep their boats and equipment—protected from weather, theft, and damage.
>
> *   **Ensure campers have well-maintained sites:** We keep campsites in good condition with nice amenities so campers enjoy their time here.
>
> *   **Foster community-building activities:** We actively create opportunities for people to connect through events and shared experiences.
>
> *   **Comply with all government regulations:** We follow federal, provincial, and municipal laws—getting proper permits, following safety rules, and being good citizens.
>
> *   **Encourage community spirit through volunteers:** We rely on members helping out through volunteer squads, social events, and working together on decisions.
>
> *   **Seek and manage funds for maintenance and improvement:** We actively look for money (fees, grants, fundraising) to keep things in good repair and make improvements.
>
> *   **Secure all necessary permits, leases, and licenses:** Before we can operate, we need official permission from various authorities. This is about making sure we have all the legal paperwork we need to exist and function.
>
> All of these aims work together to create a marina and campground that serves its members well while following the law and building community.

## ARTICLE IV: Membership & Voting Rights

This is one of the most important articles. It explains who can vote, how voting works, and the different types of membership.

### IV.1 The Voting Unit

**Source:**
Membership governance is conducted through Voting Units, defined as a single family unit using the same premises (slip or campsite). Each Voting Unit is entitled to a maximum of **two (2) votes** in total, allocated as follows:

- **One (1) base vote** per Voting Unit based on membership class.
- **One (1) additional bonus vote** if the Unit meets the volunteer requirements in Section IV.2.

No Voting Unit shall cast more than two votes on any single motion.

> **Descriptive Note:** The basic voting group is a "Voting Unit." Think of this as one family or household that rents one boat slip or one campsite. Each Unit can have up to two votes total:
> *   **One automatic base vote** just for being a member in good standing.
> *   **One potential bonus vote** that can be earned by volunteering (see next section).
>
> The key rule: No single Unit can ever cast more than two votes on any issue. This ensures fairness—a family that rents both a slip and a site (Dual Member) doesn't get more voting power than two separate families. Everyone is on a level playing field.

### IV.2 Bonus Vote for Volunteers

**Source:**
To promote active participation, a Voting Unit may earn one (1) additional vote (a "bonus vote") if at least one adult member of the Unit completes 20 or more hours of verified volunteer work in the preceding 12-month period. Volunteer hours are tracked in a public Volunteer Registry. Volunteer hours registered in the Volunteer Registry expire 12 months after they are recorded.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is our way of encouraging members to help out. If at least one adult in your family does 20 or more hours of volunteer work (verified by a Squad Coordinator or Executive member) in the past year, your Unit gets an extra vote.
>
> **Key details:**
> *   Hours are tracked in a public list called the Volunteer Registry so everyone can see progress.
> *   Hours expire after 12 months. You can't do 40 hours one year and have bonus votes for two years—you need to earn them each year.
> *   This creates an incentive for ongoing participation, not just a one-time burst of volunteering.

### IV.3 Voter Eligibility

**Source:**
Only the designated Primary or Secondary Member of a Voting Unit in good standing may exercise the Unit's vote(s). If the Unit is entitled to a bonus vote, it is exercised by the same designated voter.

> **Descriptive Note:** Not everyone in a family can vote—only the one or two people the family officially names as its voters (see IV.7). These designated voters must be "in good standing," which means their fees are paid up and they're not under suspension.
>
> If your Unit has earned a bonus vote, the same designated voter casts both votes. This prevents confusion about who gets to cast the extra vote.

### IV.4 Voting Unit Access Types

**Source:**
Membership access is classified into the following categories, which do not affect voting rights:

- Berth Holder: Holds a seasonal boat slip.
- Seasonal Camper: Holds a seasonal campsite.
- Dual Member: Holds both a boat slip and a campsite.
- Associate Member: Social member with no voting rights and no boat slip or campsite.
- Overnight Guest: Short-term visitor with no voting rights.

> **Descriptive Note:** These are categories that describe how you use the marina. They are about **access**, not voting. Your access type does not affect your voting rights—all Voting Members (Berth Holders, Seasonal Campers, Dual Members) have the same base vote.
>
> *   **Berth Holder:** You rent a boat slip for the season.
> *   **Seasonal Camper:** You rent a campsite for the season.
> *   **Dual Member:** You rent both a slip AND a campsite.
> *   **Associate Member:** You're a social member—you can participate in community activities but don't have a slip or campsite. You **cannot vote**.
> *   **Overnight Guest:** You're here for a short stay. You're not a member and have no voting rights.
>
> This system allows people to be involved at different levels while ensuring voting power stays with those who have the primary facilities.

### IV.5 Unit Classes and Voting Rights

**Source:**
The Corporation shall have **Class A Voting Unit**, divided into the following subcategories. Each subcategory is entitled to **one (1) base vote**:

1.  **Berth Holder**
    Definition: An individual who holds a berth agreement with the Corporation.
    Base Voting Rights: **One (1) vote**.

2.  **Seasonal Camper**
    Definition: An individual who holds a seasonal camping agreement with the Corporation.
    Base Voting Rights: **One (1) vote**.

3.  **Dual Member**
    Definition: An individual who holds both a seasonal camping agreement and a berth agreement with the Corporation.
    Base Voting Rights: **One (1) vote**.

4.  **Certified Volunteer**
    Definition: An individual recognized by the Corporation as a certified volunteer under its established criteria.
    Base Voting Rights: **One (1) vote**.

**Bonus Vote:** Any Voting Unit described in subcategories 1 through 4 above may earn **one (1) additional bonus vote** upon completion of 20 or more verified volunteer hours as set forth in Section IV.2.

> **Descriptive Note:** This section formally lists who gets a base vote. All these groups get one automatic vote:
> *   Berth Holders
> *   Seasonal Campers
> *   Dual Members
> *   Certified Volunteers (people recognized for significant volunteering)
>
> The "Certified Volunteer" category is interesting—it means that someone who volunteers extensively but doesn't have a slip or campsite might still get a vote. This recognizes that volunteers are essential to our community.
>
> And as before, any of these Units can earn a bonus vote by doing 20+ volunteer hours.

### IV.6 Eligibility and Verification

**Source:**
The Corporation establishes and maintains procedures for verifying eligibility within each subcategory and confirming completion of required volunteer hours for the bonus vote.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Executive will create rules to make sure people are in the right membership category and that volunteer hours are real. This might include things like checking agreements for Berth Holders, or having Squad Coordinators sign off on hours. It's about maintaining integrity in our voting system.

### IV.7 Designating Voters

**Source:**
Each Voting Unit must notify the Secretary in writing of its designated Primary Voter. A Secondary Voter may also be named, who may vote only in the absence of the Primary Voter. Only the designated Primary or Secondary Voter may cast the Unit's vote(s), and only one individual may vote on behalf of the Unit at any given meeting or vote.

> **Descriptive Note:** Each family must tell the Secretary in writing who their main voter is. They can also name a backup voter who can only vote if the main person can't be there.
>
> **Why this matters:** This prevents confusion at meetings. Only these named individuals can cast the Unit's votes, and only one person votes per Unit at any time. No "my whole family votes" situations—each Unit gets one voice, with up to two votes behind it.

### IV.8 Voting Procedure

**Source:**
For any vote, the total number of votes cast is the sum of all base votes and bonus votes from participating Voting Units. No Unit may cast more than two votes total.

> **Descriptive Note:** When we count votes, we add up all the base votes (one per participating Unit) and all the bonus votes (for Units that earned them). This gives us the total vote count. The reminder: no Unit gets more than two votes total.

### IV.9 Volunteer Hour Tracking & Verification

**Source:**
The Corporation maintains a public Volunteer Registry to monitor each Unit's progress toward the 20-hour goal. Volunteered and verified volunteer hours must be documented either by the Squad Coordinator or an Executive member. Any disputes regarding volunteer hours are resolved by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** We keep a public list of volunteer hours so everyone can see progress. Hours are recorded by Squad Coordinators or Executive members to ensure accuracy. If there's a disagreement about hours (someone claims they did 20 but the records show less), the Executive settles the dispute. Their decision is final.

### IV.10 Resignation of Membership

**Source:**
A member may resign from the Corporation at any time by delivering a written notice of resignation to the Secretary. The resignation shall be effective upon receipt by the Secretary or on a later date specified in the notice. Upon resignation, the member forfeits all rights and privileges of membership, and no refund of dues or fees shall be provided unless otherwise determined by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** If you want to quit the club, you must tell the Secretary in writing. You can resign immediately or choose a later date. Once you resign, you lose all membership rights and privileges. You generally don't get a refund, though the Executive can make exceptions in special circumstances (like if you paid for a full year but had to resign in the first month due to a medical emergency).

### IV.11 Code of Conduct Agreement

**Source:**
All members must agree to abide by the current Code of Conduct as a condition of membership and to access Corporation facilities.

> **Descriptive Note:** By being a member, you automatically agree to follow our Code of Conduct. This isn't optional—it's a condition of membership. If you don't agree to follow the Code, you can't be a member or use our facilities.

## ARTICLE V: Suspension of Membership

This article explains the serious topic of how and why someone can lose their membership.

### V.1 Termination of Membership

**Source:**
Membership may be terminated for repeated or severe violations following the suspension process outlined in this Article. The affected member shall have the right to appeal the termination decision to the membership at the next General Meeting, where a majority vote of Voting Units present shall decide the appeal. Termination shall be effective immediately upon the Executive's decision, subject to appeal.

> **Descriptive Note:** Membership can be ended for serious or repeated rule-breaking. If the Executive decides to terminate someone, that decision takes effect immediately. However, the affected person has the right to appeal to all members at the next General Meeting. At that meeting, a majority vote of the Voting Units present decides whether to uphold or overturn the termination. This provides a check on Executive power—the final decision rests with the membership.

### V.2 Grounds for Suspension

**Source:**
The Executive may suspend a membership for the following reasons:

- Failure to pay fees after due notice
- Violation of Corporation rules or bylaws, including the Code of Conduct
- Conduct deemed detrimental to the Corporation, its members, or its operations

> **Descriptive Note:** Here are the specific reasons someone can be suspended:
> *   **Not paying fees** after being reminded (this protects the club's finances).
> *   **Breaking rules**—including the Bylaws themselves, the Code of Conduct, or any other official rules.
> *   **Harmful behavior**—anything that damages the club, its property, or its members (like harassment, vandalism, or creating unsafe conditions).
>
> These grounds cover both financial and behavioral issues.

### V.3 Process and Appeal

**Source:**
Prior to any expulsion, the member shall receive written notice of the allegations and be given an opportunity to be heard by the active members at a meeting.

> **Descriptive Note:** Fairness is built into the process. Before anyone is expelled, they must:
> 1.  Receive a written notice explaining exactly what they're accused of.
> 2.  Be given a chance to explain their side at a meeting where members can hear them.
>
> This ensures no one is expelled without knowing why and without having a chance to respond.

### V.4 Expulsion Decision

**Source:**
Following the hearing, a petition for expulsion may be circulated among the active members. The member shall be expelled if the petition receives the signatures of at least 51% of the active members of the Corporation.

> **Descriptive Note:** After the hearing, a petition to expel the member can be circulated to all active members. If at least 51% of all active members (not just those at the meeting) sign the petition, the member is expelled. This is a high bar—it requires a majority of all members, not just those who attended the hearing. This ensures expulsion has broad support.

### V.5 Notification

**Source:**
The Executive shall promptly notify the suspended member in writing.

> **Descriptive Note:** Once a decision is made (whether suspension or expulsion), the Executive must tell the member in writing right away. No surprises—official notice is required.

### V.6 Fee Forfeiture

**Source:**
Suspended members forfeit their membership fee and are not entitled to a refund.

> **Descriptive Note:** If you're suspended, you lose your membership fee. No refund. This is a consequence of the behavior that led to suspension.

### V.7 Loss of Membership for Non-Use

**Source:**
A membership may be terminated if the member does not utilize their assigned slip or campsite for a full season, as follows:

**V.7.1 Definition of Non-Use**
- Non-use means the member's assigned slip or campsite remains vacant and unoccupied for the entire operational season (May 1 to October 31)
- Occasional, sporadic, or de minimis use (e.g., less than 7 total days occupied) may be deemed non-use at the Executive's discretion

> **Descriptive Note:** You can lose your spot for not using it. This section defines what counts as non-use:
> *   Your slip or campsite is completely empty for the whole season (May 1 to October 31).
> *   Using it very little—like less than 7 days total—might also count as non-use. The Executive decides based on the situation.
>
> The purpose is to ensure that scarce resources (slips and sites) are used by active members, not held vacant by people who aren't using them.

**V.7.2 Notification Requirement**
- Members anticipating non-use for an upcoming season must notify the Secretary in writing by April 1st
- Members who fail to notify and do not use their space forfeit their membership rights as outlined below

> **Descriptive Note:** If you know you won't use your spot next season, you must tell the Secretary in writing by April 1st. This gives the club time to plan and potentially offer your spot to someone on the waiting list. If you don't notify and then don't use it, you face the consequences below.

**V.7.3 Consequences of Non-Use**
- After one season of non-use, the member's assigned slip or campsite shall be forfeited and offered to the waiting list
- The member's Voting Unit status shall be converted to Associate (Non-Voting) Member status for the following season
- After two consecutive seasons of non-use, membership shall be terminated entirely, and the member must reapply as a new member if they wish to return

> **Descriptive Note:** Here's what happens:
> *   **First season of non-use:** You lose your specific slip or site—it goes to the waiting list. Your voting membership becomes a non-voting Associate membership for the next season.
> *   **Two straight seasons of non-use:** Your membership is completely ended. If you want to come back, you have to reapply as a new member (including paying any initiation fees and going on the waiting list again).
>
> This creates a clear progression: first you lose your spot, then if you still don't use it, you lose your membership entirely.

**V.7.4 Exceptions**
The Executive may grant exceptions to this policy for:
- Medical circumstances (with supporting documentation)
- Military service or extended work assignments abroad
- Major repairs to vessel or RV (with proof and timeline)
- Other exceptional circumstances as determined by the Executive

Requests for exceptions must be submitted in writing prior to the season.

> **Descriptive Note:** Life happens. The Executive can make exceptions for valid reasons:
> *   Medical issues (with doctor's note or similar)
> *   Military service or long work assignments away from home
> *   Major repairs to your boat or RV (with proof and a timeline)
> *   Other truly exceptional situations the Executive agrees to
>
> **Important:** You must ask for an exception **in writing before the season starts**. You can't wait until after you've already not used it and then ask for forgiveness.

**V.7.5 Fee Forfeiture**
Members whose membership is terminated under this section forfeit all fees paid and are not entitled to any refund.

> **Descriptive Note:** If your membership ends because of non-use, you lose your fees. No refund. This is consistent with other termination situations.

This article refers to a separate document that contains detailed behavioral rules.

### V-A.1 Adoption by Reference

**Source:**
The Corporation maintains a separate "Code of Conduct" document, which is incorporated by reference into these bylaws. Compliance with the Code of Conduct is mandatory for all members.

> **Descriptive Note:** There is a separate document called the "Code of Conduct" that contains detailed rules about behavior—things like noise levels, pet rules, safety practices, and respectful treatment of others. Even though it's a separate document, it's legally part of these Bylaws. Everyone must follow it. "Incorporated by reference" means we don't have to print the whole Code here; it's enough to say it exists and is part of our rules.

### V-A.2 Availability and Distribution

**Source:**
The current Code of Conduct shall be available to all members via the Corporation's document repository, postings in common areas, and distribution to new members upon registration. Members are responsible for familiarizing themselves with the current Code of Conduct.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Code of Conduct is always available to members. You can find it:
> *   In our online document repository
> *   Posted in common areas around the marina and campground
> *   Given to new members when they join
>
> **Crucial point:** It's your responsibility to know what's in the Code. "I didn't read it" is not an excuse for breaking the rules. Ignorance of the rules does not protect you from consequences.

### V-A.3 Amendment Process

**Source:**
The Code of Conduct may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the Executive. Substantive changes must be circulated to members for comment at least 30 days prior to adoption. Minor administrative or safety-related updates may be adopted without prior member comment.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Executive can change the Code of Conduct with a two-thirds vote. However, there are different levels of change:
> *   **Substantive changes** (big, important changes) must be shared with members for comments at least 30 days before being adopted. This gives members a chance to provide input.
> *   **Minor updates** (like fixing typos, updating contact information, or small safety tweaks) can be made without prior comment.
>
> This balances flexibility (we can update quickly when needed) with transparency (members get a say in major changes).

### V-A.4 Enforcement

**Source:**
Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in suspension of membership under Article V.

> **Descriptive Note:** If you break the Code of Conduct, you can be suspended or expelled using the process in Article V. The Code isn't just suggestions—it's enforceable rules with real consequences.

## ARTICLE VI: Membership Fees and Pricing

This article explains how fees are set and collected.

### VI.1 Annual Fee Setting Process

**Source:**
Fees are recommended by the Executive and approved by the general membership at the Annual General Meeting. The Executive must present a detailed fee schedule for the upcoming year, including justifications for any proposed increases.

Fee calculations shall be based on the following methodologies:

- **Berth Holders:** Annual fee = vessel length overall (LOA) in feet × rate per foot established annually by the membership.
- **Seasonal Campers:** Annual fee = fixed rate per campsite established annually by the membership.
- **Dual Members:** Annual fee = (boat LOA × rate per foot) + fixed campsite rate.
- **Associate Members:** Annual fee = fixed social membership rate established annually by the membership.

> **Descriptive Note:** Here's how we set fees:
> 1.  The Executive recommends a fee schedule for the next year.
> 2.  They must show a detailed breakdown and explain any proposed increases.
> 3.  The general membership votes to approve (or not) at the AGM.
>
> **How fees are calculated:**
> *   **Berth Holders:** Pay based on boat length. If the rate is $20 per foot and your boat is 25 feet, you pay $500.
> *   **Seasonal Campers:** Pay a flat rate per campsite (same for everyone).
> *   **Dual Members:** Pay both—the boat fee (length × rate) plus the campsite flat rate.
> *   **Associate Members:** Pay a flat social rate.
>
> This system is transparent—you can see exactly how your fee is determined.

### VI.2 Annual Price Increase Cap

**Source:**
Annual fee increases are capped at 4% above the previous year's fees unless the Executive provides detailed justification and the increase is specifically approved by a majority vote at the September AGM. AGM notice must include the proposed fee schedule and rationale for exceeding the cap.

> **Descriptive Note:** Fees usually can't go up by more than 4% from the previous year. This protects members from sudden, large increases. If the Executive thinks a bigger increase is needed, they must:
> 1.  Provide a detailed explanation why.
> 2.  Get specific approval from members at the AGM (a majority vote).
> 3.  Include the proposed increase and rationale in the AGM notice so members know in advance.
>
> This ensures that any increase above 4% is carefully justified and has member support.

### VI.3 Emergency Fee Adjustment

**Source:**
In extraordinary circumstances, the Executive may convene a Special Membership Meeting to consider fee adjustments outside the normal cycle. Emergency fee adjustments require approval by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of Voting Units present.

> **Descriptive Note:** Sometimes unexpected things happen—a major repair, an insurance cost spike, a new regulation requiring expensive changes. In these extraordinary circumstances, the Executive can call a Special Meeting to discuss an emergency fee change. Any such change needs a two-thirds majority of the Voting Units present at that meeting. This higher threshold ensures broad support for emergency measures.

### VI.4 Membership Year, Invoicing, and Payment

**Source:**
- **Membership Year:** All membership rights expire on April 30th each year.
- **Invoicing:** Invoices for the upcoming membership year shall be issued on **April 1st**.
- **Payment Due Date:** All fees are due by **May 1st**. Fees unpaid after May 1st shall be subject to a penalty charge as determined by the Executive, and the assigned slip or campsite may be forfeited and offered to the waiting list.
- **Late Fees:** The Executive may establish a late fee schedule, which shall be communicated to members with the invoice.

> **Descriptive Note:** Here's the timeline:
> *   **Membership Year:** Runs from May 1 to April 30. Your membership rights expire on April 30 each year—to continue, you must pay for the next year.
> *   **Invoicing:** Bills go out on April 1 for the upcoming year (starting May 1).
> *   **Payment Due:** Fees are due by May 1. If you don't pay by then:
>     *   You may face a late penalty.
>     *   You could lose your assigned slip or site—it might be offered to someone on the waiting list.
>
> This creates a strong incentive to pay on time. The Executive sets the late fee amount and includes it with the invoice so you know the consequences.

### VI.5 Fee Structure Transparency

**Source:**
The Executive shall provide a detailed breakdown of how membership fees are allocated across operational areas. This breakdown will be included in Spring General Meeting materials and available to members upon request.

> **Descriptive Note:** Members have a right to know where their money goes. The Executive will provide a breakdown showing how fees are spent—things like insurance, maintenance, utilities, administration, etc. This breakdown is included in Spring Meeting materials and is also available to any member who asks. Transparency builds trust.

### VI.6 Membership Rights Transfer

**Source:**
Membership rights are not transferable except in the event of a member's death (to a surviving spouse) or as provided for Unit transfers in Article VII.

> **Descriptive Note:** Generally, you can't give or sell your membership to someone else. There are two exceptions:
> 1.  **Death of a member:** Membership can transfer to a surviving spouse.
> 2.  **Unit transfers:** As detailed in Article VII, in certain circumstances (like selling your boat) you can transfer your membership.
>
> Otherwise, memberships are personal to you and can't be transferred.

### VI.7 Lifetime Memberships

This section deals with a special category of membership.

**Source:**
**VI.7.1 Grandfathering of Existing Lifetime Memberships**
All individuals who held a valid lifetime membership as of the 2025 season are hereby grandfathered and shall retain their lifetime membership status under the terms set forth in this section.

> **Descriptive Note:** People who already had a lifetime membership before 2025 keep it. They are "grandfathered" in—meaning the new rules don't take away their existing status. They continue as lifetime members under the rules in this section.

**Source:**
**VI.7.2 Rights and Privileges**
Lifetime members shall enjoy the following rights:
- Exemption from annual membership fees (including any base fees or social dues).
- Full voting rights as a Voting Unit, subject to the two-vote maximum in Article IV.
- Access to all Corporation facilities and participation in activities, subject to applicable usage fees (e.g., boat slip fees based on vessel length, campsite fees at the seasonal fixed rate, etc.) and compliance with all rules and regulations.

> **Descriptive Note:** Lifetime members don't pay annual membership fees. They have full voting rights (up to two votes). However, they still pay **usage fees**—like slip rental based on boat length or campsite rental. They also must follow all rules, just like everyone else. So a lifetime member doesn't pay the annual "membership fee" but still pays to actually use a slip or site.

**Source:**
**VI.7.3 Non-Transferability**
Lifetime memberships are personal to the individual member and are not transferable to any other person, including family members, except as provided in Article VII for transfers upon death to a surviving spouse who was part of the same Voting Unit.

> **Descriptive Note:** A lifetime membership belongs to that specific person. You can't give it or sell it to anyone else—not even your kids. The only exception is transfer to a surviving spouse upon death, as allowed in Article VII.

**Source:**
**VI.7.4 Revocation**
A lifetime membership may be revoked only in accordance with Article V (Suspension of Membership) for cause, or if the member resigns in writing. Upon revocation or resignation, all rights cease and no refund of any kind shall be provided.

> **Descriptive Note:** A lifetime membership can only be taken away for serious rule-breaking (following the process in Article V) or if the member voluntarily resigns in writing. If that happens, all rights end and there's no refund. This protects the club from having to continue privileges for someone who was expelled.

**Source:**
**VI.7.5 Record Keeping**
The Secretary shall maintain a register of all lifetime members, which shall be included in the corporate records.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Secretary keeps an official list of all lifetime members. This is part of our corporate records, so we always know who has this special status.

### VI.8 New Memberships and Initiation Fee

**Source:**
**VI.8.1 Availability of New Memberships**
New memberships shall be granted only when an assigned slip (for Berth Holders) or campsite (for Seasonal Campers) becomes available. The Corporation shall maintain a waiting list of applicants in the order of application received, and vacancies shall be offered to the next qualified applicant on the waiting list.

> **Descriptive Note:** New memberships are only available when someone leaves and frees up a slip or site. We keep a waiting list in the order people applied. When a spot opens, we offer it to the next person on the list. This is fair and transparent.

**Source:**
**VI.8.2 One-Time Initiation Fee**
All new members admitted to the Corporation shall pay a one-time, non-refundable initiation fee in addition to the applicable annual seasonal fees. The amount of the initiation fee shall be established annually by the Executive and presented to the membership for information at the Annual General Meeting.

> **Descriptive Note:** New members pay a one-time initiation fee on top of their regular annual fees. This fee is non-refundable—even if they quit after a month. The Executive sets the amount each year and tells members about it at the AGM. This fee helps cover the costs of bringing in new members and recognizes the value of joining an established community.

**Source:**
**VI.8.3 Exceptions**
Transfers of membership rights under Article VII are not subject to the initiation fee. The initiation fee does not apply to Associate Members unless otherwise determined by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** Two exceptions to the initiation fee:
> 1.  People who get a membership through a transfer (Article VII) don't pay it—they're taking over an existing membership, not creating a new one.
> 2.  Associate Members (social members) usually don't pay it, unless the Executive decides otherwise.
>
> This makes sense—transfers aren't "new" memberships, and Associate Members have a different, non-voting status.

## ARTICLE VII: Transfer of Membership Rights

This article explains the rules for transferring a membership to someone else.

### VII.1 Eligibility for Transfer

**Source:**
Voting Units may request to transfer membership rights under the following circumstances:

- Sale of the primary asset associated with the membership (boat or camping equipment)
- Relocation of the primary residence
- Other significant life circumstances as approved by the Executive

**Lifetime memberships are not transferable** except as provided in VI.7.4.

> **Descriptive Note:** You can ask to transfer your membership in these situations:
> *   You sell your boat or RV (the primary reason for having the membership).
> *   You move away from the area.
> *   Another major life event that the Executive agrees is a valid reason.
>
> Remember: Lifetime memberships can't be transferred except to a surviving spouse. This section is mainly for regular memberships.

### VII.2 Transfer Process

**Source:**
The current Voting Unit must submit a written request to the Secretary, stating the reason for transfer, providing contact information for the proposed new Unit, and including written consent from all Unit members. The proposed new Unit must meet all eligibility requirements, and the Executive must approve the transfer by majority vote.

> **Descriptive Note:** To transfer, you must:
> 1.  Submit a written request to the Secretary explaining why you want to transfer.
> 2.  Provide contact information for the person or family you want to transfer to.
> 3.  Get written consent from everyone in your Voting Unit.
> 4.  The proposed new member must meet all eligibility requirements (like being able to pay fees, follow rules, etc.).
> 5.  The Executive must approve the transfer by majority vote.
>
> This ensures transfers are legitimate and the new member is qualified.

### VII.3 Conditions of Transfer

**Source:**
The transfer includes all rights and responsibilities associated with the membership category. The new Unit assumes membership for the remainder of the current year. No new membership fee is required for transfers within the same year, and the transfer does not result in an additional membership slot.

> **Descriptive Note:** When a transfer happens:
> *   The new member takes over all rights and responsibilities of the membership.
> *   They get the membership for the rest of the current year (no need to wait for next season).
> *   No extra membership fee is charged for that year—they're just taking over the existing paid membership.
> *   This doesn't create a new membership slot—it's just filling the existing one. The total number of memberships stays the same.

### VII.4 Limitations

**Source:**
Only one transfer per membership is permitted each year. Transfers may not be used to circumvent waiting lists or membership caps. The Corporation may charge an administrative fee for processing transfers.

> **Descriptive Note:** Limits on transfers:
> *   Only one transfer per membership per year (prevents "membership flipping").
> *   Transfers can't be used to jump the waiting list—if someone wants a membership, they should go on the list like everyone else.
> *   The club may charge a small administrative fee to cover the cost of processing the transfer paperwork.

### VII.5 Effective Date

**Source:**
The transfer is effective upon Executive approval and completion of all required documentation.

> **Descriptive Note:** The transfer is official when:
> 1.  The Executive approves it.
> 2.  All required paperwork is completed and submitted.
>
> Until both things happen, the transfer isn't final.

## ARTICLE VIII: Membership Meetings

This article explains the different types of meetings we hold and how they work.

### VIII.1 Annual General Meeting

**Source:**
The Annual General Meeting (AGM) shall take place near season closing of each year.
The order of business at the AGM shall include the following:
- Call to Order and Approval of Previous Minutes: Opening of the meeting and approval of minutes from the previous meeting.
- Activity Report: Presentation of the year's activities by the Commodore (or presiding officer).
- Financial Report: Presentation of the financial report by the Treasurer.
- Squad Reports: Reports from Squad committees, as applicable.
- Project Report previous season: Presentation and review of project status and outcomes for projects completed or underway during the current year.
- Project Plan Next season: Presentation of proposed project plans and capital initiatives for the upcoming year (in accordance with Article X).
- Pricing for the Next Season: Presentation and approval of the membership fee schedule for the upcoming year.
- Election: Election of Executive members (in accordance with Article XI).
- Governance Amendments: Decision on any proposed amendments to the By-Laws or Corporate documents, if applicable.
- Other Business: Any other matter brought before the meeting in accordance with the agenda.

The meeting format may be in person, web conference, or hybrid, as determined by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** The AGM is our most important meeting of the year. It happens near the end of the season (September). Here's what we do:
> *   **Open and approve minutes:** Start the meeting and approve the written record of the last meeting.
> *   **Activity report:** The Commodore tells everyone what happened during the year.
> *   **Financial report:** The Treasurer presents the financial picture.
> *   **Squad reports:** Volunteer groups share what they've done.
> *   **Project reports:** Review projects from the past year.
> *   **Future project plans:** Hear about proposed projects for next year.
> *   **Set next year's fees:** Vote on the fee schedule for the coming year.
> *   **Elections:** Vote for new leaders.
> *   **Bylaw changes:** Vote on any proposed changes to our rules.
> *   **Other business:** Any other items members want to discuss (following the agenda).
>
> The meeting can be in person, online, or a mix of both—the Executive decides the format.

### VIII.2 Spring General Meeting

**Source:**
A Spring General Meeting shall be held in April of each year, with the format determined by the Executive.
The order of business at the Spring General Meeting shall include the following:
- Call to Order and Approval of Previous Minutes: Opening of the meeting and approval of minutes from the previous meeting.
- Project Voting: Presentation and vote on any proposed projects requiring membership approval (in accordance with Articles IX and X).
- Budget Presentation: Presentation and approval of the annual operating budget for the upcoming fiscal year by the Treasurer.
- Special Committees Plan: Presentation of plans and objectives by special committees for the upcoming season.
- Seasons What's new: Any changes to the upcoming season (Insurance, Hours, Services).
- Governance Voting: Discussion and voting on any proposed amendments to the By-Laws or Corporate documents, if applicable.
- Other Business: Any other matter brought before the meeting in accordance with the agenda.

Minutes from this meeting will announce voting results.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Spring Meeting happens in April, just before the new season starts. Here's what we do:
> *   **Open and approve minutes:** Start and approve last meeting's record.
> *   **Project voting:** Vote on any projects that need member approval.
> *   **Budget approval:** Vote on the operating budget for the coming year.
> *   **Committee plans:** Hear what special committees plan to do.
> *   **Season updates:** Learn about any changes for the upcoming season (insurance, hours, services).
> *   **Bylaw changes:** Vote on any proposed rule changes.
> *   **Other business:** Any other items members want to discuss.
>
> Results of votes are announced in the minutes.

### VIII.3 Seasonal Update Meetings

**Source:**
Two Seasonal Update Meetings are held during the operational season to ensure transparent communication and ongoing member engagement:

- June: First Seasonal Update Meeting
- August: Second Seasonal Update Meeting

Agendas include project updates, financial summaries, operational reports, squad updates, and open member forums. These meetings are informational and consultative; no binding votes or bylaw amendments are held.

> **Descriptive Note:** We have two informal meetings during the season (June and August) just to keep everyone informed. These are for:
> *   Project updates
> *   Financial summaries
> *   Operational reports
> *   Squad updates
> *   Open forums where members can ask questions and give input
>
> **Important:** No voting happens at these meetings. They're purely for communication and consultation—a chance to share information and get feedback before decisions are made at voting meetings.

### VIII.4 Decision Making

**Source:**
Decisions at membership meetings are determined by majority vote of total votes cast by Voting Units present in person or participating via web conference, unless stated otherwise. Each Voting Unit's vote(s) shall be counted in accordance with Article IV.

> **Descriptive Note:** How we make decisions at meetings:
> *   Most decisions are made by a simple majority—more than half of the votes cast.
> *   Votes are counted from Voting Units present (either in person or online).
> *   Each Unit's votes are counted as per Article IV (base vote + bonus vote if earned).
>
> Some decisions (like bylaw changes or borrowing money) need a higher threshold, which is noted elsewhere.

### VIII.5 Special Meetings

**Source:**
A Special Membership Meeting may be called by the Executive or by written request of at least 15 Voting Units. Notice must be circulated at least fifteen (15) days prior to the meeting and must include the purpose and web conference instructions if applicable.

> **Descriptive Note:** Sometimes we need a meeting between the regular ones. A Special Meeting can be called by:
> *   The Executive, or
> *   A written petition signed by at least 15 Voting Units
>
> Notice must be given at least 15 days in advance, and it must explain the purpose of the meeting and how to join online if that's an option. This ensures everyone knows why we're meeting and can participate.

### VIII.6 Notice of Meetings

**Source:**
Notice of the Annual General Meeting or any Special Meeting shall be given to all members not less than twenty-one (21) days nor more than sixty (60) days before the meeting date, via email, postal mail, or other electronic means as recorded in the membership register. Notice for Seasonal Update Meetings or the Spring General Meeting shall be given at least fourteen (14) days in advance.

> **Descriptive Note:** Different meetings have different notice requirements:
> *   **AGM and Special Meetings:** Notice must be given 21 to 60 days in advance, by email, mail, or other electronic means.
> *   **Spring Meeting and Seasonal Updates:** Notice must be given at least 14 days in advance.
>
> Longer notice for important meetings (AGM, Special) gives members time to prepare. Shorter notice for less formal meetings keeps things moving.

### VIII.7 Quorum

**Source:**
One-third (1/3) of eligible Voting Units constitutes a quorum for any General or Special Membership Meeting. No quorum is required for Seasonal Update Meetings.

> **Descriptive Note:** "Quorum" is the minimum number of members that must be present for decisions to be valid. For our meetings:
> *   **AGM, Spring Meeting, Special Meetings:** At least one-third of all Voting Units must be present (in person or online) for votes to count.
> *   **Seasonal Update Meetings:** No quorum needed—these are informational only.
>
> This ensures that important decisions aren't made by just a handful of people.

### VIII.8 Notice & Web Conference Details

**Source:**
Written notice of all General Membership Meetings must be provided at least twenty-one (21) days in advance for the Annual and Spring Meetings, and at least fourteen (14) days in advance for the Seasonal Update Meetings.

> **Descriptive Note:** This repeats the notice requirements: 21 days for AGM and Spring Meeting, 14 days for Seasonal Updates. Consistency matters.

### VIII.9 Participation via Web Conference

**Source:**
Members participating by web conference are considered present for all purposes. The Corporation shall ensure reasonable opportunity for participation via web conference technology.

> **Descriptive Note:** If you join a meeting online, you're considered "present" just like being there in person—your vote counts, you can ask questions, etc. The club will make sure online participation works reasonably well (good audio, ability to speak, etc.). This ensures members who can't attend in person can still participate fully.

### VIII.10 Action Without a Meeting

**Source:**
Actions required or permitted at a membership meeting may be conducted without an in-person meeting if the Corporation delivers an electronic or paper ballot to every Voting Unit entitled to vote, provided quorum and approval thresholds are met.

> **Descriptive Note:** Sometimes we can vote without holding a meeting. This might happen by:
> *   Sending electronic ballots (like online voting)
> *   Sending paper ballots by mail
>
> This is allowed as long as:
> *   Every Voting Unit gets a ballot.
> *   The same quorum and approval rules apply (e.g., if a decision normally needs 50% of votes cast, it still needs that).
>
> This gives us flexibility—we can make decisions without scheduling a full meeting.

## ARTICLE IX: Online Project Voting

This article explains how we can vote on projects online without holding a physical meeting.

### IX.1 Purpose and Scope

**Source:**
The Corporation may conduct online votes for specific, well-defined projects to facilitate member participation and efficient decision-making. A "Project" refers to a discrete capital expenditure, improvement initiative, or significant operational change, fully defined and costed using the official Project Proposal Template specified in Article X.

> **Descriptive Note:** We can vote online on specific projects. This makes it easier for members to participate—no need to attend a meeting. A "Project" is a specific thing that costs money or makes significant changes. It must be fully planned and priced using the official form described in Article X. This ensures we only vote on well-defined, properly costed initiatives.

### IX.2 Initiation of a Project Vote

**Source:**
A Project Vote may be initiated by a majority vote of the Executive or by a petition signed by at least 15% of Voting Units submitted to the Secretary.

> **Descriptive Note:** An online project vote can be started in two ways:
> 1.  The Executive votes (by majority) to put it to a vote.
> 2.  Members circulate a petition—if at least 15% of Voting Units sign it and give it to the Secretary, that also forces a vote.
>
> This gives both leaders and members the power to bring projects to a vote.

### IX.3 Project Definition and Information Package

**Source:**
Before voting, the Executive must prepare and distribute a comprehensive Project Information Package using the official Project Proposal Template.

> **Descriptive Note:** Before any vote, members need information. The Executive must prepare a complete package about the project—costs, timeline, benefits, risks—using the official form. This ensures everyone has the same information before voting.

### IX.4 Notice and Voting Period

**Source:**
The Project Information Package shall be distributed to all Voting Units at least fourteen (14) days before the online vote closes. The voting period must remain open for at least seven (7) consecutive days.

> **Descriptive Note:** Timing rules:
> *   Information must be sent at least 14 days before the vote closes—giving members time to read and think.
> *   The voting period itself must be open for at least 7 days—giving members time to cast their votes.
>
> This prevents rushed decisions and ensures everyone has a fair chance to participate.

### IX.5 Voting Platform

**Source:**
Votes are cast via a secure, third-party online voting platform, which ensures Unit authentication, vote secrecy, and result integrity. The system must apply the correct number of votes per Voting Unit (base vote plus any bonus vote), verified by the Volunteer Registry.

> **Descriptive Note:** We use a secure online voting system that:
> *   Makes sure each Unit can only vote once (authentication).
> *   Keeps votes secret (no one knows how you voted).
> *   Counts votes accurately.
> *   Automatically applies the right number of votes for each Unit (base + bonus) based on the Volunteer Registry.
>
> This ensures fair, secure, accurate voting.

### IX.6 Quorum and Passing Threshold

**Source:**
At least 25% of eligible Voting Units must cast votes for validity. A motion passes with a simple majority, unless it involves borrowing money, in which case a two-thirds (2/3) majority is required.

> **Descriptive Note:** Rules for vote validity:
> *   **Quorum:** At least 25% of all Voting Units must vote for the result to count. This prevents a tiny minority from making decisions.
> *   **Passing threshold:**
>     *   Most projects: Simple majority (more than 50% of votes cast) passes.
>     *   Borrowing money: Needs two-thirds majority (higher threshold because borrowing is more serious).
>
> This balances participation requirements with decision-making.

### IX.7 Certification and Results

**Source:**
The Secretary and Treasurer, or an independent third party appointed by the Executive, certify the results. Results must be announced to the membership within seven (7) days of the vote closing, including the number of Units voting and the total vote count. Certified results from successful online Project Votes carry the same force as resolutions passed at a General Membership Meeting.

> **Descriptive Note:** After voting:
> *   Results are certified by the Secretary and Treasurer (or an outside person if needed).
> *   Results must be announced to all members within 7 days, including how many Units voted and the total vote count.
> *   Approved projects are just as official as if they were voted on at a regular meeting.
>
> This ensures transparency and gives online votes the same legal weight as in-person votes.

## ARTICLE X: Project Definition and Management

This article defines what counts as a project and how projects are managed.

### X.1 Purpose

**Source:**
This Article provides clarity and transparency in spending authority and membership voting requirements by defining what constitutes a "Project" and establishing a standardized proposal process.

> **Descriptive Note:** The purpose of this article is to make things clear and fair. It defines what counts as a project and sets up a standard way to propose one. This ensures everyone understands how spending decisions are made and when members get to vote.

### X.2 Definition of a Project

**Source:**
A "Project" is any discrete initiative involving capital expenditure or significant changes to Corporation property or operations, with a defined scope, timeline, and budget. Projects may be executed in one or multiple phases.

> **Descriptive Note:** A project is any specific job that:
> *   Costs money (capital expenditure)
> *   Makes significant changes to our property or how we operate
> *   Has a clear goal, timeline, and budget
>
> Projects can be done all at once or in stages. For example, building a new dock might be a multi-phase project over several years.

### X.3 Project Classification

**Source:**
- Maintenance Project: Involves repair, replacement, or restoration of existing property to its original condition or intended function.
- Capital/Improvement Project: Involves new construction, expansion, enhancement, or major modification that adds new functionality, capacity, or features.

> **Descriptive Note:** Two types of projects:
> *   **Maintenance:** Fixing or replacing something that already exists. Example: Repairing an old dock.
> *   **Capital/Improvement:** Building something new or making a big upgrade that adds new features. Example: Adding a new building or expanding the campground.
>
> The distinction matters because different spending limits apply (see XII.7).

### X.4 Project Phasing

**Source:**
- Single-Phase Project: Unified scope executed continuously from start to finish.
- Multi-Phase Project: Divided into sequential phases, each with its own scope, budget, and timeline, possibly dependent on completion of previous phases and requiring separate approval.

> **Descriptive Note:** Projects can be:
> *   **Single-phase:** One continuous job from start to finish.
> *   **Multi-phase:** Broken into stages. Each stage has its own plan, budget, and timeline. Later stages might depend on earlier ones being done. Each phase might need its own approval.
>
> This allows us to tackle big projects over time without committing to everything at once.

### X.5 What is Not a Project

**Source:**
Routine operations and maintenance activities, such as annual opening/closing, lawn care, minor repairs, and purchasing consumable supplies, do not require a project proposal or membership vote if within approved budget and Executive authority.

> **Descriptive Note:** Regular day-to-day stuff does not count as a "Project." This includes:
> *   Opening and closing for the season
> *   Mowing the lawn
> *   Small repairs
> *   Buying supplies (like cleaning products, light bulbs)
>
> These things don't need a special proposal or member vote, as long as they're in the approved budget and within the Executive's spending authority. This keeps things practical—we don't vote on every light bulb purchase.

### X.6 Mandatory Project Proposal Process

**Source:**
Any initiative meeting the definition of a Project must be documented using the official Project Proposal Template, which is required for both Executive-approved projects and those requiring membership vote.

> **Descriptive Note:** If something meets the definition of a Project, it must be written up using the official Project Proposal Template. This applies whether the Executive is approving it themselves or it needs a membership vote. The template ensures consistency—every project is documented the same way.

### X.7 Template Compliance

**Source:**
The Project Information Package for online Project Voting must use the official Project Proposal Template. Incomplete templates may be rejected until all required information is provided.

> **Descriptive Note:** Any project going to an online vote must use the official template. If the form isn't filled out completely, it can be sent back until all the information is there. This ensures members have complete information before voting.

### X.8 Template Maintenance and Updates

**Source:**
The official Project Proposal Template is maintained as a separate document in the corporate repository. Updates require a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the Executive, and substantive changes must be circulated to members for comment at least 30 days prior to adoption.

> **Descriptive Note:** The project template is a separate document in our archives. Changing it requires:
> *   A two-thirds vote of the Executive (not just a majority).
> *   For big changes, members must get a chance to comment at least 30 days before adoption.
>
> This ensures the template itself—a key tool for transparency—isn't changed lightly or without member input.

## ARTICLE XI: Election Cycle

This article explains how we elect our leaders.

### XI.1 Nomination Window (August 1 – August 21)

**Source:**
Nominations for Executive positions shall be accepted via a secure digital form. Any voting member in good standing may nominate themselves or another member (with the nominee's written consent). The Election Committee shall verify the eligibility of all candidates.

> **Descriptive Note:** From August 1 to 21, you can nominate someone (or yourself) for a leadership position using an online form. If you nominate someone else, you need their written consent (they have to agree to run). The Election Committee checks that all candidates are eligible (e.g., are members in good standing, meet any other requirements).

### XI.2 Campaign Period (August 22 – September AGM)

**Source:**
Candidates shall have the opportunity to present themselves to the membership through a hybrid approach:

- **Digital:** Candidate bios, photographs, and mission statements shall be posted to the member portal for review by all Voting Units.
- **In-Person:** Candidates may engage with members at the Marina during the August Seasonal Update Meeting, which shall include a dedicated forum for candidate introductions and questions.

> **Descriptive Note:** From August 22 until the AGM in September, candidates can introduce themselves to members:
> *   **Online:** Their bio, photo, and goals are posted on the member portal for everyone to see.
> *   **In person:** At the August Seasonal Update Meeting, there's a special session where candidates can speak and members can ask questions.
>
> This gives everyone a chance to learn about the candidates before voting.

### XI.3 Voting – In Person Only at September AGM

**Source:**
Actual voting for Executive positions shall occur strictly in person at the September Annual General Meeting.

- Proxy voting is permitted as set forth in Article VIII.7.
- Voting shall be by secret ballot. The Election Committee shall oversee the voting process, distribute ballots, and ensure the integrity of the count.
- Only designated Primary or Secondary Members of Voting Units in good standing may cast ballots. Each Voting Unit is entitled to cast its base vote and, if applicable, its bonus vote, up to a maximum of two votes.
- Results shall be announced at the AGM and subsequently published to the membership.

> **Descriptive Note:** Voting for leaders happens **in person only** at the September AGM. This is an important rule—you must be there (or have a proxy) to vote in elections.
>
> Key points:
> *   **Proxy voting:** If you can't attend, you can give your proxy to someone else to vote for you (as allowed in Article VIII.7).
> *   **Secret ballot:** Votes are private—no one knows how you voted.
> *   **Election Committee** runs the vote, hands out ballots, and counts them.
> *   Only designated voters (Primary or Secondary) can vote.
> *   Each Unit casts its base vote plus any bonus vote earned.
> *   Results are announced at the meeting and then shared with all members afterward.

### XI.4 Election Committee

**Source:**
At the Spring General Meeting, the Executive shall appoint an Election Committee consisting of at least three members from different Voting Units who are not standing for election in the current cycle. The Committee shall:

- Manage the nomination process,
- Verify candidate eligibility,
- Oversee the campaign period,
- Administer the in-person voting at the AGM,
- Certify the results, and
- Resolve any disputes or challenges.

> **Descriptive Note:** At the Spring Meeting, the Executive appoints an Election Committee of at least three people. They must be from different families and cannot be running for office themselves. This ensures impartiality.
>
> The Committee does everything election-related:
> *   Handles nominations
> *   Checks that candidates are eligible
> *   Oversees the campaign period
> *   Runs the voting at the AGM
> *   Certifies the results
> *   Settles any disputes or challenges
>
> Having an independent committee ensures fair elections.

### XI.5 Assumption of Office

**Source:**
Newly elected Directors shall shadow the outgoing board during the transition period. Official signing authority and duties shall transfer on **November 15th**.

> **Descriptive Note:** New leaders don't start right away. They shadow the outgoing leaders during a transition period. They officially take over their duties and signing authority on November 15th. This gives time for a smooth handover.

### XI.6 Term of Office

**Source:**
Elected Executive members shall serve a two-year term, beginning on November 15th following their election and ending on November 14th of the second year thereafter.

> **Descriptive Note:** Leaders serve two-year terms. Their term starts on November 15th after they're elected and ends on November 14th two years later. This staggered system provides continuity—not everyone changes at once.

### XI.7 Contested Elections and Recounts

**Source:**
Any challenge to the election process or results must be submitted in writing to the Election Committee within forty-eight (48) hours of the announcement of results. The Committee may conduct a recount if warranted. The Committee's decision on any challenge shall be final.

> **Descriptive Note:** If someone thinks there was a problem with the election (e.g., voting irregularities, eligibility issues), they must tell the Election Committee in writing within 48 hours after results are announced. The Committee can do a recount if they think it's needed. Their decision on any challenge is final—no further appeals.

### XI.8 Vacancies

**Source:**
If a vacancy occurs on the Executive, the remaining Executive members may appoint a qualified member to fill the vacancy for the remainder of the term, as provided in Article XII.4.

> **Descriptive Note:** If a leader steps down mid-term, the remaining leaders can appoint someone qualified to finish that term. This keeps the Executive at full strength without requiring a special election. (See Article XII.4 for more details.)

### XI-A.1 Transition Period

**Source:**
A formal transition period from November 15th to December 31st ensures the outgoing Executive completes administrative responsibilities and onboards the incoming Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** From November 15 to December 31, the old and new leaders work together. This ensures nothing falls through the cracks and the new leaders learn what they need to know.

### XI-A.2 Responsibilities of the Outgoing Executive

The outgoing Executive (serving until November 14th) is responsible for financial closure, project completion, annual reporting, and documentation handover by November 30th, as specified.

> **Descriptive Note:** The outgoing leaders (in charge until Nov 14) have specific duties before they leave:

> *   Finish up projects
> *   Complete annual reports
> *   Hand over all documents by November 30th
>
> This ensures they leave things in good order.

## ARTICLE XII: Executive (Board of Directors)

This article describes the leadership team's structure and powers.

### XII.1 Composition

**Source:**
The Executive shall consist of no fewer than three (3) and no more than seven (7) members, elected by the Voting Units as provided in these By-Laws. This range aligns with the provisions set forth in the Corporation's Articles of Incorporation (Charter).

> **Descriptive Note:** Our leadership team must have between 3 and 7 people, elected by members. This range (set in our Charter) ensures we have enough people to share the work but not so many that decision-making becomes difficult.

### XII.2 Officers

**Source:**
**Required Executive positions** include: Commodore (Chair), Treasurer, and Harbour Master (Port Captain).

**Optional Executive positions** may include: Vice-Commodore, Secretary, Campground Manager, and Director of Entertainment & Fundraising, as determined by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** Some positions we must always have:
> *   **Commodore** (the chair)
> *   **Treasurer** (handles money)
> *   **Harbour Master** (looks after the marina)
>
> Other positions are optional—we can choose to have them if needed:
> *   Vice-Commodore
> *   Secretary
> *   Campground Manager
> *   Director of Entertainment & Fundraising
>
> This gives flexibility—we can add roles as needed for our size and activities.

### XII.3 Election and Term of Office

**Source:**
The term of office for each Executive member shall be two (2) years following their election and ending on the second year election of the new Executive. No Executive member may serve more than four (4) consecutive terms without a one-year break.

> **Descriptive Note:** Leaders serve two-year terms. There's a term limit: no one can serve more than four terms in a row (that's 8 years) without taking at least one year off. This prevents anyone from holding power indefinitely and brings fresh perspectives.

### XII.4 Vacancies

**Source:**
The Executive may appoint a qualified member to fill any vacancy for the remainder of the term.

> **Descriptive Note:** If a leader steps down mid-term, the remaining leaders can appoint someone qualified to finish that term. This keeps the team at full strength without requiring a special election.

### XII.5 Disqualification of Directors

**Source:**
A person shall be disqualified from serving as a Director if they:

(a) are less than eighteen (18) years of age;
(b) have been declared incapable by a court;
(c) are an undischarged bankrupt; or
(d) have been convicted of an offence involving fraud or dishonesty.

Any Director becoming disqualified shall immediately resign or be removed per Article XIV.

> **Descriptive Note:** You cannot be a leader if:
> *   You're under 18.
> *   A court has declared you mentally incapable.
> *   You're an undischarged bankrupt (haven't completed bankruptcy).
> *   You've been convicted of fraud or dishonesty.
>
> If a leader becomes disqualified (e.g., is convicted of fraud), they must resign immediately or be removed. This ensures our leaders are trustworthy and legally eligible.

### XII.6 Executive Meetings & Quorum

**Source:**
Executive meetings are held at least six times per year. A majority of filled Executive positions constitutes a quorum.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Executive must meet at least six times a year. For a meeting to be official, more than half of the current leaders must be present. This prevents a small group from making decisions.

### XII.7 Spending Authority

**Source:**
The Executive may spend on day-to-day operations without a membership vote. For Maintenance Projects, up to \$2,500 per project is permitted without prior approval. For Capital/Improvement Projects, up to \$1,000 per project is allowed without prior approval. Projects exceeding these thresholds, or multiple projects exceeding \$2,000 in a year, require a majority vote of Voting Units.

Borrowing money requires a two-thirds majority vote of Voting Units. The Corporation must maintain transparency by keeping a list of all projects using the official Project Proposal Template and making it available to members upon request.

> **Descriptive Note:** This section defines how much the Executive can spend without asking members:
>
> *   **Day-to-day operations:** No limit—this is normal running costs.
>
> *   **Maintenance Projects** (fixing existing stuff):
>     *   Up to $2,500 per project: Executive can approve without member vote.
>     *   Over $2,500: Needs member vote.
>
> *   **Capital/Improvement Projects** (new stuff):
>     *   Up to $1,000 per project: Executive can approve without member vote.
>     *   Over $1,000: Needs member vote.
>
> *   **Multiple projects:** If total of all projects in a year exceeds $2,000, even if individually under limits, needs member vote.
>
> *   **Borrowing money:** Always needs a two-thirds member vote.
>
> *   **Transparency:** We keep a list of all projects using the official form, available to any member who asks.
>
> This balances efficiency (Executive can handle small things) with member control (members decide on big spending).

### XII.8 Banking and Borrowing

**Source:**
Banking documents must be signed by any two of the Commodore, Treasurer, or Secretary. Borrowing money requires a special resolution (two-thirds majority) of Voting Units.

> **Descriptive Note:** For bank transactions (checks, withdrawals, etc.), two of these three people must sign: Commodore, Treasurer, or Secretary. This provides security—no one person can move money alone.
>
> Borrowing money always needs a two-thirds vote of members. This high threshold ensures broad support for taking on debt.

### XII.9 Committees

**Source:**
The Executive may appoint special committees with specific mandates, which report directly to the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Executive can create special committees for specific tasks (e.g., a committee to plan a fundraising event). These committees report back to the Executive, who oversees their work. This allows focused work on specific issues without expanding the Executive.

### XVIII.3 Directors' and Officers' Insurance

**Source:**
The Corporation shall maintain Directors' and Officers' Liability Insurance.

> **Descriptive Note:** The club will buy insurance to protect its leaders against certain claims related to their roles. This provides an extra layer of protection beyond the indemnification clause.

## ARTICLE XIX-A: Public Accountant

This article details rules about our outside accountant.

### XIX-A.1 Appointment

**Source:**
At each annual meeting of members, the members shall appoint a public accountant to hold office until the close of the next annual meeting. If an appointment is not made, the incumbent public accountant continues in office until a successor is appointed.

> **Descriptive Note:** At every AGM, members vote to appoint an outside accountant. This person serves until the next AGM. If no new appointment is made (e.g., we forget to vote), the current accountant stays on. This ensures we always have an accountant.

### XIX-A.2 Qualifications

**Source:**
To be eligible as public accountant, a person must:
- Be a member in good standing of an institute or association of accountants incorporated under an Act of a province
- Be independent of the Corporation, its affiliates, and its directors and officers
- Meet any other qualifications required by law

> **Descriptive Note:** The accountant must be:
> *   A qualified professional (member of a provincial accounting body).
> *   Independent—not connected to the club, its leaders, or related organizations.
> *   Meet all legal requirements.
>
> This ensures we have a qualified, impartial professional.

### XIX-A.3 Independence

**Source:**
Independence is a question of fact. A person is deemed not independent if they or their business partner:
- Is a business partner, director, officer, or employee of the Corporation or any of its affiliates
- Beneficially owns or controls a material interest in the debt obligations of the Corporation or any of its affiliates
- Has been a receiver, liquidator, or trustee in bankruptcy of the Corporation within two years before their proposed appointment

> **Descriptive Note:** Independence is about real relationships, not just appearances. An accountant is **not** independent if:
> *   They or their business partner is a leader, employee, or business partner of the club.
> *   They have a significant financial interest in the club's debts.
> *   They've been involved in any bankruptcy or receivership of the club in the past two years.
>
> These rules ensure the accountant has no conflicts of interest.

### XIX-A.4 Removal and Resignation

**Source:**
- The members may by ordinary resolution at a special meeting remove a public accountant from office.
- A public accountant who becomes disqualified shall resign immediately after becoming aware of the disqualification.
- A resignation becomes effective when a written resignation is sent to the Corporation or at the time specified in the resignation, whichever is later.

> **Descriptive Note:** Accountants can leave in several ways:
> *   Members can vote to remove them at a special meeting (ordinary resolution—majority vote).
> *   If the accountant becomes disqualified (e.g., loses their professional standing), they must resign immediately.
> *   They can resign by sending written notice—it takes effect when received or at a later date they specify.
>
> This covers all the ways an accountant might stop serving.

### XIX-A.5 Right to Attend Meetings

**Source:**
The public accountant is entitled to attend meetings of members at the expense of the Corporation and be heard on matters relating to their duties.

> **Descriptive Note:** The accountant can attend member meetings at the club's expense and can speak on matters related to their work. This allows them to answer questions or provide information directly to members.

### XIX-A.6 Duty to Attend

**Source:**
If a director or member gives written notice at least ten (10) days before a meeting of members to the public accountant, the public accountant shall attend the meeting at the expense of the Corporation and answer questions relating to their duties.

> **Descriptive Note:** If a leader or member gives the accountant at least 10 days' notice, the accountant must come to the meeting (at club expense) and answer questions about their work. This ensures members can get direct answers from the accountant when needed.

### XIX-A.7 Notice of Errors

**Source:**
If the public accountant or a director or officer becomes aware of an error or misstatement in a financial statement on which the public accountant has reported, and if in their opinion the error or misstatement is material, they shall inform each director accordingly. The directors shall then prepare and issue revised financial statements or otherwise inform the members.

> **Descriptive Note:** If the accountant or any leader discovers a significant error in financial statements that the accountant had already reported on, they must tell all the leaders. The leaders must then correct the statements and inform members. This ensures errors are corrected promptly.

- **Operational Records:** Rules and regulations, emergency plans, inspection reports

> **Descriptive Note:** Here's what we keep:
> *   **Governance:** Charter, Bylaws, meeting minutes, policies, Code of Conduct
> *   **Financial:** Statements, budgets, tax returns, bank records
> *   **Legal:** Insurance, contracts, permits, legal mail
> *   **Membership:** Lists, voter info, volunteer records
> *   **Property:** Site plans, equipment lists, leases
> *   **Operations:** Rules, emergency plans, inspection reports

> This comprehensive list ensures we keep everything important.

### XXIII.3 Repository Management

**Source:**
- **Responsibility:** The Secretary is responsible for maintaining the document repository.
- **Organization:** Documents must be organized in a logical, searchable manner.
- **Retention Schedule:** The Corporation shall maintain a document retention schedule.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Secretary is in charge of keeping the document archive. Everything must be organized so it's easy to find. **Source:**
- **Executive Access:** All Executive members have full access to the document repository.
- **Member Access:** Voting Units have access to governance documents, minutes, financial statements, and policies.
- **Confidentiality:** Sensitive documents are restricted to Executive access only.
- **Security:** The repository shall be secured against unauthorized access.

> **Descriptive Note:** Different people can see different things:
> *   **All leaders:** Can see everything.
> *   **Voting members:** Can see governance documents, minutes, financial statements, and policies.
> *   **Sensitive stuff:** Only leaders can see things like contracts, personnel records, legal correspondence.
>
> The archive must be secure—not accessible to just anyone.

### XXIII.5 Digital Repository

**Source:**
- **Primary System:** The Corporation shall maintain a secure digital repository accessible to authorized users.
- **Backup:** Regular backups must be maintained with off-site or cloud storage.
- **Format:** Documents should be maintained in searchable formats whenever possible.
- **Index:** A master index of all documents shall be maintained and updated quarterly.

> **Descriptive Note:** Our main archive will be digital (online), accessible to those who need it. We back it up regularly, off-site or in the cloud, so we don't lose everything if something happens. Documents should be in formats you can search (like PDF with text, not just scanned images). We keep a master list of all documents, updated every three months.

### XXIII.6 Physical Documents

**Source:**
- **Secure Storage:** Original signed documents must be stored securely in a climate-controlled environment.
- **Digital Copies:** Physical documents should be digitized and included in the digital repository.
- **Retention:** Physical documents must be retained according to the established retention schedule.

> **Descriptive Note:** Original signed papers (like contracts) must be kept safe in a climate-controlled place (no damp basements!). We should scan them and add them to the digital archive. We keep physical copies according to our retention schedule—some we keep forever, some we can destroy after a certain time.

### XXIII.7 Document Review and Purge

**Source:**
- **Annual Review:** The Executive shall conduct an annual review of the repository.
- **Secure Disposal:** Documents scheduled for disposal must be securely destroyed.
- **Archiving:** Historical documents of enduring value shall be properly archived.

> **Descriptive Note:** Leaders will review the archive once a year. Documents we don't need anymore must be destroyed securely (shredded, not just thrown out). Important historical documents will be kept in a proper archive (maybe physically, maybe digitally). This keeps the archive current and manageable.

### XXIII.8 Access to Records

**Source:**
Any member in good standing may inspect the Corporation's records, including minutes, financial statements, and membership registers (excluding personal contact details), at the head office during regular business hours upon providing reasonable notice to the Secretary. Copies may be provided at the member's expense, subject to any restrictions under privacy law.

> **Descriptive Note:** Any member in good standing can look at club records—like minutes, financial statements, and membership lists—at our office during business hours, as long as they give the Secretary reasonable notice. They can get copies at their own expense, subject to privacy laws (so no getting copies of other members' contact info). This ensures transparency while protecting privacy.

## ARTICLE XXIV: Dissolution

This article covers what happens if we ever close down.

### XXIV.1 Application of Law

**Source:**
The dissolution of the Corporation shall be governed by Part 14 of the Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act.

> **Descriptive Note:** If we ever close down, we follow the rules in the federal Not-for-profit Corporations Act. This ensures we do it legally and properly.

### XXIV.2 Distribution of Remaining Property

**Source:**
In the event of the dissolution or winding up of the Corporation, after the payment of all debts and liabilities, the remaining property and assets of the Corporation shall be distributed in the following order:

Payment of Debts: All known debts, liabilities, and reasonable expenses of dissolution shall be paid in full.
Distribution to Members: The remaining assets shall be liquidated and the proceeds distributed equally among the members of the Corporation who are in good standing at the time of dissolution, in accordance with a formula established by the Executive.
Residual to Non-Profit: Any assets or funds that cannot be distributed to members (for example, unclaimed distributions) shall be transferred to one or more qualified non-profit or charitable organizations in Canada with purposes similar to those of the Corporation, as determined by the Executive.

This provision is unalterable and reflects the distribution order set forth in the Corporation's Articles of Incorporation (Charter).

> **Descriptive Note:** This section tells us what happens if we ever close down the organization (called "dissolution" or "winding up"). It follows a three-step order that **cannot be changed** (unalterable):
>
> 1.  **Pay all debts:** First, we use our assets to pay off anyone we owe money to—creditors, suppliers, loans, and the reasonable costs of shutting down. This ensures we leave no unpaid obligations.
>
> 2.  **Distribute to members:** After all debts are paid, any remaining assets are converted to cash (liquidated) and distributed among members who are in good standing at the time of dissolution. The distribution is equal among eligible members, following a formula that the Executive will determine. This might mean equal shares per member, or it could be based on length of membership or other fair criteria. This step recognizes that the members have built the organization's value over time.
>
> 3.  **Residual to non-profits:** If any assets can't be distributed to members (for example, if a member has died and no one claims their share, or a member can't be located), those remaining funds go to other Canadian non-profit or charitable organizations with purposes similar to ours—like other marinas, community boating clubs, or campground associations. The Executive decides which organizations receive these residual assets.
>
> This three-step process ensures a fair and orderly wind-down: first satisfy obligations, then return value to members, and finally ensure that any unclaimed assets stay in the non-profit community to continue serving purposes similar to ours.

### XXIV.3 Qualified Donees

**Source:**
If the Corporation is a registered charity or a soliciting corporation, any property remaining on liquidation after the discharge of any liabilities shall be distributed to one or more qualified donees within the meaning of subsection 248(1) of the Income Tax Act.

> **Descriptive Note:** If we're a registered charity, any leftover assets must go to other registered charities as defined by tax laws. This ensures charitable assets stay in the charitable sector.

### XXIV.4 Procedure

**Source:**
The dissolution shall be carried out in accordance with the procedures set out in the Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act, including the sending of statements of intent to dissolve, articles of dissolution, and obtaining certificates of dissolution from the Director.

> **Descriptive Note:** We follow all the legal steps to close down properly, including filing the right paperwork with the government and getting official approval. No shortcuts.

## ARTICLE XXV: By-Law Amendments

**Source:**
- Any proposal to amend, repeal, or create a new By-Law must be submitted to the Executive in writing at least thirty (30) days prior to any General Membership Meeting.
- Notice of all proposed amendments shall be distributed to Voting Units at least fifteen (15) days prior to the meeting.
- Amending or repealing any By-Law requires approval by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of votes of Voting Units present and eligible to vote, provided quorum is met.
- If an amendment affects the rights of a specific class or group of members in a manner different from other members, the members of that class or group are entitled to vote separately as a class or group, and the amendment must be approved by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of that class or group.
- A copy of any by-law, amendment, or repeal shall be sent to the Director under the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act* within the prescribed period after adoption.

> **Descriptive Note:** Here's how we change these rules:
>
> 1.  **Proposal:** Any change must be given to the Executive in writing at least 30 days before a member meeting. This gives time for review.
>
> 2.  **Notice to members:** All members must be told about proposed changes at least 15 days before the meeting.
>
> 3.  **Vote:** Changing a bylaw needs a **two-thirds majority** of the Voting Units present at the meeting (provided enough members are there—quorum). This higher threshold ensures broad support for rule changes.
>
> 4.  **Class votes:** If a change only affects a specific group of members (like only campers), that group gets to vote separately on it, and it needs two-thirds approval from that group too. This protects minority interests.
>
> 5.  **Government filing:** After a change is approved, the Secretary must send a copy to the government within the required time.
>
> This process ensures bylaw changes are carefully considered, well-communicated, and have strong support.

## ARTICLE XXVII: Definitions

**Source:**
For the purposes of these Bylaws, the following definitions apply:

- **"Act"** means the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act*, S.C. 2009, c. 23, as amended from time to time.
- **"Director"** means the Director appointed under section 281 of the Act.
- **"Public Accountant"** means the public accountant appointed for the Corporation under Article XIX-A.
- **"Special Resolution"** means a resolution passed by a majority of not less than two-thirds of the votes cast on that resolution.
- **"Ordinary Resolution"** means a resolution passed by a majority of the votes cast on that resolution.
- **"Voting Unit"** means a single family unit using the same premises, entitled to one base vote and potentially one bonus vote, up to a maximum of two votes total.

> **Descriptive Note:** Here's what some key terms mean:
>
> *   **"Act":** The federal law that governs not-for-profit corporations in Canada. It's the legal framework we operate under.
>
> *   **"Director":** The government official appointed to oversee not-for-profit corporations. We file certain documents with this person.
>
> *   **"Public Accountant":** The outside accountant we appoint to review our finances. They provide independent oversight.
>
> *   **"Special Resolution":** A vote that needs at least two-thirds (67%) approval. Used for important decisions like bylaw changes.
>
> *   **"Ordinary Resolution":** A vote that needs more than half (50%+) approval. Used for most regular decisions.
>
> *   **"Voting Unit":** A family renting one slip or campsite. They get one base vote and can earn one bonus vote, for a maximum of two votes total.
>
> These definitions ensure we all mean the same thing when we use these terms.
