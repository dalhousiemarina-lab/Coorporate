## 1. Purpose

This document provides a comprehensive, consolidated gap analysis of the Dalhousie Regional Marina Club's (DRMC) governance documentation framework. It combines all previously identified gaps with newly discovered needs, prioritises them, and provides a complete roadmap for achieving a fully resilient governance system.

The analysis is based on:

- Requirements of the DRMC Bylaws
- Legal and regulatory obligations (PIPEDA, *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act*, etc.)
- Operational needs identified by Squads and the Executive
- Member feedback and common questions
- Best practices for non-profit marinas and campgrounds
- Consultation with insurance providers and legal advisors
- Review of member feedback and operational pain points from the 2025 season
- Comparison with similar organisations' governance frameworks

## 2. Methodology

## 5. Detailed Gap Descriptions

### 5.1 High Priority Gaps (Must Address Immediately)

**Document Retention Policy (#1)**
- **Why Needed:** Bylaws Article XXIII explicitly require a policy defining how long different types of records must be kept and how they are securely disposed of. Without it, the club risks legal non-compliance, increased liability, and inefficient record-keeping.
- **Current Status:** The file `DRMC Document Retention Policy.md` exists but contains the text of the Reserves & Investment Policy (a duplicate). A proper policy has not been drafted.
- **Recommended Content:** Retention schedules for incorporation documents (permanent), financial records (7 years), membership applications (duration + 1 year), meeting minutes (permanent), etc. Include protocols for digital vs. physical storage and secure destruction.

**Conflict of Interest Policy (#2)**
- **Why Needed:** While Bylaws Article XVII and the Director Agreement address conflicts for directors, there is no policy applicable to all members and volunteers. This is especially important for procurement decisions, volunteer screening, and maintaining trust.
- **Current Status:** Not started.
- **Recommended Content:** Definitions, disclosure requirements, recusal procedures, consequences for non-disclosure, and applicability to all members and volunteers.

**Boat Launch & Fuel Dock Operating Procedure (#14)**
- **Why Needed:** The Code of Conduct (Section 3.04) prohibits fueling at docks and requires fueling at the designated ramp area. However, there is no detailed procedure for safe fueling operations, traffic management during peak times, or spill response specific to the launch area. This creates safety and environmental risks.
- **Recommended Content:** Step-by-step fueling protocol, traffic flow and queuing guidelines, spill kit locations and deployment instructions, hours of operation, role of Harbour Master or volunteers in monitoring.

**Winterization & Spring Launch Procedure (#15)**
- **Why Needed:** The Dock Squad currently coordinates seasonal dock installation and removal, but there is no formal documented procedure. This leads to inconsistencies, safety risks, and reliance on institutional memory.
- **Recommended Content:** Timeline and coordination plan, safety checklist and required equipment, volunteer sign-up and task assignments, inspection protocols before and after installation, communication plan for members.

**Child Protection / Youth Policy (#16)**
- **Why Needed:** If the club offers any youth programs (sailing lessons, junior activities, etc.), a formal child protection policy is essential for legal compliance and insurance requirements. Even without formal programs, children are present on site, and clear supervision guidelines are needed.
- **Recommended Content:** Definition of youth/minors, supervision requirements, screening requirements for volunteers working with children, reporting procedures for suspected abuse or neglect, prohibited conduct.

### 5.2 Medium Priority Gaps

**Draft Documents Awaiting Approval (#3–10)**
- The eight documents listed as drafted (items 3–10) are complete in draft form but have not yet been formally approved by the Executive. They represent a significant body of operational guidance that, once ratified, will greatly enhance consistency and transparency.

**Key Control & Master Key Policy (#17)**
- **Why Needed:** The Access Control Policy draft addresses general key/fob issuance but may not cover special controls for master keys, emergency access keys, or high-security areas (electrical rooms, pump stations, fuel shutoffs). These require stricter oversight.
- **Recommended Action:** Ensure the Access Control Policy includes a specific section on master key controls, including who holds them, audit requirements, and emergency access protocols.

**Guest Pass & Visitor Policy (#18)**
- **Why Needed:** Member responsibilities for guests are mentioned in the Code of Conduct and Membership Agreement, but there is no detailed policy on guest duration limits, registration requirements, or consequences for guest misconduct.
- **Recommended Content:** Definition of guest vs. member, maximum duration of guest stays, registration process (if any), member liability for guest conduct, rules for unaccompanied guests.

**Donation & Sponsorship Acceptance Policy (#19)**
- **Why Needed:** The SaveTheMarina Squad is actively seeking grants and sponsorships. A clear policy ensures that donations are accepted ethically, that donor expectations are managed, and that conflicts of interest are avoided.
- **Recommended Content:** Criteria for accepting/rejecting donations, ethical considerations (e.g., tobacco, alcohol, political), donor recognition guidelines, process for sponsorship agreements, conflict of interest disclosure.

**Whistleblower Policy (#20)**
- **Why Needed:** Best practice for non-profits, especially those handling member funds. A whistleblower policy protects individuals who report wrongdoing (financial misconduct, safety violations, harassment) from retaliation.
- **Recommended Content:** Definition of reportable concerns, confidential reporting mechanism, investigation process, non-retaliation pledge, protection of confidentiality.

**Records Access & Member Inspection Procedure (#21)**
- **Why Needed:** Bylaws Article XXIII.8 grants members the right to inspect certain records. A detailed procedure would clarify how to request access, what fees (if any) apply, and timelines for response.
- **Recommended Content:** Request form and submission process, designated records custodian, fees for copies, timeline for response, grounds for denial (privacy, legal privilege).

**IT & Cybersecurity Policy (#22)**
- **Why Needed:** The club increasingly relies on digital tools (member database, email, financial software, cloud storage). A cybersecurity policy protects against data breaches, phishing, and unauthorised access.
- **Recommended Content:** Password requirements, access controls for club systems, email and internet use guidelines, data backup requirements, breach reporting procedure.

**Noise Control & Generator Policy (#23)**
- **Why Needed:** The Code of Conduct has quiet hours (11 PM–7 AM) but does not specifically address generator use, which can be a significant noise source in the campground.
- **Recommended Content:** Permitted generator types, hours of operation, placement requirements, noise level limits.

**Social Media & Online Conduct Policy (#24)**
- **Why Needed:** Guidelines for member conduct on club social media pages and member forums are needed to maintain a positive online community.
- **Recommended Action:** Ensure Member Communication Policy includes social media guidelines and moderation rules.

## 10. Conclusion

This comprehensive gap analysis identifies **30 documents** needed for a fully resilient governance framework:

- **5 high-priority gaps** requiring immediate attention
- **16 medium-priority gaps** (including 8 drafts awaiting approval)
- **9 low-priority items** for future consideration

With the approval of the eight pending drafts and the creation of the five high-priority missing policies, the club will have addressed its most critical governance needs. The remaining medium and low-priority items can be developed systematically over the next 12–18 months.

The DRMC governance framework is evolving rapidly. With systematic attention to these remaining gaps, the club will achieve best-practice governance and be well-positioned for sustainable, transparent, and effective operations.
