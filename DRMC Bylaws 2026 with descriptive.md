# BYLAWS OF DALHOUSIE REGIONAL MARINA LTD.

**Effective Date:** February 28, 2026
**Version:** 3.3 (Fully Compliant with Enhanced Descriptions)

---

## Preamble

These Bylaws constitute the primary governing rules for the Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd. ("the Corporation"). They are designed to ensure the fair, transparent, and efficient operation of the marina and campground for the benefit of all members. This document explains not only the rules but also the purpose behind them, fostering a shared understanding of our community's operating principles.

---

## ARTICLE I: Name

The official name of the corporation shall be "Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd" (referred to as "the Corporation" throughout these bylaws).

> **Descriptive Note:** This article establishes the legal identity of our organization. This is the name under which we are incorporated, hold property, sign contracts, and are known to government agencies and the public. Using the full, correct name in all official documents ensures legal consistency and protects the Corporation's separate legal identity from its members.

---

## ARTICLE II: Objects (Purpose)

The Corporation is established with the primary objective of operating and maintaining a marina and campground for the enjoyment of its members, their families, and their guests. Its aims include providing boaters with secure facilities for boats and equipment, and ensuring campers have access to well-maintained sites and amenities. The Corporation fosters community-building activities, upholds compliance with Federal, Provincial, and Municipal regulations, and encourages a strong community spirit through volunteer squads, social events, and collaborative decision-making. Additionally, the Corporation is responsible for seeking and managing funds for facility maintenance and improvement, and for securing all necessary permits, leases, and licenses required for operations.

> **Descriptive Note:** This "Objects" clause (sometimes called a Purpose clause) defines the very reason our Corporation exists under the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act*. It acts as a guiding star for all decisions made by the Executive and the membership. Any major activity, expenditure, or policy should ultimately serve one or more of these stated aims, such as maintaining facilities, building community, or ensuring legal compliance. This protects the Corporation and ensures we stay focused on our core mission, and it is also a reference point for regulators and potential funders.

---

## ARTICLE III: Head Office

The head office of the Corporation shall be located at **111, Rock Island Rd, Dalhousie, Nouveau Brunswick E8C 2K9**. If necessary, the Executive may designate a different location for the head office.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is the official registered address of the Corporation, required by law for receiving legal documents (like service of process) and official correspondence from government bodies (like the Canada Revenue Agency). It is our public anchor point. While day-to-day operations happen throughout the property, this is our formal "home base" in the eyes of the law. The flexibility to change the address allows the Corporation to adapt to future circumstances, such as a move to a new administrative building.

## ARTICLE IV: Membership & Voting Rights

> **Article IV Purpose:** This foundational article establishes the principle that voting power is tied to a "Voting Unit" (a family sharing a premises) rather than to individuals. This structure prevents any single family from having disproportionate influence. It also actively encourages participation by offering a "bonus vote" for verified volunteer work, ensuring that those who contribute to our community's upkeep have an enhanced voice in its direction.

### IV.1 The Voting Unit
Membership governance is conducted through Voting Units, defined as a single family unit using the same premises (slip or campsite). Each Voting Unit is entitled to a maximum of **two (2) votes** in total, allocated as follows:
- **One (1) base vote** per Voting Unit based on membership class.
- **One (1) additional bonus vote** if the Unit meets the volunteer requirements in Section IV.2.
No Voting Unit shall cast more than two votes on any single motion.

> **Descriptive Note:** This structure balances fairness with encouragement for involvement. Defining the family as the voting unit prevents a large family from having more sway than a couple or single person. The two-vote maximum, with the second being earned through service, rewards community contribution while preventing any single entity from dominating a vote. It makes the fundamental statement that in our community, voice is tied to both presence and participation.

### IV.2 Bonus Vote for Volunteers
To promote active participation, a Voting Unit may earn one (1) additional vote (a "bonus vote") if at least one adult member of the Unit completes 20 or more hours of verified volunteer work in the preceding 12-month period. Volunteer hours are tracked in a public Volunteer Registry. Volunteer hours registered in the Volunteer Registry expire 12 months after they are recorded.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is the core mechanism to incentivize hands-on involvement, which is vital for a volunteer-run organization like ours. The 20-hour threshold is set to a level that represents a significant, meaningful contribution over a year (e.g., helping with a few work bees or serving on a squad). The public registry ensures transparency and allows members to track their progress and see others' contributions. The 12-month expiration ensures that the bonus vote reflects recent and ongoing engagement, not a single burst of activity from years ago, keeping the incentive current.

### IV.3 Voter Eligibility
Only the designated Primary or Secondary Member of a Voting Unit in good standing may exercise the Unit's vote(s). If the Unit is entitled to a bonus vote, it is exercised by the same designated voter.

> **Descriptive Note:** This clarifies exactly who from each Unit can cast the vote(s), preventing confusion or disputes at meetings. "In good standing" is a key legal term meaning the Unit's membership is active, all fees and dues are paid up-to-date, and they are not under any current suspension.

### IV.4 Voting Unit Access Types
Membership access is classified into the following categories, which do not affect voting rights:
- Berth Holder: Holds a seasonal boat slip.
- Seasonal Camper: Holds a seasonal campsite.
- Dual Member: Holds both a boat slip and a campsite.
- Associate Member: Social member with no voting rights and no boat slip or campsite.
- Overnight Guest: Short-term visitor with no voting rights.

> **Descriptive Note:** These categories define the *services* and *facility access* a member receives. Importantly, the category of service (e.g., Berth Holder vs. Camper) does not change a Unit's *voting* rights; all seasonal members have one base vote. This ensures that boaters and campers have an equal voice in governance, preventing one group from dominating the other. Associate and Guest members are non-voting because they do not hold a seasonal premises and their commitment is of a different nature.

### IV.5 Unit Classes and Voting Rights
The Corporation shall have **Class A Voting Unit**, divided into the following subcategories. Each subcategory is entitled to **one (1) base vote**:
1.  **Berth Holder** ...
2.  **Seasonal Camper** ...
3.  **Dual Member** ...
4.  **Certified Volunteer** ...
**Bonus Vote:** Any Voting Unit described in subcategories 1 through 4 above may earn **one (1) additional bonus vote** upon completion of 20 or more verified volunteer hours as set forth in Section IV.2.

> **Descriptive Note:** This section formally lists all types of Voting Units eligible for a base vote. The "Certified Volunteer" subcategory exists to formally recognize and enfranchise individuals who contribute significantly but may not hold a slip or campsite (e.g., a dedicated community member who runs social events or serves as the webmaster). It reinforces that contribution, not just facility use, is a valued path to having a say in governance, aligning with our objects of fostering community spirit.

### IV.6 Eligibility and Verification
The Corporation establishes and maintains procedures for verifying eligibility within each subcategory and confirming completion of required volunteer hours for the bonus vote.

> **Descriptive Note:** This empowers the Executive to create practical, fair, and documented processes (like checking the Volunteer Registry, verifying boat ownership with a registration, or confirming campsite occupancy) to ensure that votes are cast only by those entitled to them, maintaining the integrity of all elections and votes.

### IV.7 Designating Voters
Each Voting Unit must notify the Secretary in writing of its designated Primary Voter. A Secondary Voter may also be named, who may vote only in the absence of the Primary Voter. Only the designated Primary or Secondary Voter may cast the Unit's vote(s), and only one individual may vote on behalf of the Unit at any given meeting or vote.

> **Descriptive Note:** This provides clarity and flexibility for families. A family can decide internally who their representative will be. Naming a secondary voter ensures the Unit's voice can still be heard if the primary voter is unavailable (e.g., due to work or illness), without needing to amend the official designation for every single meeting. This streamlines the voting process.

### IV.8 Voting Procedure
For any vote, the total number of votes cast is the sum of all base votes and bonus votes from participating Voting Units. No Unit may cast more than two votes total.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a simple, transparent mathematical statement of how votes are counted. It reinforces the two-vote maximum and makes it clear that a Unit's bonus vote is not separate but adds to the total count, giving them a proportional increase in influence.

### IV.9 Volunteer Hour Tracking & Verification
The Corporation maintains a public Volunteer Registry to monitor each Unit's progress toward the 20-hour goal. Volunteered and verified volunteer hours must be documented either by the Squad Coordinator or an Executive member. Any disputes regarding volunteer hours are resolved by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** Transparency and accountability are paramount for the bonus vote system to be perceived as fair. A public registry allows all members to see progress, fostering a healthy sense of community contribution. Designating specific verifiers (Squad Coordinators, Executive) prevents fraudulent self-reporting. Giving the Executive clear authority to resolve disputes provides a definitive and efficient path for addressing any disagreements about hours logged, preventing minor issues from escalating.

### IV.10 Resignation of Membership
A member may resign from the Corporation at any time by delivering a written notice of resignation to the Secretary. The resignation shall be effective upon receipt by the Secretary or on a later date specified in the notice. Upon resignation, the member forfeits all rights and privileges of membership, and no refund of dues or fees shall be provided unless otherwise determined by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** This formalizes the process for a member to leave the Corporation. The "no refund" clause is a standard provision for membership organizations, as the Club's annual operating budget and commitments (e.g., insurance, lease payments) are calculated based on expected membership fees. Granting a refund would create a financial shortfall. It gives the Executive limited discretion to potentially grant a refund only in the most extraordinary and compassionate circumstances, ensuring fiscal responsibility.

### IV.11 Code of Conduct Agreement
All members must agree to abide by the current Code of Conduct as a condition of membership and to access Corporation facilities.

> **Descriptive Note:** This critical clause legally links these Bylaws to the separate "Code of Conduct & Facility Regulations" document. By signing their membership agreement or using the facilities, a member is automatically agreeing to follow the Code of Conduct, making it an enforceable part of their membership contract. This allows the Corporation to address behavioral issues through the suspension process in Article V.

## ARTICLE V: Suspension of Membership

> **Article V Purpose:** This article establishes a fair, progressive, and transparent process for addressing serious breaches of the rules. It balances the need to protect the Corporation, its property, and the enjoyment of its members with the rights of the individual member to be heard and to appeal decisions. The process moves from potential grounds to a hearing and finally to a membership vote for expulsion.

### V.1 Termination of Membership
Membership may be terminated for repeated or severe violations following the suspension process outlined in this Article. The affected member shall have the right to appeal the termination decision to the membership at the next General Meeting, where a majority vote of Voting Units present shall decide the appeal. Termination shall be effective immediately upon the Executive's decision, subject to appeal.

> **Descriptive Note:** This section sets the ultimate consequence for serious or repeated infractions. Critically, it embeds a right of appeal to the general membership, ensuring that the final decision on expulsion rests with the community, not just the Executive. The termination is immediate for safety and order but is explicitly "subject to appeal," meaning the member's status is pending the outcome of the vote, which is a crucial protection of their rights.

### V.2 Grounds for Suspension
The Executive may suspend a membership for the following reasons:
- Failure to pay fees after due notice
- Violation of Corporation rules or bylaws, including the Code of Conduct
- Conduct deemed detrimental to the Corporation, its members, or its operations

> **Descriptive Note:** This defines the "why" – the specific, actionable reasons that can lead to suspension. It covers financial responsibility, rule-breaking, and the broader concept of "detrimental conduct," which is necessary to address unforeseen behaviors that harm the community even if they don't violate a specific written rule. The phrase "deemed detrimental" gives the Executive the authority to interpret, but that interpretation is always subject to challenge through the subsequent appeal process.

### V.3 Process and Appeal
Prior to any expulsion, the member shall receive written notice of the allegations and be given an opportunity to be heard by the active members at a meeting.

> **Descriptive Note:** This enshrines the principle of "natural justice" or "procedural fairness." A member cannot be expelled without knowing exactly what they are accused of (the allegations) and having a chance to tell their side of the story (be heard) in front of the community that will decide their fate. This prevents arbitrary or secret decisions by the Executive.

### V.4 Expulsion Decision
Following the hearing, a petition for expulsion may be circulated among the active members. The member shall be expelled if the petition receives the signatures of at least 51% of the active members of the Corporation.

> **Descriptive Note:** This outlines the specific mechanism for the community vote. A petition is used as a formal way to gauge the membership's will. The 51% threshold of *all active members* (not just those at a meeting) is a very high bar, ensuring that expulsion requires a clear and broad consensus from the entire community, not just a simple majority of those who show up to one meeting. This protects the member from a potentially unrepresentative vote.

### V.5 Notification
The Executive shall promptly notify the suspended member in writing.

> **Descriptive Note:** This ensures the member is formally and officially informed of the outcome, closing the loop on the process and providing clarity on their status. Written notification is important for record-keeping.

### V.6 Fee Forfeiture
Suspended members forfeit their membership fee and are not entitled to a refund.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a standard consequence, reinforcing that membership fees are not refundable upon termination for cause, as the costs associated with that member's potential disruption or rule-breaking are not recoverable.

### V.7 Loss of Membership for Non-Use
A membership may be terminated if the member does not utilize their assigned slip or campsite for a full season, as follows:

> **Descriptive Note:** This section addresses a different kind of "cause" – not misconduct, but underutilization of a scarce resource. The marina and campground have limited slips and sites. This policy ensures that these valuable assets are actively used by engaged members, and that those on waiting lists get a fair opportunity.

**V.7.1 Definition of Non-Use**
- Non-use means the member's assigned slip or campsite remains vacant and unoccupied for the entire operational season (May 1 to October 31)
- Occasional, sporadic, or de minimis use (e.g., less than 7 total days occupied) may be deemed non-use at the Executive's discretion

> **Descriptive Note:** This provides a clear, objective baseline (the entire season) but also gives the Executive discretion to address edge cases. "De minimis use" (a Latin term meaning "about minimal things") allows the Executive to judge if a member used the site so little that it effectively constitutes non-use, preventing someone from technically occupying it for one day just to keep the spot. This balances clarity with fairness.

**V.7.2 Notification Requirement**
- Members anticipating non-use for an upcoming season must notify the Secretary in writing by April 1st
- Members who fail to notify and do not use their space forfeit their membership rights as outlined below

> **Descriptive Note:** This creates a proactive communication requirement. If a member knows they won't be using their site, telling the Executive by April 1st allows the Corporation to plan, perhaps by offering a temporary sublet (if permitted) or by freeing up the spot for someone on the waitlist sooner. Failure to notify means the member loses the benefit of the doubt.

**V.7.3 Consequences of Non-Use**
- After one season of non-use, the member's assigned slip or campsite shall be forfeited and offered to the waiting list
- The member's Voting Unit status shall be converted to Associate (Non-Voting) Member status for the following season
- After two consecutive seasons of non-use, membership shall be terminated entirely, and the member must reapply as a new member if they wish to return

> **Descriptive Note:** This establishes a graduated, fair response. The first step is losing the specific spot (which is the Corporation's main asset to manage), but the person retains a non-voting affiliation with the community. Only after two years of complete absence is membership fully terminated. This gives a member a year to re-engage if their circumstances change.

**V.7.4 Exceptions**
The Executive may grant exceptions to this policy for:
- Medical circumstances (with supporting documentation)
- Military service or extended work assignments abroad
- Major repairs to vessel or RV (with proof and timeline)
- Other exceptional circumstances as determined by the Executive
Requests for exceptions must be submitted in writing prior to the season.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a crucial compassionate clause, recognizing that life events can temporarily prevent use. By requiring documentation and a request *before* the season, it allows the Executive to plan and ensures the exception is legitimate, preventing abuse of the policy.

**V.7.5 Fee Forfeiture**
Members whose membership is terminated under this section forfeit all fees paid and are not entitled to any refund.

> **Descriptive Note:** Consistent with other termination clauses, fees are not refunded, as the Corporation's expenses for the season were incurred based on the membership count.

## ARTICLE V-A: Code of Conduct

> **Article V-A Purpose:** This article formally recognizes that the detailed rules of behavior are maintained in a separate, more easily updated document called the "Code of Conduct & Facility Regulations." This separation allows for detailed safety and operational rules to be updated without needing a full bylaw amendment, while still giving them the full force of the Corporation's governance.

### V-A.1 Adoption by Reference
The Corporation maintains a separate "Code of Conduct" document, which is incorporated by reference into these bylaws. Compliance with the Code of Conduct is mandatory for all members.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is the legal "bridge" that makes the separate Code of Conduct an enforceable part of the membership agreement. By referencing it here, any violation of the Code becomes a violation of the Bylaws, triggering the suspension and appeal processes in Article V.

### V-A.2 Availability and Distribution
The current Code of Conduct shall be available to all members via the Corporation's document repository, postings in common areas, and distribution to new members upon registration. Members are responsible for familiarizing themselves with the current Code of Conduct.

> **Descriptive Note:** This places a dual responsibility on the Corporation (to make the Code easily accessible) and on the member (to know and follow it). Posting it in common areas and online ensures no one can claim they were unaware of the rules. "Ignorance of the law is no excuse" applies here.

### V-A.3 Amendment Process
The Code of Conduct may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the Executive. Substantive changes must be circulated to members for comment at least 30 days prior to adoption. Minor administrative or safety-related updates may be adopted without prior member comment.

> **Descriptive Note:** Because the Code of Conduct contains detailed, often safety-critical rules (e.g., "no swimming off the dock," "quiet hours"), it needs to be updated more nimbly than the Bylaws. This section grants the Executive that power but with important checks. "Substantive changes" (like a new pet policy) require a member comment period, allowing for community input. Minor changes (like clarifying a parking line) can be made immediately for safety or efficiency.

### V-A.4 Enforcement
Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in suspension of membership under Article V.

> **Descriptive Note:** This explicitly links the two articles, confirming that the serious consequences of Article V (suspension, expulsion) can be triggered by breaches of the Code of Conduct.

## ARTICLE V-B: Member Responsibilities

> **Article V-B Purpose:** This article outlines the positive duties and expectations of all members. While the Code of Conduct focuses on prohibited behavior, this section focuses on the proactive responsibilities that make our community function smoothly and safely. It sets the standard for being a good community member.

### V-B.1 Duty to Uphold
Every Voting Unit and member has a responsibility to support the Corporation as defined in Article II, and to act in a manner that is not detrimental to the Corporation, its property, or its members.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a broad, overarching statement of good citizenship. It asks members to be positive contributors and to actively avoid harm to the community or its assets. It reinforces that membership is a two-way street of rights and responsibilities.

### V-B.2 Compliance with Documents
All members shall comply with the Corporation's Articles of Incorporation, these Bylaws, the Code of Conduct, and all duly adopted rules and regulations.

> **Descriptive Note:** This clarifies the entire legal framework that governs a member's conduct. It's not just the Bylaws; it's the founding Charter, the specific Code, and any other rules (like speed limits on the access road) properly adopted by the Executive or membership.

### V-B.3 Financial Responsibility
All members are responsible for the timely payment of all membership fees and assessments as set forth in Article VI.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a straightforward statement of the financial bedrock of membership. The Corporation cannot operate without funds, and timely payment is an essential responsibility.

### V-B.4 Site Improvement
All site improvements must be approved by the maintenance and executive. Units delimited must be respected.

> **Descriptive Note:** This protects the Corporation's property and the equitable use of space. It ensures that any modifications (like a new deck at a campsite or a shed on a dock) are safe, compliant with regulations, and don't encroach on common areas or other members' sites. The phrase "Units delimited must be respected" reinforces the importance of property boundaries.

### V-B.5 Care of Facilities
Members shall exercise reasonable care in the use of Corporation facilities and property, and shall be responsible for any damages caused by their negligence or willful misconduct.

> **Descriptive Note:** This establishes a standard of care ("reasonable") and personal accountability. If a member accidentally damages a dock with their boat due to carelessness (negligence) or intentionally damages property (willful misconduct), they are financially responsible for the repairs, not the entire membership.

## ARTICLE VI: Membership Fees and Pricing

> **Article VI Purpose:** This article establishes a transparent, predictable, and fair system for funding the Corporation's operations. It defines how fees are calculated, how increases are managed, and the payment schedule. Its goal is to ensure financial stability while protecting members from arbitrary or excessive fee hikes.

### VI.1 Annual Fee Setting Process
Fees are recommended by the Executive and approved by the general membership at the Annual General Meeting. The Executive must present a detailed fee schedule for the upcoming year, including justifications for any proposed increases.

Fee calculations shall be based on the following methodologies:
- **Berth Holders:** Annual fee = vessel length overall (LOA) in feet × rate per foot established annually by the membership.
- **Seasonal Campers:** Annual fee = fixed rate per campsite established annually by the membership.
- **Dual Members:** Annual fee = (boat LOA × rate per foot) + fixed campsite rate.
- **Associate Members:** Annual fee = fixed social membership rate established annually by the membership.

> **Descriptive Note:** This section ensures democratic control over the Corporation's primary revenue stream. The Executive proposes a budget and a corresponding fee structure, but the membership has the final say at the AGM. The detailed fee methodologies provide clarity and predictability; boaters know they pay by the foot, campers know they pay a flat site fee, and everyone can see how the fees for their category are constructed.

### VI.2 Annual Price Increase Cap
Annual fee increases are capped at 4% above the previous year's fees unless the Executive provides detailed justification and the increase is specifically approved by a majority vote at the September AGM. AGM notice must include the proposed fee schedule and rationale for exceeding the cap.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a key member protection clause. It provides budget predictability for members, assuring them that fees won't spike unexpectedly. The 4% cap is a common benchmark linked to inflation. It forces the Executive to provide a strong, transparent business case if a larger increase is absolutely necessary (e.g., for a major unforeseen capital repair), and requires a specific membership vote to approve it, preventing it from being buried in other agenda items.

### VI.3 Emergency Fee Adjustment
In extraordinary circumstances, the Executive may convene a Special Membership Meeting to consider fee adjustments outside the normal cycle. Emergency fee adjustments require approval by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of Voting Units present.

> **Descriptive Note:** This provides a safety valve for genuine emergencies, such as an uninsured loss, a sudden regulatory requirement, or a critical infrastructure failure. Requiring a Special Meeting and a supermajority (2/3) vote ensures that this power is used only for true emergencies, not for convenience or poor planning.

### VI.4 Membership Year, Invoicing, and Payment
- **Membership Year:** All membership rights expire on April 30th each year.
- **Invoicing:** Invoices for the upcoming membership year shall be issued on **April 1st**.
- **Payment Due Date:** All fees are due by **May 1st**. Fees unpaid after May 1st shall be subject to a penalty charge as determined by the Executive, and the assigned slip or campsite may be forfeited and offered to the waiting list.
- **Late Fees:** The Executive may establish a late fee schedule, which shall be communicated to members with the invoice.

> **Descriptive Note:** This defines the clear annual cycle. The membership year ending April 30th aligns with the start of the operational season. Invoicing on April 1st gives members a full month to pay. The May 1st deadline is firm, and the consequences (late fees, potential forfeiture of the spot) are clearly stated, encouraging timely payment and allowing the Corporation to manage its cash flow and waiting list effectively.

### VI.5 Fee Structure Transparency
The Executive shall provide a detailed breakdown of how membership fees are allocated across operational areas. This breakdown will be included in Spring General Meeting materials and available to members upon request.

> **Descriptive Note:** Transparency builds trust. Knowing where the money goes (e.g., insurance, property taxes, utilities, maintenance, reserve fund) helps members understand the value they receive and the financial realities of running the facility. It empowers them to make informed decisions when voting on fees and budgets.

### VI.6 Membership Rights Transfer
Membership rights are not transferable except in the event of a member's death (to a surviving spouse) or as provided for Unit transfers in Article VII.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a standard rule for membership corporations. Memberships are not property that can be bought or sold. They are a personal relationship with the Corporation. This prevents a market from developing in memberships and ensures that the Executive and membership retain control over who joins the community. The exception for a surviving spouse is a compassionate measure to allow a family to continue their association.

### VI.7 Lifetime Memberships

> **Article VI.7 Purpose:** This section manages the legacy of previously granted lifetime memberships. It grandfathers existing holders, clearly defines their rights, and explicitly closes the program to new awards, ensuring this historical category does not create future financial or governance imbalances.

**VI.7.1 Grandfathering of Existing Lifetime Memberships**
All individuals who held a valid lifetime membership as of the 2025 season are hereby grandfathered and shall retain their lifetime membership status under the terms set forth in this section.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a "grandfather clause," a common legal tool that preserves the rights of existing individuals when a rule is changed. It honors past commitments made by the Corporation while allowing the policy to be updated for the future.

**VI.7.2 Rights and Privileges**
Lifetime members shall enjoy the following rights:
- Exemption from annual membership fees (including any base fees or social dues).
- Full voting rights as a Voting Unit, subject to the two-vote maximum in Article IV.
- Access to all Corporation facilities and participation in activities, subject to applicable usage fees (e.g., boat slip fees based on vessel length, campsite fees at the seasonal fixed rate, etc.) and compliance with all rules and regulations.

> **Descriptive Note:** This clarifies exactly what "lifetime" means. It exempts them from the annual *membership* fee, but not from site-specific *usage* fees (like the per-foot charge for their boat slip). This distinction is fair and financially necessary, as usage fees cover the direct costs of maintaining that specific asset (the dock, the electrical pedestal), which are incurred whether the member is lifetime or not. They retain full voting rights as part of their Unit.

**VI.7.3 Non-Transferability**
Lifetime memberships are personal to the individual member and are not transferable to any other person, including family members, except as provided in Article VII for transfers upon death to a surviving spouse who was part of the same Voting Unit.

> **Descriptive Note:** This reinforces that the lifetime status is a personal privilege, not a piece of property that can be passed on. The only exception is the compassionate transfer to a surviving spouse, preventing a sudden loss for a family.

**VI.7.4 Revocation**
A lifetime membership may be revoked only in accordance with Article V (Suspension of Membership) for cause, or if the member resigns in writing. Upon revocation or resignation, all rights cease and no refund of any kind shall be provided.

> **Descriptive Note:** This confirms that lifetime members are still subject to the same rules and disciplinary processes as any other member. Their "lifetime" status does not grant them immunity from the consequences of violating the Bylaws or Code of Conduct.

**VI.7.5 Record Keeping**
The Secretary shall maintain a register of all lifetime members, which shall be included in the corporate records.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a simple but important administrative requirement to keep an official list for reference, voting verification, and historical purposes.

### VI.8 New Memberships and Initiation Fee

**VI.8.1 Availability of New Memberships**
New memberships shall be granted only when an assigned slip (for Berth Holders) or campsite (for Seasonal Campers) becomes available. The Corporation shall maintain a waiting list of applicants in the order of application received, and vacancies shall be offered to the next qualified applicant on the waiting list.

> **Descriptive Note:** New memberships shall be granted only when an assigned slip (for Berth Holders) or campsite (for Seasonal Campers) becomes available. The Corporation shall maintain a waiting list of applicants in the order of application received, and vacancies shall be offered to the next qualified applicant on the waiting list.

**VI.8.2 One-Time Initiation Fee**
All new members admitted to the Corporation shall pay a one-time, non-refundable initiation fee in addition to the applicable annual seasonal fees. The amount of the initiation fee shall be established annually by the Executive and presented to the membership for information at the Annual General Meeting.

> **Descriptive Note:** All new members admitted to the Corporation shall pay a one-time, non-refundable initiation fee in addition to the applicable annual seasonal fees. The amount of the initiation fee shall be established annually by the Executive and presented to the membership for information at the Annual General Meeting.

**VI.8.3 Exceptions**
Transfers of membership rights under Article VII are not subject to the initiation fee. The initiation fee does not apply to Associate Members unless otherwise determined by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** Transfers of membership rights under Article VII are not subject to the initiation fee. The initiation fee does not apply to Associate Members unless otherwise determined by the Executive.

## ARTICLE VII: Transfer of Membership Rights

> **Article VII Purpose:** This article creates a limited, controlled process for a member to transfer their rights to another person or family. It recognizes that life circumstances change, but balances that flexibility with the need to maintain the integrity of the membership, manage waiting lists, and prevent commercial speculation in memberships.

### VII.1 Eligibility for Transfer
Voting Units may request to transfer membership rights under the following circumstances:
- Sale of the primary asset associated with the membership (boat or camping equipment)
- Relocation of the primary residence
- Other significant life circumstances as approved by the Executive
**Lifetime memberships are not transferable** except as provided in VI.7.4.

> **Descriptive Note:** This defines the *why* of a transfer. It is not an automatic right but is tied to specific, verifiable life events. This prevents memberships from being casually traded or sold. The catch-all "other significant life circumstances" gives the Executive discretion to handle unforeseen but equally compelling situations fairly.

### VII.2 Transfer Process
The current Voting Unit must submit a written request to the Secretary, stating the reason for transfer, providing contact information for the proposed new Unit, and including written consent from all Unit members. The proposed new Unit must meet all eligibility requirements, and the Executive must approve the transfer by majority vote.

> **Descriptive Note:** This outlines the step-by-step process, ensuring it is documented and transparent. The requirement for Executive approval acts as a final check to ensure the new Unit is a good fit for the community and that the transfer meets all the rules.

### VII.3 Conditions of Transfer
The transfer includes all rights and responsibilities associated with the membership category. The new Unit assumes membership for the remainder of the current year. No new membership fee is required for transfers within the same year, and the transfer does not result in an additional membership slot.

> **Descriptive Note:** This clarifies the mechanics. The new Unit steps into the shoes of the old one. Importantly, a transfer does *not* create a new membership; it's a replacement. This is crucial for managing the total number of members and the waiting list.

### VII.4 Limitations
Only one transfer per membership is permitted each year. Transfers may not be used to circumvent waiting lists or membership caps. The Corporation may charge an administrative fee for processing transfers.

> **Descriptive Note:** These are anti-abuse provisions. Limiting transfers prevents a membership from being used as a revolving door. The administrative fee covers the Corporation's time and effort in processing the transfer.

### VII.5 Effective Date
The transfer is effective upon Executive approval and completion of all required documentation.

> **Descriptive Note:** This establishes a clear point at which the new Unit officially becomes a member and assumes all rights and responsibilities.

## ARTICLE VIII: Membership Meetings

> **Article VIII Purpose:** This article establishes the rhythm of our democratic life. It defines the different types of meetings we hold, their purposes, and the rules for how they are conducted. It ensures that members have regular, structured opportunities to be informed, provide input, and make binding decisions for the Corporation.

### VIII.1 Annual General Meeting
The Annual General Meeting (AGM) shall take place near season closing of each year. 
The order of business at the AGM shall include the following:
- Call to Order and Approval of Previous Minutes
- Activity Report
- Financial Report
- Squad Reports
- Project Report previous season
- Project Plan Next season
- Pricing for the Next Season
- Election: Election of Executive members (in accordance with Article XI).
- Governance Amendments
- Other Business

The meeting format may be in person, web conference, or hybrid, as determined by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** The AGM is the most important meeting of the year. It's where the outgoing Executive reports on the year's activities and finances, where the community approves the fee structure for the next year, and crucially, where elections are held. The standard order of business ensures a consistent and comprehensive agenda, covering accountability, future planning, and governance.

### VIII.2 Spring General Meeting
A Spring General Meeting shall be held in April of each year, with the format determined by the Executive. 
The order of business at the Spring General Meeting shall include the following:
- Call to Order and Approval of Previous Minutes
- Budget Presentation
- Project Voting
- Special Committees Plan
- Governance Voting
- Other Business
Minutes from this meeting will announce voting results.

> **Descriptive Note:** The Spring Meeting is the planning and approval meeting for the upcoming season. Its focus is forward-looking: approving the detailed operating budget, voting on proposed capital projects, and hearing from committees about their plans. This timing allows the Executive to start the season with a clear mandate and a funded plan.

### VIII.3 Seasonal Update Meetings
Two Seasonal Update Meetings are held during the operational season to ensure transparent communication and ongoing member engagement:
- June: First Seasonal Update Meeting
- August: Second Seasonal Update Meeting

Agendas include project updates, financial summaries, operational reports, squad updates, and open member forums. These meetings are informational and consultative; no binding votes or bylaw amendments are held.

> **Descriptive Note:** These are less formal but vital touchpoints. They keep the membership informed of progress and challenges during the busy season. The "open member forum" provides a regular, structured opportunity for members to raise concerns, ask questions, and offer suggestions directly to the Executive and the community. The non-binding nature allows for open discussion without the pressure of formal votes.

### VIII.4 Decision Making
Decisions at membership meetings are determined by majority vote of total votes cast by Voting Units present in person or participating via web conference, unless stated otherwise. Each Voting Unit's vote(s) shall be counted in accordance with Article IV.

> **Descriptive Note:** This establishes the basic rule of democratic decision-making: a simple majority of those voting decides the outcome, with each Unit's vote(s) counted as per the rules in Article IV. It explicitly includes remote participants, ensuring inclusivity.

### VIII.5 Special Meetings
A Special Membership Meeting may be called by the Executive or by written request of at least 15 Voting Units. Notice must be circulated at least fifteen (15) days prior to the meeting and must include the purpose and web conference instructions if applicable.

> **Descriptive Note:** This provides a mechanism to address urgent or significant matters that cannot wait for the next scheduled AGM or Spring Meeting. The low threshold for a member-requested meeting (15 Units) empowers the membership to force a discussion and vote on important issues if the Executive is reluctant to do so. The notice requirement ensures everyone has time to prepare.

### VIII.6 Notice of Meetings
Notice of the Annual General Meeting or any Special Meeting shall be given to all members not less than twenty-one (21) days nor more than sixty (60) days before the meeting date, via email, postal mail, or other electronic means as recorded in the membership register. Notice for Seasonal Update Meetings or the Spring General Meeting shall be given at least fourteen (14) days in advance.

> **Descriptive Note:** This sets clear notice periods to ensure members have adequate time to schedule attendance and, for the AGM and Special Meetings, to consider any proposals or nominations. The longer notice for the most important meetings reflects their significance.

### VIII.7 Quorum
One-third (1/3) of eligible Voting Units constitutes a quorum for any General or Special Membership Meeting. No quorum is required for Seasonal Update Meetings.

> **Descriptive Note:** Quorum is the minimum number of members that must be present for the meeting's decisions to be valid. This prevents a small, unrepresentative group from making binding decisions for the whole Corporation. The 1/3 threshold is a common and reasonable standard. Update Meetings are exempt because no binding votes are held.

### VIII.8 Notice & Web Conference Details
Written notice of all General Membership Meetings must be provided at least twenty-one (21) days in advance for the Annual and Spring Meetings, and at least fourteen (14) days in advance for the Seasonal Update Meetings.

> **Descriptive Note:** (This section appears to be a duplicate of VIII.6 and may be intended for a summary table. It reinforces the notice periods.)

### VIII.9 Participation via Web Conference
Members participating by web conference are considered present for all purposes. The Corporation shall ensure reasonable opportunity for participation via web conference technology.

> **Descriptive Note:** This modern and inclusive clause ensures that members who cannot attend in person (due to distance, work, or health) can still fully participate in their Corporation's governance. It places a positive duty on the Corporation to provide the necessary technology.

### VIII.10 Action Without a Meeting
Actions required or permitted at a membership meeting may be conducted without an in-person meeting if the Corporation delivers an electronic or paper ballot to every Voting Unit entitled to vote, provided quorum and approval thresholds are met.

> **Descriptive Note:** This allows for votes to be held entirely by mail or electronic ballot, which can be useful for specific issues where a physical meeting is impractical. It still requires achieving quorum (through returned ballots) and the necessary approval threshold (e.g., majority or 2/3) for the motion to pass.

## ARTICLE IX: Online Project Voting

> **Article IX Purpose:** This article establishes a modern, efficient, and secure method for making decisions on specific capital projects outside of the traditional meeting schedule. It is designed to increase member participation by making voting more convenient while ensuring the process is transparent and the results are legitimate.

### IX.1 Purpose and Scope
The Corporation may conduct online votes for specific, well-defined projects to facilitate member participation and efficient decision-making. A "Project" refers to a discrete capital expenditure, improvement initiative, or significant operational change, fully defined and costed using the official Project Proposal Template specified in Article X.

> **Descriptive Note:** This clarifies that online voting is not for general governance (like electing the Executive) but for specific, tangible "projects." This scope limitation prevents it from replacing the essential functions of membership meetings. The requirement for a fully defined and costed proposal ensures members have all the information they need to make an informed choice.

### IX.2 Initiation of a Project Vote
A Project Vote may be initiated by a majority vote of the Executive or by a petition signed by at least 15% of Voting Units submitted to the Secretary.

> **Descriptive Note:** This dual path for initiation provides flexibility. The Executive can bring forward projects they've developed. The petition process empowers the membership to force a vote on a project they feel is important, even if the Executive is not supportive, ensuring that ideas can bubble up from the community.

### IX.3 Project Definition and Information Package
Before voting, the Executive must prepare and distribute a comprehensive Project Information Package using the official Project Proposal Template.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is the cornerstone of an informed vote. The mandatory use of the standardized template ensures that every project is presented with the same level of detail, including scope, budget, timeline, and impact, allowing for fair comparison and thorough evaluation by members.

### IX.4 Notice and Voting Period
The Project Information Package shall be distributed to all Voting Units at least fourteen (14) days before the online vote closes. The voting period must remain open for at least seven (7) consecutive days.

> **Descriptive Note:** This provides a guaranteed minimum period for members to read the materials and cast their vote, preventing "rushed" decisions. The two-week notice before the poll closes gives ample time for discussion and questions.

### IX.5 Voting Platform
Votes are cast via a secure, third-party online voting platform, which ensures Unit authentication, vote secrecy, and result integrity. The system must apply the correct number of votes per Voting Unit (base vote plus any bonus vote), verified by the Volunteer Registry.

> **Descriptive Note:** Security and integrity are paramount. Using a reputable third-party platform provides a neutral, auditable system. The requirement for the system to automatically apply bonus votes, based on the official registry, removes a potential source of human error and ensures every Unit's full voting power is accurately counted.

### IX.6 Quorum and Passing Threshold
At least 25% of eligible Voting Units must cast votes for validity. A motion passes with a simple majority, unless it involves borrowing money, in which case a two-thirds (2/3) majority is required.

> **Descriptive Note:** This establishes the rules for a valid vote. The 25% quorum for online votes is lower than for a physical meeting, recognizing that online participation, while more convenient, may still not reach the same level. Borrowing money is a more significant financial commitment, so it rightly requires a higher supermajority threshold for approval.

### IX.7 Certification and Results
The Secretary and Treasurer, or an independent third party appointed by the Executive, certify the results. Results must be announced to the membership within seven (7) days of the vote closing, including the number of Units voting and the total vote count. Certified results from successful online Project Votes carry the same force as resolutions passed at a General Membership Meeting.

> **Descriptive Note:** This formal certification process validates the outcome. Announcing the results promptly with key data (turnout, vote count) maintains transparency. Stating that the result has the same force as an in-person vote gives it full legal and governance weight.

## ARTICLE X: Project Definition and Management

> **Article X Purpose:** This article creates a clear and consistent framework for how the Corporation conceives, plans, and approves projects. By defining what a project is, classifying its type, and mandating a standardized proposal template, it ensures accountability, transparency, and informed decision-making by both the Executive and the membership.

### X.1 Purpose
This Article provides clarity and transparency in spending authority and membership voting requirements by defining what constitutes a "Project" and establishing a standardized proposal process.

> **Descriptive Note:** This opening statement sets the clear intention of the article: to eliminate ambiguity about when a membership vote is needed and to ensure all projects are well-documented from the start.

### X.2 Definition of a Project
A "Project" is any discrete initiative involving capital expenditure or significant changes to Corporation property or operations, with a defined scope, timeline, and budget. Projects may be executed in one or multiple phases.

> **Descriptive Note:** This provides the core definition, focusing on three key elements: it's a "discrete initiative" (not ongoing operations), it involves spending or significant change, and it has a defined plan (scope, timeline, budget). The allowance for phasing acknowledges that large endeavors may need to be broken down over time.

### X.3 Project Classification
- Maintenance Project: Involves repair, replacement, or restoration of existing property to its original condition or intended function.
- Capital/Improvement Project: Involves new construction, expansion, enhancement, or major modification that adds new functionality, capacity, or features.

> **Descriptive Note:** This classification helps determine the appropriate approval pathway. Maintenance is about preserving what we have; improvements are about adding value or capability. This distinction is used in Article XII to set different spending limits for the Executive.

### X.4 Project Phasing
- Single-Phase Project: Unified scope executed continuously from start to finish.
- Multi-Phase Project: Divided into sequential phases, each with its own scope, budget, and timeline, possibly dependent on completion of previous phases and requiring separate approval.

> **Descriptive Note:** This acknowledges the reality of large-scale work, where it may be prudent to plan and fund stages separately. It makes clear that each phase may require its own approval, ensuring ongoing oversight.

### X.5 What is Not a Project
Routine operations and maintenance activities, such as annual opening/closing, lawn care, minor repairs, and purchasing consumable supplies, do not require a project proposal or membership vote if within approved budget and Executive authority.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a crucial clarification. It distinguishes between the strategic, one-off nature of a "Project" and the ongoing, routine work of running the facility. This prevents the Corporation from being bogged down by voting on every light bulb change or lawn mowing.

### X.6 Mandatory Project Proposal Process
Any initiative meeting the definition of a Project must be documented using the official Project Proposal Template, which is required for both Executive-approved projects and those requiring membership vote.

> **Descriptive Note:** This makes the template mandatory for all projects, large or small. Even if a project is within the Executive's spending authority and doesn't need a member vote, using the template ensures it is properly planned, costed, and documented in the corporate records, creating an invaluable project history.

### X.7 Template Compliance
The Project Information Package for online Project Voting must use the official Project Proposal Template. Incomplete templates may be rejected until all required information is provided.

> **Descriptive Note:** This ensures that any project put to a membership vote meets a minimum standard of completeness, protecting members from voting on poorly conceived or partially defined proposals.

### X.8 Template Maintenance and Updates
The official Project Proposal Template is maintained as a separate document in the corporate repository. Updates require a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the Executive, and substantive changes must be circulated to members for comment at least 30 days prior to adoption.

> **Descriptive Note:** This recognizes that the template itself may need to evolve over time to remain useful. Giving the Executive the power to update it, but with a supermajority vote and a member comment period for substantive changes, balances flexibility with the need for stability and input on a key governance tool.

## ARTICLE XI: Election Cycle

> **Article XI Purpose:** This article establishes a clear, fair, and transparent process for electing the Executive Board. It outlines the timeline from nominations through to the assumption of office, defines the roles of an independent Election Committee, and sets the rules for campaigning and voting to ensure a legitimate and orderly transition of leadership.

### XI.1 Nomination Window (August 1 – August 21)
Nominations for Executive positions shall be accepted via a secure digital form. Any voting member in good standing may nominate themselves or another member (with the nominee's written consent). The Election Committee shall verify the eligibility of all candidates.

> **Descriptive Note:** This sets a fixed, three-week nomination period in late summer, giving ample time for members to consider and come forward. The digital form makes it easy to participate. Requiring written consent from nominees prevents someone from being placed on the ballot against their will. The Election Committee's verification step ensures all candidates are eligible to serve (e.g., are of age, are members in good standing).

### XI.2 Campaign Period (August 22 – September AGM)
Candidates shall have the opportunity to present themselves to the membership through a hybrid approach:
- **Digital:** Candidate bios, photographs, and mission statements shall be posted to the member portal for review by all Voting Units.
- **In-Person:** Candidates may engage with members at the Marina during the August Seasonal Update Meeting, which shall include a dedicated forum for candidate introductions and questions.

> **Descriptive Note:** This creates a fair and accessible campaign period. The digital component ensures all members, even those not at the marina in late August, can learn about the candidates. The in-person forum at the August meeting allows for direct interaction and Q&A, a vital part of the democratic process.

### XI.3 Voting – In Person Only at September AGM
Actual voting for Executive positions shall occur strictly in person at the September Annual General Meeting.
- Proxy voting is permitted as set forth in Article VIII.7.
- Voting shall be by secret ballot. The Election Committee shall oversee the voting process, distribute ballots, and ensure the integrity of the count.
- Only designated Primary or Secondary Members of Voting Units in good standing may cast ballots. Each Voting Unit is entitled to cast its base vote and, if applicable, its bonus vote, up to a maximum of two votes.
- Results shall be announced at the AGM and subsequently published to the membership.

> **Descriptive Note:** This specifies that the election itself happens at the AGM, making it a key part of that important meeting. In-person, secret ballot voting is a traditional and trusted method for leadership elections. The detailed description of the process, including the role of the Election Committee and proxy voting, ensures a structured and fair vote.

### XI.4 Election Committee
At the Spring General Meeting, the Executive shall appoint an Election Committee consisting of at least three members from different Voting Units who are not standing for election in the current cycle. The Committee shall:
- Manage the nomination process,
- Verify candidate eligibility,
- Oversee the campaign period,
- Administer the in-person voting at the AGM,
- Certify the results, and
- Resolve any disputes or challenges.

> **Descriptive Note:** An independent Election Committee is a cornerstone of a fair election. Appointing them months in advance (at the Spring Meeting) gives them time to prepare. The requirement that members are from different Units and are not candidates themselves ensures impartiality and reduces the risk of bias. Their defined duties cover the entire election lifecycle.

### XI.5 Assumption of Office
Newly elected Directors shall shadow the outgoing board during the transition period. Official signing authority and duties shall transfer on **November 15th**.

> **Descriptive Note:** This provides a crucial handover period. The six weeks between the September election and the November 15th transfer of authority allows the outgoing and incoming Executives to work together, share knowledge, and ensure a smooth transition without a gap in leadership.

### XI.6 Term of Office
Elected Executive members shall serve a two-year term, beginning on November 15th following their election and ending on November 14th of the second year thereafter.

> **Descriptive Note:** A two-year term provides stability and continuity, allowing elected officers to learn the role, implement plans, and see projects through, rather than facing re-election every year. The fixed start and end dates provide clarity.

### XI.7 Contested Elections and Recounts
Any challenge to the election process or results must be submitted in writing to the Election Committee within forty-eight (48) hours of the announcement of results. The Committee may conduct a recount if warranted. The Committee's decision on any challenge shall be final.

> **Descriptive Note:** This provides a clear, short window for raising challenges, ensuring disputes are dealt with promptly while memories are fresh. Giving the Election Committee the final say (subject to any applicable laws) provides a definitive resolution, preventing endless disputes.

### XI.8 Vacancies
If a vacancy occurs on the Executive, the remaining Executive members may appoint a qualified member to fill the vacancy for the remainder of the term, as provided in Article XII.4.

> **Descriptive Note:** This provides a mechanism for continuity if a Director resigns or is removed mid-term. It allows the remaining Executive to appoint a replacement, ensuring the Board remains functional until the next election.

## ARTICLE XI-A: Executive Transition Process

> **Article XI-A Purpose:** This article details the critical handover period between the outgoing and incoming Executive teams. A structured transition ensures that institutional knowledge is preserved, administrative tasks are completed, and the new team is set up for success from day one, minimizing disruption to the Corporation's operations.

### XI-A.1 Transition Period
A formal transition period from November 15th to December 31st ensures the outgoing Executive completes administrative responsibilities and onboards the incoming Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** This defines a specific, six-week window for the transition, giving it the importance and structure it deserves, rather than leaving it to chance.

### XI-A.2 Responsibilities of the Outgoing Executive
The outgoing Executive (serving until November 14th) is responsible for financial closure, project completion, annual reporting, and documentation handover by November 30th, as specified.

> **Descriptive Note:** This holds the outgoing team accountable for wrapping up their term's work and preparing a complete handover package by a deadline, ensuring the new team isn't left chasing down information.

### XI-A.3 Responsibilities of the Incoming Executive
The incoming Executive works with the outgoing Executive during transition, participating in joint meetings, shadowing, and planning for the upcoming season.

> **Descriptive Note:** This defines the incoming team's role as active learners during the transition, not passive recipients of information. This collaborative approach accelerates their learning.

### XI-A.4 Mandatory Transition Documentation
The outgoing Executive delivers a formal Transition Package, stored in the corporate repository, which includes the Year in Review report, reviewed financial statements, membership lists, Volunteer Registry, active contracts, insurance policies, permits, lease agreements, account access details, asset inventories, and summaries of ongoing issues.

> **Descriptive Note:** This comprehensive list ensures that all critical knowledge is captured in a single, permanent document. This "corporate memory" is invaluable and protects against the loss of information when individuals leave.

### XI-A.5 Joint Transition Meetings
At least two joint meetings are held during the transition, focusing on review and handover of the Transition Package and strategic planning for the upcoming season.

> **Descriptive Note:** Mandating at least two meetings ensures there is structured, face-to-face (or virtual) time for the two teams to work through the materials and discuss strategy.

### XI-A.6 Formal Handover Ceremony
A formal handover takes place at the Spring General Meeting, with outgoing and incoming Commodores presenting summaries and strategic visions.

> **Descriptive Note:** This public ceremony at the Spring Meeting marks the official and symbolic completion of the transition, showcasing continuity to the membership and allowing the new leadership to share their vision.

### XI-A.7 Emergency Continuity Clause
If the handover is incomplete by the deadline, outgoing Executive members remain available in an advisory, non-voting capacity until transition is formally resolved or until January 31st, whichever is sooner.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a safety net. If a transition hits unexpected problems, this clause ensures the outgoing members are still available for consultation, preventing a leadership vacuum, but limits this advisory role to a maximum of two months to avoid an indefinite extension of their responsibilities.

## ARTICLE XII: Executive (Board of Directors)

> **Article XII Purpose:** This article defines the composition, powers, and basic operating rules for the Executive Board (the Board of Directors), which is responsible for the day-to-day management of the Corporation between membership meetings. It establishes the range of Board size, identifies required and optional officer positions, sets terms, and defines key authorities like spending limits.

### XII.1 Composition
The Executive shall consist of no fewer than three (3) and no more than seven (7) members, elected by the Voting Units as provided in these By-Laws. This range aligns with the provisions set forth in the Corporation's Articles of Incorporation (Charter).

> **Descriptive Note:** This sets the size of the Board. A minimum of three is often required by law. A maximum of seven keeps the Board small enough to be efficient and make decisions effectively, but large enough to distribute workload and bring diverse perspectives. It must align with the founding Charter document.

### XII.2 Officers
**Required Executive positions** include: Commodore (Chair), Treasurer, and Harbour Master (Port Captain).
**Optional Executive positions** may include: Vice-Commodore, Secretary, Campground Manager, and Director of Entertainment & Fundraising, as determined by the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** This distinguishes between roles that are considered essential for the Corporation's legal and operational functioning (Commodore, Treasurer, Harbour Master) and those that can be added based on the Board's current needs and available talent. This provides flexibility in Board structure.

### XII.3 Election and Term of Office
The term of office for each Executive member shall be two (2) years following their election and ending on the second year election of the new Executive. No Executive member may serve more than four (4) consecutive terms without a one-year break.

> **Descriptive Note:** The two-year term is reiterated here. The term limit (4 consecutive terms = 8 years) is designed to bring fresh perspectives and prevent any one individual from holding a position for an extended period, while still allowing for long service. The one-year break requirement ensures a genuine rotation of leadership.

### XII.4 Vacancies
The Executive may appoint a qualified member to fill any vacancy for the remainder of the term.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is the mechanism to fill a mid-term vacancy. The appointment is for the remainder of the unexpired term, at which point the position would be up for election in the normal cycle.

### XII.5 Disqualification of Directors
A person shall be disqualified from serving as a Director if they:
(a) are less than eighteen (18) years of age;  
(b) have been declared incapable by a court;  
(c) are an undischarged bankrupt; or  
(d) have been convicted of an offence involving fraud or dishonesty.  
Any Director becoming disqualified shall immediately resign or be removed per Article XIV.

> **Descriptive Note:** This lists the legal and ethical disqualifiers for serving on a Board of Directors, as required by the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act*. These are standard provisions to ensure the integrity and legal capacity of the Board.

### XII.6 Executive Meetings & Quorum
Executive meetings are held at least six times per year. A majority of filled Executive positions constitutes a quorum.

> **Descriptive Note:** This sets a minimum frequency for Board meetings to ensure ongoing oversight. The quorum rule (e.g., if there are 5 filled positions, a quorum is 3) allows the Board to make decisions even if some members are absent, as long as a majority is present.

### XII.7 Spending Authority
The Executive may spend on day-to-day operations without a membership vote. For Maintenance Projects, up to \$2,500 per project is permitted without prior approval. For Capital/Improvement Projects, up to \$1,000 per project is allowed without prior approval. Projects exceeding these thresholds, or multiple projects exceeding \$2,000 in a year, require a majority vote of Voting Units.
Borrowing money requires a two-thirds majority vote of Voting Units. The Corporation must maintain transparency by keeping a list of all projects using the official Project Proposal Template and making it available to members upon request.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a critical section balancing Executive efficiency with membership control over finances. It gives the Executive clear spending limits for routine operations and smaller projects. It sets a higher limit for necessary maintenance (repairing what we have) than for new capital improvements (adding new things), reflecting different risk/benefit profiles. Projects above these limits must go to the membership. Borrowing, a significant financial commitment, requires the highest level of approval (2/3 membership vote). The transparency requirement ensures members can always see how money is being spent.

### XII.8 Banking and Borrowing
Banking documents must be signed by any two of the Commodore, Treasurer, or Secretary. Borrowing money requires a special resolution (two-thirds majority) of Voting Units.

> **Descriptive Note:** The "any two" signing authority provides security and checks and balances for financial transactions. The borrowing rule is repeated here for emphasis and clarity.

### XII.9 Committees
The Executive may appoint special committees with specific mandates, which report directly to the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** This empowers the Executive to create temporary or standing committees (e.g., a "Dock Repair Committee" or a "Canada Day Committee") to focus on specific tasks and report back, distributing workload and engaging members.

## ARTICLE XIII: Duties of Executive Officers

> **Article XIII Purpose:** This article provides a clear job description for each elected officer position. It defines their primary responsibilities, ensuring that both the person in the role and the rest of the Board and membership understand what is expected of them, promoting accountability and smooth operations.

- **Commodore:** Presides at all meetings, serves as an ex-officio member of all committees, and ensures adherence to the Code of Conduct.
> **Descriptive Note:** The Commodore is the chief executive officer, the public face of the Corporation. Presiding over meetings ensures order. Being an ex-officio (automatic) member of all committees allows them to stay informed without having voting power on every committee. They are the ultimate guardian of the community's standards (Code of Conduct).

- **Vice-Commodore:** Acts as Commodore in their absence or disability.
> **Descriptive Note:** This is the standard role of a vice-president: to provide continuity and support, stepping in when the Commodore is unavailable.

- **Secretary:** Maintains meeting minutes, issues notices, is custodian of records and corporate seal, maintains member register and document repository.
> **Descriptive Note:** The Secretary is the guardian of the Corporation's official memory and records. This role is critical for legal compliance, transparency, and institutional knowledge. The duties listed are the core administrative functions that keep the Corporation organized.

- **Treasurer:** Manages funds and securities, keeps accurate accounts, deposits monies, presents financial reports at the AGM, and maintains financial documents.
> **Descriptive Note:** The Treasurer is the chief financial officer, responsible for the stewardship of the Corporation's money. Accurate accounts, timely reporting, and proper documentation are the hallmarks of this role, ensuring financial integrity and accountability.

- **Harbour Master:** Oversees maintenance and safety of marina facilities.
> **Descriptive Note:** This officer is the expert and lead for all things related to the water: docks, slips, water safety, boat launch, and related equipment. They are responsible for the operational readiness and safety of the marina.

- **Campground Manager:** Oversees maintenance, safety, and operations of the campground.
> **Descriptive Note:** This officer holds the parallel responsibility for the land-based operations: campsites, washrooms/showers, common areas, and campground rules. They ensure the campground is safe, clean, and welcoming.

- **Director of Entertainment & Fundraising:** Plans and promotes social events, implements fundraising strategies, works with volunteers, manages sponsorships and grants, and coordinates the entertainment and fundraising budget with the Treasurer.
> **Descriptive Note:** This role focuses on the "community-building" aspect of our objects. They are the chief social architect, organizing events that bring members together. They also seek external funding (sponsorships, grants) to support these activities and potentially reduce the burden on membership fees.

## ARTICLE XIV: Executive Governance

> **Article XIV Purpose:** This article provides the basic rules for how the Executive Board governs itself, specifically addressing the processes for removal of a director and for calling meetings.

### XIV.1 Removal
An Executive officer may be removed from office for cause by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the remaining Executive members, or by a simple majority vote of Voting Units at a Special Membership Meeting.

> **Descriptive Note:** This outlines the serious but necessary process for removing a director who is not fulfilling their duties or is acting improperly. "For cause" means a specific, valid reason (like those in XII.5 or a breach of duty). It provides two paths: action by the Board itself (with a high supermajority) or action by the membership, ensuring accountability to both peers and the community.

### XIV.2 Meeting Notice
Notice of all Executive meetings must be provided to all Executive members at least three (3) days prior to the meeting.

> **Descriptive Note:** This ensures all Board members have reasonable advance notice of meetings, allowing them to prepare and attend, promoting full participation and informed discussion.

## ARTICLE XV: Volunteer Squads

> **Article XV Purpose:** This article formally establishes the Volunteer Squad system as a key part of our operational model. It encourages member participation by creating structured teams focused on specific areas, recognizes the contributions of Squad members, and links this participation directly to the bonus vote system established in Article IV.

### XV.1 Purpose
To encourage member participation and distribute operational workloads, the Corporation shall maintain a system of Volunteer Squads focused on specific areas of operation.

> **Descriptive Note:** This states the core philosophy: we are a volunteer-powered organization. Squads are the primary vehicle for turning individual volunteer efforts into coordinated, effective work that benefits the whole community.

### XV.2 Squad Formation and Leadership
- The Executive may establish standing Squads for areas including, but not limited to, Social Events, Grounds & Maintenance, Water Safety, and Conservation.
- Any member from a Voting Unit in good standing may form a new Squad by presenting a charter to the Executive.

> **Descriptive Note:** This provides both top-down (Executive-initiated) and bottom-up (member-initiated) pathways for creating squads. This flexibility allows the organization to respond to identified needs and also empowers passionate members to lead new initiatives. Requiring a "charter" ensures that new squads have a clear purpose and plan.

### XV.3 Squad Authority and Reporting
- Squads may plan and execute projects within their mandate with approval from the Executive for expenditures over \$100.
- Squad Coordinators shall provide regular updates at Seasonal Update Meetings.

> **Descriptive Note:** This grants squads autonomy to operate within their defined area, but with a financial check (Executive approval for spending over $100) to ensure alignment with the budget. The public reporting at Update Meetings keeps the whole membership informed of squad activities and progress.

### XV.4 Bonus Vote for Participation
- **Purpose:** To recognize and reward substantial contributions of active Squad participants and community volunteers.
- **Eligibility:** A Voting Unit may earn one (1) bonus vote if at least one adult member of the Unit completes 20 or more verified volunteer hours within a 12-month period, as set forth in Article IV.
- **Documentation:** Squad Coordinators must maintain accurate records of member participation hours and activities.
- **Verification:** The Secretary verifies and publishes the Volunteer Registry quarterly.
- **Appeals Process:** Members may appeal their participation classification to the Executive, whose decision is final.

> **Descriptive Note:** This section operationalizes the bonus vote concept from Article IV. It clarifies that Squad Coordinators are responsible for tracking hours, the Secretary for official verification, and provides an appeals process for any disputes. This creates a complete, fair, and transparent system that connects volunteer effort directly to enhanced voting power.

## ARTICLE XVI: Contracts

Executive officers are not authorized to negotiate contracts on behalf of the Corporation unless such contracts are ratified by a majority of the Executive or the general membership. No contract shall be binding until ratification.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a crucial protection for the Corporation. It prevents a single officer from unilaterally entering into a legally binding agreement that could commit the organization to significant obligations. It ensures that all contracts are reviewed and approved by the collective body (the Executive or membership) before they become binding.

## ARTICLE XVII: Conflict of Interest

Any Executive officer or member with a direct or material interest in any business or contract involving the Corporation must declare their interest and abstain from voting on related matters.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a fundamental principle of good governance and ethics. It requires transparency when a decision-maker stands to personally benefit from a decision. Declaring the interest and abstaining from the vote ensures that decisions are made in the best interest of the Corporation, not for personal gain, protecting both the individual and the organization from accusations of impropriety.

## ARTICLE XVIII: Liability and Indemnification

> **Article XVIII Purpose:** This article provides important legal protections for the volunteers who serve on the Executive, encouraging members to step into these roles without fear of personal financial ruin for honest mistakes made while acting in good faith for the Corporation.

### XVIII.1 General Liability Protection
Executive officers are not personally responsible for errors, omissions, or negligence of the Executive related to the administration of the Corporation, provided they have acted honestly and in good faith.

> **Descriptive Note:** This reflects the legal principle that directors and officers of a not-for-profit are generally protected from personal liability for the honest, good-faith performance of their duties. It provides a crucial shield, but note the key qualifiers: "honestly and in good faith." This protection does not cover fraudulent, illegal, or self-interested actions.

### XVIII.2 Indemnification
The Corporation will indemnify and hold harmless all current and former Executive members and officers from any costs, charges, and expenses reasonably incurred by them.

> **Descriptive Note:** "Indemnification" means the Corporation agrees to pay for legal costs, damages, or other expenses if a director is sued as a result of their role with the Corporation (provided they meet the conditions in XVIII.1). This is a powerful commitment that provides significant financial security to volunteers.

### XVIII.3 Directors' and Officers' Insurance
The Corporation shall maintain Directors' and Officers' Liability Insurance.

> **Descriptive Note:** This mandates the purchase of D&O insurance, which is a policy that pays for the costs of indemnification and legal defense. This is a prudent financial measure that protects the Corporation's assets and ensures it can fulfill its indemnification promises. It's a tangible demonstration of the Corporation's commitment to protecting its volunteers.

## ARTICLE XIX: Financial and Fiscal Matters

> **Article XIX Purpose:** This article establishes the financial rules and calendar for the Corporation, ensuring accountability, transparency, and sound financial management. It defines the fiscal year, the requirements for financial statements, the budget process, and the role of an external financial reviewer.

### XIX.1 Fiscal Year
The Corporation's fiscal year ends on December 31st each year.

> **Descriptive Note:** This sets the accounting period. A calendar year-end is simple and common, allowing for year-end financial work to be done over the winter, a quieter operational period.

### XIX.2 Financial Statements
The directors shall place before the members at every annual meeting:
- Comparative financial statements covering the most recently completed financial year, prepared in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles
- The report of the public accountant, if any
- Any further information respecting the financial position of the Corporation required by the articles or by-laws

> **Descriptive Note:** This outlines what financial information must be presented to members at the AGM. "Comparative" statements (e.g., showing this year vs. last year) provide context. Following GAAP ensures consistency and professional standards.

### XIX.3 Approval of Financial Statements
The directors shall approve the financial statements before they are issued to members, and the approval shall be evidenced by the manual signature of one or more directors or a facsimile of the signatures reproduced in the statements.

> **Descriptive Note:** This formal step ensures the Board takes ownership of the financial statements, confirming they have reviewed and approved them before they are released to the membership.

### XIX.4 Financial Review
- Members shall appoint a public accountant at each AGM to conduct a Review Engagement or Audit of the financial statements, as required by law.
- The Treasurer shall present reviewed or audited financial statements to the membership for approval at the AGM.

> **Descriptive Note:** This mandates an annual external financial review by a professional. A "Review Engagement" is less detailed than a full Audit but still provides an independent opinion on the plausibility of the statements. The level required may depend on the Corporation's size and revenue, as per the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act*. The key is independent oversight.

### XIX.5 Budget and Fee Integration
- The Executive shall prepare and present an annual operating budget for the upcoming fiscal year to the membership for approval at the Spring General Meeting.
- The budget presentation must clearly show the relationship between projected expenses and proposed membership fees.
- Any proposed fee increase exceeding the 4% cap must be clearly identified and justified in the budget materials.

> **Descriptive Note:** This links the budget and fee-setting processes. The Spring Meeting budget must clearly demonstrate *why* fees need to be a certain amount to cover planned expenses. It reinforces the 4% cap rule from Article VI by requiring special justification for any larger increase.

## ARTICLE XIX-A: Public Accountant

> **Article XIX-A Purpose:** This article provides detailed rules regarding the appointment, qualifications, independence, and duties of the Public Accountant (the external financial reviewer), ensuring a professional and arms-length relationship.

### XIX-A.1 Appointment
At each annual meeting of members, the members shall appoint a public accountant to hold office until the close of the next annual meeting. If an appointment is not made, the incumbent public accountant continues in office until a successor is appointed.

> **Descriptive Note:** This ensures annual continuity of the external review role. The "holdover" provision prevents a gap if the meeting fails to make a new appointment.

### XIX-A.2 Qualifications
To be eligible as public accountant, a person must:
- Be a member in good standing of an institute or association of accountants incorporated under an Act of a province
- Be independent of the Corporation, its affiliates, and its directors and officers
- Meet any other qualifications required by law

> **Descriptive Note:** This sets the professional standards, requiring the accountant to be a licensed, qualified professional. Independence is the most critical qualification for an objective review.

### XIX-A.3 Independence
Independence is a question of fact. A person is deemed not independent if they or their business partner:
- Is a business partner, director, officer, or employee of the Corporation or any of its affiliates
- Beneficially owns or controls a material interest in the debt obligations of the Corporation or any of its affiliates
- Has been a receiver, liquidator, or trustee in bankruptcy of the Corporation within two years before their proposed appointment

> **Descriptive Note:** This provides clear, objective criteria for what constitutes a lack of independence, preventing conflicts of interest (e.g., hiring the Treasurer's spouse's accounting firm).

### XIX-A.4 Removal and Resignation
- The members may by ordinary resolution at a special meeting remove a public accountant from office.
- A public accountant who becomes disqualified shall resign immediately after becoming aware of the disqualification.
- A resignation becomes effective when a written resignation is sent to the Corporation or at the time specified in the resignation, whichever is later.

> **Descriptive Note:** This outlines the formal processes for the end of the accountant's term, whether by member vote, disqualification, or resignation.

### XIX-A.5 Right to Attend Meetings
The public accountant is entitled to attend meetings of members at the expense of the Corporation and be heard on matters relating to their duties.

> **Descriptive Note:** This allows the accountant to be present to answer questions about the financial statements, enhancing transparency.

### XIX-A.6 Duty to Attend
If a director or member gives written notice at least ten (10) days before a meeting of members to the public accountant, the public accountant shall attend the meeting at the expense of the Corporation and answer questions relating to their duties.

> **Descriptive Note:** This gives members and directors the power to compel the accountant's attendance to answer questions, a powerful tool for ensuring accountability.

### XIX-A.7 Notice of Errors
If the public accountant or a director or officer becomes aware of an error or misstatement in a financial statement on which the public accountant has reported, and if in their opinion the error or misstatement is material, they shall inform each director accordingly. The directors shall then prepare and issue revised financial statements or otherwise inform the members.

> **Descriptive Note:** This creates a duty to correct the record if a significant error is discovered after the financial statements have been issued, maintaining the integrity of the Corporation's financial reporting.

## ARTICLE XXIII: Corporate Records and Documentation

> **Article XXIII Purpose:** This comprehensive article establishes a robust system for managing the Corporation's official documents and records. It recognizes that proper record-keeping is essential for legal compliance, historical preservation, transparency, and operational continuity. It assigns responsibility and sets standards for organization, access, and security.

### XXIII.1 Document Repository Requirement
The Corporation shall maintain a secure, organized repository containing all corporate documents, records, and important communications. This repository serves as the official archive and reference source for all Corporation business.

> **Descriptive Note:** This mandates a central, official "single source of truth" for all important documents, preventing loss and confusion caused by scattered files.

### XXIII.2 Types of Documents to be Maintained
- **Governance Documents:** Certificate of Incorporation, Bylaws, meeting minutes, policies, Code of Conduct
- **Financial Records:** Financial statements, budgets, tax returns, banking records
- **Legal and Compliance Documents:** Insurance policies, contracts, permits, legal correspondence
- **Membership Records:** Membership lists, voting designations, Volunteer Registry
- **Property and Assets:** Site plans, equipment inventories, lease agreements
- **Operational Records:** Rules and regulations, emergency plans, inspection reports

> **Descriptive Note:** This provides a detailed, categorized list of what should be kept, serving as a checklist for the Secretary and ensuring nothing critical is overlooked.

### XXIII.3 Repository Management
- **Responsibility:** The Secretary is responsible for maintaining the document repository.
- **Organization:** Documents must be organized in a logical, searchable manner.
- **Retention Schedule:** The Corporation shall maintain a document retention schedule.

> **Descriptive Note:** This assigns clear responsibility (Secretary) and sets standards for usability (logical, searchable). It also mandates a retention schedule, which is a formal policy saying how long different types of documents must be kept (e.g., 7 years for tax records) and when they can be destroyed, ensuring legal compliance and preventing clutter.

### XXIII.4 Access and Security
- **Executive Access:** All Executive members have full access to the document repository.
- **Member Access:** Voting Units have access to governance documents, minutes, financial statements, and policies.
- **Confidentiality:** Sensitive documents are restricted to Executive access only.
- **Security:** The repository shall be secured against unauthorized access.

> **Descriptive Note:** This balances transparency with confidentiality. Members can see governing and financial info, but sensitive personal or legal documents are restricted. The security requirement protects against data breaches.

### XXIII.5 Digital Repository
- **Primary System:** The Corporation shall maintain a secure digital repository accessible to authorized users.
- **Backup:** Regular backups must be maintained with off-site or cloud storage.
- **Format:** Documents should be maintained in searchable formats whenever possible.
- **Index:** A master index of all documents shall be maintained and updated quarterly.

> **Descriptive Note:** This section focuses on the practicalities of the digital system, which will likely be the primary repository. It mandates backups (critical for disaster recovery) and a searchable index for easy retrieval.

### XXIII.6 Physical Documents
- **Secure Storage:** Original signed documents must be stored securely in a climate-controlled environment.
- **Digital Copies:** Physical documents should be digitized and included in the digital repository.
- **Retention:** Physical documents must be retained according to the established retention schedule.

> **Descriptive Note:** This covers the handling of original paper documents, recognizing they may still exist. The key principle is to digitize them for easy access and then store the originals safely.

### XXIII.7 Document Review and Purge
- **Annual Review:** The Executive shall conduct an annual review of the repository.
- **Secure Disposal:** Documents scheduled for disposal must be securely destroyed.
- **Archiving:** Historical documents of enduring value shall be properly archived.

> **Descriptive Note:** This establishes an annual "housekeeping" process to ensure the repository doesn't become bloated with outdated materials. It mandates secure destruction (shredding) for sensitive documents and proper archiving for historically significant items.

### XXIII.8 Access to Records
Any member in good standing may inspect the Corporation's records, including minutes, financial statements, and membership registers (excluding personal contact details), at the head office during regular business hours upon providing reasonable notice to the Secretary. Copies may be provided at the member's expense, subject to any restrictions under privacy law.

> **Descriptive Note:** This formalizes a member's right to inspect records, a key aspect of transparency and accountability under the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act*. It balances this right with practicalities (reasonable notice, during business hours, at member's expense for copies) and privacy law.

### XXIII.9 Government Filings
- The Secretary shall ensure that a copy of these Bylaws and any amendments thereto are sent to the Director under the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act* within the prescribed period after adoption.
- The Secretary shall ensure that an annual return is filed with the Director in the form and within the period fixed by the Director.
- The Secretary shall send to the Director notice of any change among the directors or of the address of a director within fifteen (15) days of such change.

> **Descriptive Note:** This outlines the Secretary's key legal obligations to file documents with the government (Corporations Canada) to keep the Corporation in good standing. Missing these filings can lead to administrative dissolution.

## ARTICLE XXIV: Dissolution

> **Article XXIV Purpose:** This article plans for the worst-case scenario – the winding up of the Corporation. It ensures that any remaining assets are distributed in a way consistent with our non-profit objects, preventing them from being distributed to members for personal gain.

### XXIV.1 Application of Law
The dissolution of the Corporation shall be governed by Part 14 of the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act*.

> **Descriptive Note:** This simply states that the legal process for dissolution will follow the federal law that governs us.

### XXIV.2 Distribution of Remaining Property
If the Corporation is dissolved or wound up, any remaining assets after paying all liabilities shall be transferred to one or more qualified non-profit organizations in Canada with purposes similar to those of the Corporation. This provision is unalterable.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is the core asset lock. It ensures that if we cease to exist, our assets (money, land, equipment) don't get cashed out to members but go to another organization with a similar charitable or community purpose. This is a fundamental requirement for non-profits. Labeling it "unalterable" provides an extra layer of protection, ensuring this clause cannot be easily removed.

### XXIV.3 Qualified Donees
If the Corporation is a registered charity or a soliciting corporation, any property remaining on liquidation after the discharge of any liabilities shall be distributed to one or more qualified donees within the meaning of subsection 248(1) of the *Income Tax Act*.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a more specific requirement if the Corporation is also a registered charity. It restricts the recipient organizations to "qualified donees" (like other registered charities) as defined by the CRA, ensuring the assets stay within the charitable sector.

### XXIV.4 Procedure
The dissolution shall be carried out in accordance with the procedures set out in the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act*, including the sending of statements of intent to dissolve, articles of dissolution, and obtaining certificates of dissolution from the Director.

> **Descriptive Note:** This references the specific legal steps required to formally dissolve, ensuring the process is done correctly and legally.

## ARTICLE XXV: By-Law Amendments

Any proposal to amend, repeal, or create a new By-Law must be submitted to the Executive in writing at least thirty (30) days prior to any General Membership Meeting.
Notice of all proposed amendments shall be distributed to Voting Units at least fifteen (15) days prior to the meeting.
Amending or repealing any By-Law requires approval by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of votes of Voting Units present and eligible to vote, provided quorum is met.
If an amendment affects the rights of a specific class or group of members in a manner different from other members, the members of that class or group are entitled to vote separately as a class or group, and the amendment must be approved by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of that class or group.
A copy of any by-law, amendment, or repeal shall be sent to the Director under the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act* within the prescribed period after adoption.

> **Descriptive Note:** This article sets the rules for changing the Bylaws themselves. It ensures that amendments are carefully considered, with sufficient notice (30 days to Executive, 15 days to members). It sets a high bar for passage (2/3 majority), recognizing that Bylaws are foundational and should not be changed lightly. The class vote provision protects distinct member groups (e.g., if a change only affected boaters, they would get a separate vote). The final sentence reminds the Secretary of the legal duty to file the change with the government.

## ARTICLE XXVI: Unanimous Member Agreements

### XXVI.1 Authorization
The Corporation may enter into a unanimous member agreement as provided in section 170 of the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act*.

### XXVI.2 Effect
A unanimous member agreement may restrict, in whole or in part, the powers of the directors to manage or supervise the management of the activities and affairs of the Corporation.

### XXVI.3 Documentation
Any unanimous member agreement shall be in writing, signed by all members of the Corporation, and shall be kept with the corporate records. A copy shall be provided to any new member upon admission.

### XXVI.4 Termination
A unanimous member agreement may be terminated by a special resolution of the members or as otherwise provided in the agreement.

> **Descriptive Note:** This article provides for a rare but possible legal structure called a Unanimous Member Agreement (UMA). A UMA is a contract signed by every single member that can, by law, transfer some or all of the directors' powers to the membership. This is a very significant step that would fundamentally change how the Corporation is governed. This article simply authorizes the possibility, sets out the basic rules (must be in writing, signed by all, kept on file, provided to new members), and notes it can be terminated by a special resolution. It's a "just in case" provision, allowing for this structure if the membership ever desired it, but it is not something that would be used in normal circumstances.

## ARTICLE XXVII: Definitions

For the purposes of these Bylaws, the following definitions apply:

- **"Act"** means the *Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act*, S.C. 2009, c. 23, as amended from time to time.
- **"Director"** means the Director appointed under section 281 of the Act.
- **"Public Accountant"** means the public accountant appointed for the Corporation under Article XIX-A.
- **"Special Resolution"** means a resolution passed by a majority of not less than two-thirds of the votes cast on that resolution.
- **"Ordinary Resolution"** means a resolution passed by a majority of the votes cast on that resolution.
- **"Voting Unit"** means a single family unit using the same premises, entitled to one base vote and potentially one bonus vote, up to a maximum of two votes total.

> **Descriptive Note:** This is a glossary of key terms used throughout the Bylaws. Defining them here ensures consistent interpretation. "Act" refers to the specific federal law that governs us. "Director" in this context means the government official at Corporations Canada, not a member of our Executive. "Special Resolution" and "Ordinary Resolution" are given their standard legal definitions, clarifying the voting thresholds required for different types of decisions throughout the document. "Voting Unit" is defined here, pulling together the concepts from Article IV.
