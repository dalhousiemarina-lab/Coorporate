### Our Commitment & Guiding Principle

Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd. (DRMC) is a member-run, non-profit
community dedicated to creating a safe, inclusive, and enjoyable
environment for all. We are stewards of a shared regional treasure. This
Code of Conduct, guided by the Good Neighbor Policy, establishes clear
standards to protect our community, property, and natural surroundings.
Every individual is expected to act with empathy and integrity, ensuring
their actions do not infringe upon the safety, peace, or enjoyment of
others. Your cooperation is vital to maintaining these standards.

### Who Must Follow These Rules

This Code applies to all individuals present on the property, including
members, their families, guests, visitors, renters, staff, volunteers,
and contractors.

Member Responsibility: Members are solely responsible for the actions of
their guests and family members. Any violation by a guest will be
considered a violation by the member.

### 1. Core Principles

1.1 Respect for All

- Treat every individual with dignity and respect.
- Harassment, bullying, threats, and discrimination of any kind are
  strictly prohibited. This includes physical, verbal, or digital
  harassment of any member, guest, staff, or volunteer.
- Use respectful language at all times. Profanity, loud arguments, and
  abusive speech are not permitted.
- Respect the privacy and peaceful enjoyment of others\' sites and
  vessels.

1.2 Safety is Non-Negotiable

- Use of Facilities Is at Your Own Risk. The Corporation does not accept
  responsibility for accidents, injuries, or damage to personal
  property.
- Always act with safety in mind for yourself and others.
- Report all accidents, hazards, or near-miss incidents immediately to
  the office or Harbour Master.
- Children under 13 years of age must be actively supervised by an adult
  (18+) at all times.
- Be familiar with the location of emergency equipment, including fire
  extinguishers, AEDs, and first aid kits.

1.3 Protect Our Environment & Property

- Maintain cleanliness of your site and common areas. The \"Pack it in,
  pack it out\" principle is strongly encouraged.
- Use designated bins for garbage, recycling, and compost. Do not leave
  trash outside overnight.
- Do not feed wildlife; this is dangerous and attracts pests.
- Inspect and clean your boat and trailer before arrival to prevent the
  spread of invasive species.

### 2. Marina-Specific Rules & Maritime Decorum

2.01 Vessel Insurance: All vessels must have valid proof of liability
insurance on file. Failure to provide proof will result in suspension of
docking privileges.

2.02 Basin Speed Limits: A strict \"No Wake\" (maximum 5 knots) policy
is enforced within the marina basin. Members are responsible for any
damage caused by their vessel\'s wake.

2.03 Swimming: Swimming is strictly prohibited within the marina basin
and near boat launch areas.

2.04 Equipment Standards:

- Vessels must be secured with appropriate marine-grade lines.
- Only certified marine-grade shore power cords may be used. Household
  extension cords are strictly forbidden.
- Dock carts must be returned to the top of the ramp or designated area
  after use and must yield to pedestrians.

### 3. Campground & Site-Specific Rules

3.01 Fires: Fires are permitted only in provided or approved, raised
fire rings. Never leave a fire unattended, and ensure it is completely
extinguished. Always follow fire ban notices.

3.02 Site Aesthetics:

- Assigned sites must be kept free of non-marine debris, unsightly
  storage, and overgrown vegetation.
- No permanent decks, sheds, or fences may be erected without a
  Board-approved permit.

3.03 General Rules:

- No running in unauthorized areas, no rough play.
- Shoes must be worn in designated areas; appropriate attire required at
  all times.

### 4. Social Conduct & Noise

4.01 Quiet Hours: Strict quiet hours are observed from 11:00 PM to 7:00
AM in all areas (Marina docks/vessels and Campground sites). During this
time, all sounds, including generators, music, and voices, must be
contained within your vessel or site and not disturb others.

4.02 Special Event Noise Exception Process: A one-time exception to
extend noise hours may be available.

1.  Application: Submit a written request to the Board at
    conduct@dalhousiemarina.ca at least 14 days in advance.
2.  Details: Specify the date, duration of noise extension, and plan to
    minimize impact.
3.  Approval: Approval is at the sole discretion of the Board and
    requires unanimous consent.
4.  Conditions: If approved, the member must notify all adjacent
    site/vessel holders at least 48 hours prior. The member remains
    fully responsible for all guests and any complaints.
5.  Limitation: This exception is granted only once per member/vessel
    per season.

### 5. Pet Policy

5.01 Control: All pets must be on a physical leash (maximum 6 ft) and
under control at all times in common areas and trails.

5.02 Clean-Up: Pet waste must be picked up and disposed of immediately.

5.03 Nuisance: Pets must not disturb others with persistent noise (e.g.,
barking). Aggressive animals are prohibited.

5.04 Restrictions: Pets are not allowed in buildings, except for
certified service animals.

### 6. Environmental Stewardship (Zero Tolerance)

6.01 Zero Discharge: The discharge of blackwater (sewage), greywater, or
oily bilge water into the basin or on land is strictly prohibited.
Members are liable for any damage caused.

6.02 Hazardous Materials: Dumping of any pollutants, including oil,
fuel, or chemicals, is prohibited. Used oil, old batteries, and fuel
containers must be transported off-site by the member for proper
recycling. Do not place these in general waste bins.

6.03 Wildlife Protection: Harassing wildlife or disturbing nesting areas
is strictly prohibited.

### 7. Volunteer Etiquette

7.01 Respect for Squads: Members shall treat Volunteer Squad members
(Docks, Landscaping, etc.) with respect. Squad Coordinators have the
authority to issue safety directions on-site; non-compliance is a breach
of this Code.

### 8. Resolving Issues, Enforcement & Appeals

8.01 Resolving Issues:

1.  Direct Conversation: If safe and comfortable, address issues
    directly with the individual involved.
2.  Seek Mediation: If unresolved, contact a Squad Coordinator or the
    Office for mediation.
3.  Formal Report: For serious or unresolved issues, submit a written
    report via email or the office drop-box. All reports will be kept
    confidential.
4.  Urgent Matters: For immediate concerns, contact the on-duty manager
    or Harbour Master.

8.02 Consequences for Violations: The Executive will apply consequences
appropriate to the severity. Possible consequences include:

- Verbal or Written Warning
- Fine (e.g., credited to Landscaping Squad for pet waste violations)
- Temporary or Season-Long Suspension of Privileges (e.g., gate access,
  docking)
- Termination of Membership (for serious, repeated, or illegal acts)

8.03 Right to be Heard: Any member facing disciplinary action has the
right to present their case to the Board.
