### Our Commitment & Guiding Principle

Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd. (DRMC) is a member-run, non-profit community dedicated to creating a safe, inclusive, and enjoyable environment for all. We are stewards of a shared regional treasure. This Code of Conduct, guided by the Good Neighbor Policy, establishes clear standards to protect our community, property, and natural surroundings. Every individual is expected to act with empathy and integrity, ensuring their actions do not infringe upon the safety, peace, or enjoyment of others. Your cooperation is vital to maintaining these standards.

---

### Who Must Follow These Rules

This Code applies to all individuals present on the property, including members, their families, guests, visitors, renters, staff, volunteers, and contractors.

**Member Responsibility:** Members are solely responsible for the actions of their guests and family members. Any violation by a guest will be considered a violation by the member.

---

### 1. Core Principles

**1.1 Respect for All**
- Treat every individual with dignity and respect.
- Harassment, bullying, threats, and discrimination of any kind are strictly prohibited. This includes physical, verbal, or digital harassment of any member, guest, staff, or volunteer.
- Use respectful language at all times. Profanity, loud arguments, and abusive speech are not permitted.
- Respect the privacy and peaceful enjoyment of others' sites and vessels.

**1.2 Safety is Non-Negotiable**
- **Use of Facilities Is at Your Own Risk.** The Corporation does not accept responsibility for accidents, injuries, or damage to personal property.
- Always act with safety in mind for yourself and others.
- Report all accidents, hazards, or near-miss incidents immediately to the office or Harbour Master.
- Children under 13 years of age must be actively supervised by an adult (18+) at all times.
- Be familiar with the location of emergency equipment, including fire extinguishers, AEDs, and first aid kits.

**1.3 Protect Our Environment & Property**
- Maintain cleanliness of your site and common areas. The "Pack it in, pack it out" principle is strongly encouraged.
- Use designated bins for garbage, recycling, and compost. Do not leave trash outside overnight.
- Do not feed wildlife; this is dangerous and attracts pests.
- Inspect and clean your boat and trailer before arrival to prevent the spread of invasive species.

---

### 2. Membership & Financial Obligations

**2.01 Membership Dues:** All membership dues and fees must be paid in full by the annual deadline (**May 1st**) to maintain good standing and privileges. Failure to pay by the deadline may result in the offer of your dock or site to the next person on the waiting list.

**2.02 Insurance Requirements:**
- **Vessel Insurance:** All vessels must have valid proof of liability insurance on file with the office **before the vessel is launched**. Failure to provide proof will result in suspension of docking privileges.
- **Camper Insurance:** All campers and trailers must have valid proof of liability insurance on file with the office.

---

### 3. Marina-Specific Rules & Maritime Decorum

**3.01 Basin Speed Limits:** A strict **"No Wake" (maximum 5 knots / 3 knots)** policy is enforced within the marina basin. Members are responsible for any damage caused by their vessel's wake.

**3.02 Swimming:** Swimming is strictly prohibited within the marina basin and near boat launch areas.

**3.03 Equipment & Safety Standards:**
- Vessels must be secured with appropriate marine-grade lines.
- Only certified marine-grade shore power cords (e.g., CSA approved) may be used. Household extension cords are strictly forbidden.
- Dock carts must be returned to the top of the ramp or designated area after use and must yield to pedestrians.

**3.04 Fueling Regulations:**
- **It is strictly forbidden to fuel any vessel at its dock.** All fueling, whether gas or diesel, must be done at the designated boat launch ramp area.
- When fueling, use extreme caution: ensure no one is inside the vessel, operate the blower before starting the engine, and properly discard any oil-soaked rags. Avoid all fuel spillage.

**3.05 Vessel Sales & Transfers:**
- If a member sells their vessel and does not intend to replace it, the member must notify the Board. The new owner of the vessel will be offered a dock slip (subject to availability and applicable fees), but will not automatically inherit the previous owner's specific slip.
- If a member intends to replace their vessel with a longer or larger one, they must notify the Board to determine if a suitable slip is available.
- Members are prohibited from subleasing their dock or moving their vessel to another dock without prior authorization from the Harbour Master or Board.

**3.06 Fishing:** Fishing is strictly prohibited anywhere on marina premises (docks, launch areas, breakwaters).

**3.07 Fireworks:** The possession, preparation, or discharge of fireworks is strictly prohibited anywhere on DRMC property.

---

### 4. Campground & Site-Specific Rules

**4.01 Fires:** Fires are permitted only in provided or approved, raised fire rings. Never leave a fire unattended, and ensure it is completely extinguished. Always follow fire ban notices.

**4.02 Site Aesthetics & Use:**
- Assigned sites must be kept free of non-marine debris, unsightly storage, and overgrown vegetation.
- No permanent decks, sheds, or fences may be erected without a Board-approved permit.
- **Boat and boat trailers must not be parked on your campsite.** Use designated storage areas.

**4.03 Vehicle & Golf Cart Use:**
- Obey all posted speed limits (**MAX 10 km/h**). Drive with caution at all times.
- Golf carts are permitted and must have at least one functioning headlight and one taillight for operation after dark.

**4.04 Camper Sales:** If a member decides to sell their camper unit, the campsite itself is not included in the sale and remains the property of DRMC. The placement of a new member on a site will be managed by the Board based on the waiting list.

---

### 5. Social Conduct & Noise

**5.01 Quiet Hours:** Strict quiet hours are observed from **11:00 PM to 7:00 AM** in all areas (Marina docks/vessels and Campground sites). During this time, all sounds, including generators, music, and voices, must be contained within your vessel or site and not disturb others. This rule applies to all days of the week.

**5.02 Special Event Noise Exception Process:** A one-time exception to extend noise hours may be available for special, pre-determined community events (e.g., Bon Ami Festival).
1.  **Application:** Submit a written request to the Board at conduct@dalhousiemarina.ca at least 14 days in advance.
2.  **Details:** Specify the date, duration of noise extension, and plan to minimize impact.
3.  **Approval:** Approval is at the sole discretion of the Board and requires unanimous consent.
4.  **Conditions:** If approved, the member must notify all adjacent site/vessel holders at least 48 hours prior. The member remains fully responsible for all guests and any complaints.
5.  **Limitation:** This exception is granted only once per member/vessel per season.

---

### 6. Pet Policy

**6.01 Control:** All pets must be on a physical leash (maximum 6 ft) and under the control of a responsible person **at all times** in common areas, docks, and trails.

**6.02 Clean-Up:** Pet waste must be picked up immediately and disposed of in proper garbage receptacles.

**6.03 Nuisance & Safety:** Pets must not be left unattended. They must not disturb others with persistent noise (e.g., barking). Aggressive animals are prohibited.

**6.04 Restrictions:** Pets are not allowed in buildings, except for certified service animals.

---

### 7. Environmental Stewardship (Zero Tolerance)

**7.01 Zero Discharge:** The discharge of blackwater (sewage), greywater, or oily bilge water into the basin or on land is strictly prohibited. Members are liable for all clean-up costs and damages.

**7.02 Hazardous Materials:** Dumping of any pollutants, including oil, fuel, or chemicals, is prohibited. Used oil, old batteries, and fuel containers must be transported off-site by the member for proper recycling. **Do not place these in general waste bins.**

**7.03 Wildlife Protection:** Harassing wildlife or disturbing nesting areas is strictly prohibited.

---

### 8. Volunteer Etiquette

**8.01 Respect for Squads:** Members shall treat Volunteer Squad members (Docks, Landscaping, etc.) with respect. Squad Coordinators have the authority to issue safety directions on-site; non-compliance is a breach of this Code.

---

### 9. Resolving Issues, Enforcement & Appeals

**9.01 Resolving Issues:**
1.  **Direct Conversation:** If safe and comfortable, address issues directly with the individual involved.
2.  **Seek Mediation:** If unresolved, contact a Squad Coordinator or the Office for mediation.
3.  **Formal Report:** For serious or unresolved issues, submit a written report via email or the office drop-box. All reports will be kept confidential.
4.  **Urgent Matters:** For immediate concerns, contact the on-duty manager or Harbour Master.

**9.02 Consequences for Violations:** The Executive will apply consequences appropriate to the severity. Possible consequences include:
- Verbal or Written Warning
- Fine (e.g., credited to Landscaping Squad for pet waste violations)
- Temporary or Season-Long Suspension of Privileges (e.g., gate access, docking)
- **Immediate Ejection and/or Termination of Membership** (for serious, repeated, or illegal acts, or for non-compliance with mandatory insurance requirements).

**9.03 Right to be Heard:** Any member facing disciplinary action has the right to present their case to the Board.
