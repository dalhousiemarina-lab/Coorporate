# DRMC EXECUTIVE ELECTION & NOMINATION PROTOCOL

**Document Version: 3.0 – Effective Date: February 28, 2026**

This protocol establishes a structured, transparent, and skills-based process for electing the Board of Directors of the Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd (DRMC), as mandated by **Article XI** of the Corporate Bylaws.

### 1. FORMATION OF THE GOVERNANCE & NOMINATING COMMITTEE (GNC)

To move away from informal recruitment, a standing committee shall be formed no later than **June 1st** of each year.

- **Composition:** Three (3) members in Good Standing. One (1) current Director (not up for re-election) and two (2) non-Director members appointed by the Board.
- **Mandate:** \* Audit the current Board's skill gaps (e.g., lack of legal, financial, or maritime engineering expertise).
  - Identify and recruit candidates who embody the "Human-Centric" stewardship model.
  - Evaluate upcoming vacancies based on the staggered term schedule (**Section 11.7**).

### 2. STRUCTURED NOMINATION PROCESS

The GNC shall follow a three-step vetting process to ensure high-quality leadership:

**Step A: Skills Audit & Succession Planning**

- The GNC interviews outgoing Directors to identify critical   "Institutional Memory" that needs to be transferred.
- A "Call for Nominations" is issued to the membership by **August 1st**, highlighting the specific skills needed for the current cycle.

**Step B: Candidate Vetting & Interviews**

- Interested candidates must submit a brief "Expression of Interest" and a CV.
- The GNC conducts interviews to ensure candidates understand their   **Fiduciary Duties** and the **4% Rule**.
- Qualifications are checked (e.g., verifying "Good Standing" and lack of bankruptcy status).

**Step C: Nominations by Petition**

- To ensure fairness and prevent "Board Gatekeeping," any member in Good Standing may bypass GNC recommendation by submitting a petition signed by **10% of the Voting Units**.
- Petitions must be submitted to the Secretary at least 21 days before the AGM.

### 3. ELECTION PROCEDURES

Elections take place during the **September Annual General Meeting
(AGM)**.

- **The Slate Presentation:** The GNC presents its "Recommended Slate" alongside any "Petition Candidates." Each candidate is granted 3 minutes to speak to the membership.
- **Voting Method:** \* Voting shall be conducted via **Secret Written Ballot** to ensure privacy and prevent social coercion within the community.
  - Digital/Hybrid participants shall use a secure, encrypted voting platform that verifies their **Voting Unit** status.
- **Majority Requirement:** An **Absolute Majority (50% + 1)** of votes cast is required for a Director to be seated. If no candidate reaches this threshold in a multi-candidate race, a run-off between the top two shall occur immediately.

### 4. SAFEGUARDING VALUE & CONTINUITY

To protect the Corporation's long-term regional mandate, the following
safeguards are enforced:

- **Staggered Terms:** As per **Section 11.7**, the Board remains split (approximately half of the directors elected each year) to ensure the Marina never loses all its experienced leaders at once.
- **Term Limits:** No Director may serve more than **four consecutive two-year terms (8 years)**. After an 8-year tenure, a Director must vacate the Board for at least one full term (2 years) before seeking re-election.
- **Conflict Disclosure:** All candidates must sign a preliminary
  **Conflict of Interest Disclosure** before their name appears on the ballot.

### 5. DOCUMENTATION & LEGAL RECORD

- **Official Minutes:** The Secretary must record the total number of ballots cast, the number of spoiled ballots, and the final tally for each candidate.
- **Ballot Retention:** Physical and digital ballots must be secured by the Secretary for 90 days following the election in case of a requested recount or audit.
- **Federal Filing:** Within 15 days of the election, the Secretary must file **Form 4006 (Change of Directors)** with Corporations Canada to maintain federal compliance.

### 6. REMOVAL OF OFFICERS

In accordance with **Section 11.8** of the Bylaws and the NFP Act:

- An Officer or Director may be removed by an **Ordinary Resolution** of the members at a Special Meeting if they fail to uphold the NPO's values or breach their Fiduciary Duty.
- Automatic resignation occurs if a Director misses **three consecutive meetings** without a medical excuse (**Section 9.04** of the Bylaws).   *(Note: Section 9.04 refers to the previous numbering; this is now covered under Article XIV of the Bylaws.)*
