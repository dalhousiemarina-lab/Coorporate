# DRMC EXECUTIVE ELECTION & NOMINATION PROTOCOL

**Document Version: 3.1 – Effective Date: February 28, 2026**

This protocol serves as the definitive operational guide for the annual election of the Board of Directors (the Executive) of the Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd (DRMC). It is established in accordance with **Article XI** of the Corporate Bylaws and outlines a structured, transparent, and skills-based process to ensure leadership continuity and community representation.

---

## 1. PURPOSE & GOVERNING PRINCIPLES

This document details the specific procedures, timelines, and responsibilities for the Executive election cycle. The process is designed to:

- **Identify & Recruit:** Proactively seek candidates with the skills needed to fill identified gaps on the Board.
- **Ensure Fairness:** Provide an equitable and transparent path to the ballot for all eligible members.
- **Mandate Compliance:** Strictly adhere to the voting and eligibility rules defined in the Bylaws.
- **Safeguard Continuity:** Manage the transition of power to preserve institutional knowledge.

---

## 3. PHASE 1: GOVERNANCE & NOMINATING COMMITTEE (GNC) FORMATION

**Timeline: By June 1st**

To ensure an independent and rigorous process, a standing GNC is formed annually.

- **Composition:** Three (3) members in Good Standing.
    - One (1) current Director who is **not** up for re-election in the current cycle.
    - Two (2) non-Director members appointed by the Board.
- **Responsibilities:**
    - Manage the entire nomination and election process as outlined in this protocol.
    - Remain neutral; members of the GNC cannot be candidates in the election they oversee.
    - Report directly to the membership on the outcome of the election.

---

## 4. PHASE 2: STRUCTURED NOMINATION PROCESS

### Step A: Skills Audit & Call for Nominations (June 1 – August 1)

1.  **Skills Audit:** The GNC interviews outgoing Directors to identify critical skills, experience, and "Institutional Memory" that will be lost and need to be replaced or supplemented on the new Board.
2.  **Call for Nominations:** By **August 1st**, the GNC issues a formal "Call for Nominations" to all members. This notice must:
    - Highlight the specific skills and expertise needed for the upcoming term (e.g., financial, legal, engineering, volunteer coordination).
    - Provide the deadline for submissions (**August 21st**).
    - Include the "Expression of Interest" form and instructions for submission.

### Step B: Candidate Vetting & Interviews (August 22 – August 31)

1.  **Submission:** Interested candidates submit a completed "Expression of Interest" form and a CV or summary of relevant experience to the GNC.
2.  **Eligibility Verification:** The GNC verifies that each candidate meets the legal and Bylaw requirements:
    - Is a member in Good Standing of a Voting Unit.
    - Is not an undischarged bankrupt.
    - Is over the age of 18.
    - Has no conflict of interest that would prevent them from serving.
3.  **Interviews:** The GNC conducts interviews to assess the candidate's understanding of:
    - Their **Fiduciary Duties** as a Director.
    - The Corporation's **Objects** (as per Charter Article II).
    - The **4% Rule** for fee increases (Bylaw Article VI.2).
    - The "Human-Centric" stewardship model of the Marina.

### Step C: Nominations by Petition

To prevent "Board Gatekeeping" and ensure any eligible member can stand for election:

- Any member in Good Standing who was not recommended by the GNC may bypass the committee by submitting a petition.
- The petition must be signed by at least **10% of the total Voting Units**.
- The signed petition must be submitted to the Secretary no later than **21 days before the AGM**.
- Upon verification, the candidate's name will be added to the final ballot alongside the GNC's recommended slate.

---

## 5. PHASE 3: FINAL SLATE & CAMPAIGN PERIOD

**Timeline: August 22nd – September AGM**

- **Announcement of Final Slate:** The GNC publishes the final list of candidates to all Voting Units. This list includes:
    - The GNC's "Recommended Slate."
    - Any "Petition Candidates."
    - For each candidate: a bio, photograph, and mission statement as provided during the application process.
- **Campaigning:**
    - **Digital:** Candidate information is hosted on the member portal.
    - **In-Person:** A dedicated **Candidate Forum** is held during the **August Seasonal Update Meeting**. Each candidate is granted equal time (e.g., 3 minutes) to speak and answer questions from the membership. The GNC moderates this forum to ensure fairness.

---

## 6. PHASE 4: ELECTION DAY PROCEDURES (AT THE AGM)

**Timeline: September (at the AGM)**

- **Voting Method:** Voting shall be conducted strictly **in person** via **Secret Written Ballot**. (Digital/Hybrid participation for the meeting is permitted, but voting itself is in-person only to ensure integrity, as per Bylaw Article XI.3).
- **Voter Eligibility:**
    - Only the designated **Primary or Secondary Member** of a Voting Unit in Good Standing may cast the Unit's ballot(s).
    - The GNC and Secretary will maintain a final voters list at the registration table.
- **Casting Votes:**
    - Each eligible Voting Unit receives a ballot.
    - The voter must indicate their vote for each open Board position.
    - The GNC oversees the voting booth and ballot box.
- **Vote Counting:**
    - The GNC counts the ballots immediately following the close of voting, in the presence of at least one witness who is not a candidate.
    - The Secretary records the official results: total ballots cast, spoiled ballots, and the final tally for each candidate.
- **Majority Requirement:** A candidate must receive an **Absolute Majority (50% + 1)** of the votes cast for that position to be elected. If no candidate reaches this threshold in a multi-candidate race, a run-off vote between the top two candidates shall be conducted immediately.

---

## 7. PHASE 5: POST-ELECTION & TRANSITION

- **Challenge & Recount:** Any challenge to the election process or results must be submitted in writing to the GNC within **48 hours** of the results announcement. The GNC may conduct a recount. The GNC's decision on any challenge is final (Bylaw XI.7).
- **Federal Filing:** Within **15 days** of the election, the Secretary must file **Form 4006 (Change of Directors)** with Corporations Canada (Bylaw XXIII.9).
- **Official Transition Period:**
    - **November 15th:** Newly elected Directors officially assume office. A formal transition period begins, running until December 31st (Bylaw Article XI-A).
    - Outgoing Directors must provide a formal **Transition Package** to the incoming Board by November 30th.
    - Joint meetings are held to ensure the seamless transfer of institutional knowledge, signing authority, and operational plans.

---

## 8. DOCUMENTATION & LEGAL RECORD

The Secretary is responsible for securing the following records for a minimum of 90 days post-election:

- The final certified voters list.
- All submitted candidate applications and petitions.
- The official Minutes of the AGM, including the recorded vote tally.
- All physical and digital ballots (secured and sealed).
