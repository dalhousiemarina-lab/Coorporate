# Meeting Procedures: Quick Reference Guide

## 1. The Right to Speak

**Role of the Chair**
- **Grants permission to speak.** Speaking time is limited to **3 minutes** per subject (proposition, amendment, sub-amendment).
- Ensures only **one question is discussed at a time**.
- Does not participate in the debate, except to provide clarifications.

**Role of the Speaker**
- Must address the Chair and use respectful language.
- Remarks must be relevant to the question at hand and avoid repetition.

**Point of Order**
- A delegate can interrupt to ask the Chair to enforce the rules.
- The Chair rules on the point or may put the question to a vote.

## 2. Propositions and Amendments

**Main Proposition**
1.  **Reading:** The resolution is read aloud.
2.  **Motion:** A delegate must **propose** the motion, and another must **second** it.
3.  **Debate:** The motion becomes property of the assembly. The proposer speaks first, followed by the seconder (if desired), then others.
4.  **Right of Reply:** At the end of the debate, the proposer speaks again to summarize arguments.

**Amendments**
- A delegate (with a seconder) can propose an **amendment** to the main motion.
- If accepted by the Chair, debate on the main motion **stops** and focuses **exclusively on the amendment**.
- An amendment can insert, delete, or replace words/phrases.
- It must be within regulations and not equivalent to rejecting the main motion.

**Sub-Amendments**
- A delegate (with a seconder) can propose a **sub-amendment** to an amendment.
- Debate then focuses **exclusively on the sub-amendment**.
- **Sub-amendments cannot be amended.**

## 3. Voting Procedures

**Calling the Vote**
- When no one else requests the floor, debate is closed. The Chair reads the motion and calls for a vote.
- Only **one proposition** is voted on at a time.

**Motion to Call the Question (Cloture)**
- A delegate (with a seconder) can move to end debate and vote immediately.
- The Chair puts this motion to a vote. If a majority agrees, debate ends and the main motion is voted on.

**Types of Vote**
- **Show of Hands (Default):** The Chair asks for "For," "Against," and "Abstentions."
- **Secret Ballot:** Can be requested by two delegates. The request is approved by a majority show of hands.

**Majority & Chair's Vote**
- **Decision:** Adopted by **absolute majority** (more than half of the votes cast).
- **Chair's Vote:** The Chair votes only in a secret ballot or to break a tie. In case of a tie, the Chair can cast the deciding vote or call for a second vote.

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Propose Main Motion** | Yes | Yes | Majority | Initiates debate on a resolution. |
| **Amend a Motion** | Yes | Yes (on amendment only) | Majority | Modifies the main motion. |
| **Sub-Amend** | Yes | Yes (on sub-amendment only) | Majority | Modifies an amendment. Cannot be amended. |
| **Call the Question** | Yes | No | Majority | Ends debate and forces a vote on the motion. |
| **Point of Order** | No | No | Chair Ruling | Asks the Chair to enforce the rules. |
| **Secret Ballot** | Yes (2 delegates) | No | Majority | Changes the voting method for the current question. |
