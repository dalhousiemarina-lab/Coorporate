## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Emergency Response Plan (ERP) is to establish clear procedures for responding to emergencies at Dalhousie Regional Marina Ltd. (DRMC) facilities. This plan ensures the safety of members, guests, volunteers, and staff; protects property and the environment; and enables a coordinated, effective response to crises. It fulfills the requirement set out in Bylaw Article XX.2 to maintain an emergency response plan for both water and land operations.

---

## 2. Scope

This plan applies to all DRMC property, including:

- Marina basin and docks
- Campground and campsites
- Clubhouse and office
- Boat launch ramp
- Parking areas
- Common grounds and trails
- Storage areas

All members, guests, volunteers, contractors, and staff are expected to familiarize themselves with this plan and follow its procedures in an emergency.

---

## 3. Definitions

- **Emergency**: An unplanned event requiring immediate action to protect life, property, or the environment (e.g., fire, medical crisis, fuel spill, severe weather, man‑overboard).

- **Incident Commander (IC)**: The person in charge of coordinating the emergency response until professional responders arrive. Typically the most senior DRMC official present (Harbour Master, Campground Manager, or Executive member).

- **First Aid Attendant**: A person with current first aid certification (e.g., Standard First Aid, CPR) who can provide initial medical care.

- **AED**: Automated External Defibrillator.

- **SOP**: Standing Operating Procedure.

- **Muster Point**: A designated safe location where people gather during an evacuation.

## 7. Emergency Procedures

### 7.1 General Emergency Response (Any Incident)

1. **Assess** the situation – ensure your own safety first.
2. **Call 911** if there is immediate danger to life, threat of serious injury, or significant property/environmental damage.
3. **Provide aid** if trained and safe to do so (first aid, fire extinguisher use, etc.).
4. **Notify** the Harbour Master, Campground Manager, or office immediately.
5. **Follow** the specific procedures below as applicable.
6. **Document** the incident using the Incident Report Form (Appendix A) as soon as it is safe to do so.

---

### 7.2 Fire

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Small fire** (e.g., trash can, BBQ) | Use appropriate fire extinguisher if safe. Ensure extinguisher is rated for the type of fire. After extinguishing, notify office and await inspection. |
| **Large fire** (building, dock, vehicle) | 1. Evacuate the immediate area.<br>2. Call 911 immediately.<br>3. Alert nearby persons – shout "FIRE!"<br>4. Proceed to designated muster point.<br>5. Notify Harbour Master/Campground Manager.<br>6. Do not re‑enter until cleared by fire department. |
| **Wildfire** | 1. Evacuate immediately to designated muster point or off‑site if directed.<br>2. Call 911.<br>3. Follow instructions from emergency personnel. |

**Fire Prevention**:
- No open flames on docks.
- BBQ use in campground only in designated areas.
- Fireworks strictly prohibited.
- Report faulty electrical equipment immediately.

---

### 7.3 Medical Emergency

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Minor injury** (cuts, bruises) | Provide first aid. Document incident. |
| **Serious injury** (unconscious, bleeding, difficulty breathing, suspected heart attack, stroke) | 1. Call 911 immediately.<br>2. Provide first aid if trained.<br>3. Send someone to direct emergency responders to the location.<br>4. Do not move the person unless necessary for safety.<br>5. If AED is available and needed, retrieve and use as directed. |
| **Drowning / Near‑drowning** | 1. Call 911.<br>2. If safe, rescue using throw bag, life ring, or boat. Do not enter water unless trained.<br>3. Begin CPR immediately if person is unresponsive and not breathing.<br>4. Use AED as soon as available. |

**First Aid Certified Personnel**:
- A list of current first aid certified members and staff is maintained in the office and updated annually.

---

### 7.4 Fuel Spill / Hazardous Material Release

**On Land**:
1. Stop the source of the spill if safe.
2. Contain the spill using spill kit (absorbent pads, booms).
3. Call 911 if spill poses immediate threat to people or environment.
4. Report to Environment Canada Spill Reporting (1-800-565-1633) if spill exceeds reportable quantity (contact office for guidance).
5. Notify Harbour Master/Campground Manager immediately.
6. Do not wash down with water – use absorbent materials.
7. Dispose of contaminated materials as hazardous waste.

**In Water**:
1. Stop the source of the spill if safe.
2. Contain using spill boom (if available).
3. Call 911 and Canadian Coast Guard (VHF Channel 16 or 1-800-267-7270).
4. Notify Harbour Master immediately.
5. Do not use dispersants unless directed by authorities.

**Fueling Prohibition**: Fueling vessels at docks is strictly prohibited. All fueling must occur at the designated boat launch ramp area (per Code of Conduct Section 3.04).

---

### 7.5 Man Overboard

1. **Shout** "MAN OVERBOARD!" and point to the person.
2. **Throw** a life ring, throw bag, or any floating object to the person.
3. **Assign** someone to keep visual contact at all times.
4. **Call 911** and notify Harbour Master immediately.
5. **Deploy** rescue boat if available and trained operator is present.
6. **Do not** dive in unless you are trained and it is safe.
7. **Recover** person using boat if possible; if not, direct emergency responders.
8. **Provide** first aid/CPR as needed.

---

### 7.6 Severe Weather

| Weather Event | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| **Thunderstorm / Lightning** | 1. Seek shelter indoors or in hard‑topped vehicle.<br>2. Stay off docks, out of water, and away from tall objects.<br>3. Wait 30 minutes after last thunder before resuming activities. |
| **High Winds / Gale Warning** | 1. Secure boats, equipment, and loose items.<br>2. Add extra lines/fenders.<br>3. Campers should lower awnings and secure outdoor items.<br>4. Monitor marine forecasts. |
| **Hurricane / Extreme Storm** | 1. Follow directives from Executive.<br>2. Evacuate if advised by authorities.<br>3. Secure all property before leaving. |

**Monitoring**: The Harbour Master and Campground Manager shall monitor Environment Canada marine and weather forecasts daily during the operating season.

---

### 7.7 Missing Person

1. Gather information: last seen, description, clothing, direction of travel.
2. Search immediate area.
3. If not found within 15 minutes, call 911.
4. Notify Harbour Master/Campground Manager.
5. Coordinate with emergency responders.
6. Preserve last known location for search teams.

---

### 7.8 Bomb Threat / Suspicious Package

1. **Do not** touch or move the package.
2. Evacuate the immediate area calmly.
3. Call 911 immediately.
4. Notify Harbour Master/Campground Manager.
5. Follow instructions from law enforcement.
6. Do not re‑enter until cleared.

---

### 7.9 Power Failure

1. Check if neighbours are affected.
2. Report outage to NB Power (1-800-663-6272).
3. Use flashlights – **do not** use candles.
4. If prolonged, check on vulnerable members (elderly, those with medical needs).
5. Harbour Master to check sump pumps (if applicable) and dock electrical safety.
6. Campground Manager to ensure campers are safe.

## 8. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities in an Emergency |
|------|----------------------------------|
| **Harbour Master** | Overall incident command for marina‑related emergencies. Maintains dock emergency equipment. Liaises with Coast Guard and marine responders. |
| **Campground Manager** | Overall incident command for campground‑related emergencies. Maintains campground emergency equipment. Liaises with fire and ambulance. |
| **Commodore** | Executive oversight. Authorizes official communications. Liaises with media if necessary. |
| **Treasurer** | Manages insurance claims and financial aspects of response/recovery. |
| **Secretary** | Documents the incident, maintains records, handles member notifications. |
| **All Members/Staff** | Report emergencies immediately. Follow instructions from Incident Commander. Assist as directed and trained. |

---

## 9. Training and Drills

- **Annual Training**: All Executive members and squad coordinators shall review this ERP annually.
- **First Aid/CPR**: At least two members/staff on site during peak seasons should hold current first aid certification.
- **Drills**:
  - Fire drill (campground and clubhouse) – annually.
  - Man overboard drill (marina) – annually, before peak season.
  - Spill response drill – every two years.
- **New Member Orientation**: All new members shall receive a summary of emergency procedures upon registration.

Drills shall be documented, including date, participants, and lessons learned. Improvements to the ERP shall be made as needed.

---

## 10. Post‑Incident Reporting

After any emergency incident:

1. Complete the **Incident Report Form** (Appendix A) within 24 hours.
2. Submit to the Secretary for filing in the Document Repository.
3. Executive shall review the incident and response within 30 days to identify lessons learned and update the ERP if necessary.
4. If required, notify insurers and file claims through the Treasurer.

---

## 11. Plan Review and Updates

This Emergency Response Plan shall be reviewed:

- **Annually** by the Executive before the start of the operating season.
- **After any significant incident** to incorporate lessons learned.
- **When facilities, equipment, or contact information changes significantly.**

Updates require approval by a simple majority of the Executive. Members shall be notified of significant changes via email or notice board.
