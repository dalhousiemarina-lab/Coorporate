### 4. USE OF FACILITY & CODE OF CONDUCT

The Member agrees to adhere to the DRMC Code of Conduct, including:

4.1 General Conduct:

- Quiet Hours: 11:00 PM to 8:00 AM. No generators or loud music.
- Liveaboard Prohibition: The assigned slip or campsite may not be used as a permanent residence or liveaboard at any time.
- Fireworks: Absolutely NO fireworks are permitted.
- RV Alterations: All alterations, additions, and new deck structures must be approved by management.
- Fishing: No fishing is permitted on the marina premises.
- Golf Carts: Golf carts must have at least one light in the front and one in the back.

4.2 Safety & Environmental Rules:

- Speed Limit: "No Wake" / 3 knots maximum in the basin.
- Fueling Prohibition: It is strictly forbidden to fuel any boat or vessel at your dock. All boats must be fueled at the designated ramp area.
- Zero Discharge: No dumping of greywater, blackwater, fuel, or hazardous chemicals.
- Waste: All hazardous waste (batteries, oil) must be disposed of off-island at a certified facility.
- Wildlife: Feeding or disturbing nesting birds is strictly prohibited.
- Electrical Safety: Members must use a C.S.A. approved extension cord, properly fastened to the dock.
- Children on Docks: Children under 12 years old should not be alone on the docks without a life jacket and must be under the supervision of a parent.

4.3 Pet Control:

- Unleashed pets are prohibited. All pets must be kept on a leash at all times, must not disturb other members, and must never be left alone. Owners must clean up after them.

### 5. TRANSIENT RE-RENTAL POLICY

1.  Notification Requirement: Member must notify the Office if their slip or campsite will be vacant for a period exceeding 48 hours.
2.  Right to Re-Rent: The Corporation retains the right to rent the declared vacant slip or campsite to transient visitors.
3.  Revenue Retention: All revenue generated is retained by the Corporation.

### 6. INSURANCE, LIABILITY & INDEMNIFICATION

6.1 Mandatory Insurance: The Member must maintain a minimum of $1,000,000 Liability Insurance on their vessel or RV and provide proof to the Corporation.

6.2 Waiver of Claims and Release (Strongest Clause):

- Risk: Use of the facility is entirely at the Member's own risk. The Corporation assumes no liability for loss or damage to personal property due to weather, fire, theft, vandalism, or mechanical failure of facilities.
- Hold Harmless: The Member hereby waives any and all claims they have, or may have in the future, against the Corporation and releases the Corporation from all liability, including those that arise from the use of the Marina and its premises, due to any cause whatsoever, including negligence, breach of contract, or breach of any statutory or other duty of care.

6.3 Damage Liability: The Member is fully responsible for all costs associated with damage to the Corporation's property (docks, buildings, utilities, etc.) caused by the Member, their family, their vessel, their vehicle, or their guests. Payment for such damages is due immediately upon receipt of a repair invoice.

6.4 Insurance Warning: WARNING TO MEMBERS: You should notify your insurer that you have signed this agreement, as this Waiver of Claims may invalidate your insurance unless you have the consent of the insurer in writing.

### 7. FINANCIAL SECURITY & ASSIGNMENT

7.1 Non-Assignment & Transfer Policy:

- Non-Assignment of Space: This Agreement and the right to occupy the assigned slip or campsite are non-transferable and non-assignable. The Member cannot sublease their dock or move their boat to another dock without permission.
- Selling an RV or Boat on Site: If the Member sells their RV or boat, the site itself must not be included in the sale. The site will be offered to the waiting list.
- Selling a Boat with Intent to Vacate: If a Member sells their boat and does not buy a replacement, the new owner will be placed on the waiting list. The original member receives no refund.

7.2 Corporation's Lien Rights: The Corporation shall have a general and specific lien against the Member's vessel(s), equipment, RV, and contents for unpaid sums due for use of facilities and services, fees, or for damage caused. The Corporation may detain the property until all outstanding sums are paid in full.

### 8. DISCIPLINE & TERMINATION

The Board reserves the right to terminate this agreement with 15 days' notice for material breaches of the Code of Conduct or non-payment. In the event of expulsion, the Member must remove all property from the island within 7 days.
