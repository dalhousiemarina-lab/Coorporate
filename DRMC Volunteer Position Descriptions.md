## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to provide clear, written expectations for all volunteer roles within the Dalhousie Regional Marina Club (DRMC). Article XV of the Bylaws establishes the volunteer structure (Squads and Committees), but without formal descriptions, roles can become ambiguous. These descriptions are designed to:

- Help recruit suitable volunteers with the right skills and interests.
- Set clear performance expectations for each role.
- Prevent volunteer burnout by defining the scope and time commitment.
- Ensure continuity and institutional knowledge when volunteers transition.

These descriptions supplement the Bylaws and should be reviewed by all volunteers before accepting a role.

---

## 2. General Principles for All Volunteers

All DRMC volunteers are expected to:

- **Align with the Six Pillars:** Conduct themselves in a manner that reflects the club's commitment to Governance, Transparency, Teamwork, Ingenuity, Modernization, and Accountability.
- **Follow Governing Documents:** Adhere to the Bylaws, Code of Conduct, and any relevant policies or procedures.
- **Communicate Proactively:** Keep their Squad Lead or the Executive informed of their availability, progress, and any challenges they encounter.
- **Respect Confidentiality:** Treat sensitive information (e.g., member data, financial details, personnel matters) with appropriate confidentiality.
- **Prioritize Safety:** Report any hazards or safety concerns immediately and follow all safety protocols.

---

## 3. Squad Structure and Leadership

DRMC volunteers are organized into **Squads**, each focused on a specific area of club operations. Each Squad is led by a **Squad Lead**, who is responsible for coordinating activities, reporting to the Executive, and serving as the primary point of contact for that Squad.

Squad Leads are typically appointed by the Executive and may be (but are not required to be) members of the Board of Directors. All Squad members report to the Squad Lead.

## 4. Squad Descriptions

The following sections detail the purpose, composition, and responsibilities of each active DRMC Squad.

### 4.1 FinOps Squad

**Date of Founding:** October 2025  
**Squad Lead:** Treasurer (Susan Savoie-Arsenault)

**Purpose:** To ensure the financial stability, transparency, and accountability of the DRMC. The FinOps Squad is responsible for all financial matters, including budgeting, bookkeeping, reporting, and compliance.

**Typical Composition:** 3–5 members, including the Treasurer. Should include individuals with experience in accounting, finance, or bookkeeping.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Maintain accurate financial records using the club's accounting software (e.g., SAGE).
- Prepare and present monthly financial reports to the Executive and quarterly reports to the membership.
- Lead the annual budgeting process.
- Ensure timely filing of all CRA returns (including payroll, HST, and T4s).
- Manage accounts payable and receivable, including the issuance of invoices and payment of bills.
- Reconcile bank and credit card accounts monthly.
- Track and report on the status of the Emergency & Bug Project Reserve Fund.
- Coordinate the annual financial review or audit as required by the Bylaws.
- Develop and maintain financial policies (e.g., Reserves Policy, Procurement Policy, Expense Reimbursement Policy).

**Time Commitment:** 5–10 hours per month, with increased time during budget season and year-end closing.

**Reporting Structure:** Reports directly to the Executive (Board of Directors).

### 4.2 Communication Squad

**Date of Founding:** October 2025  
**Squad Lead:** [To be appointed by Executive]

**Purpose:** To ensure clear, consistent, and transparent communication between the Executive, the membership, and the public. The Communication Squad manages official club communications and maintains the club's public presence.

**Typical Composition:** 3–5 members with skills in writing, graphic design, social media, or website management.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Draft and distribute the official newsletter (at least weekly during the operating season).
- Maintain and grow the official mailing list.
- Manage the club's official Facebook page and other social media accounts.
- Respond to public inquiries and direct messages.
- Ensure all official communications (emails, website posts, notices) are clear, professional, and aligned with the club's values.
- Assist the Executive in drafting announcements for major decisions or proposals.
- Maintain a content calendar for public-facing communications.
- Coordinate with other Squads to highlight their work and achievements.

**Time Commitment:** 3–8 hours per month, depending on the season and upcoming events.

**Reporting Structure:** Reports to the Executive.

---

### 4.3 FunSquad

**Date of Founding:** January 2026  
**Squad Lead:** [To be appointed by Executive]

**Purpose:** To organize and host social activities and community-building events that bring members together and foster a vibrant club culture.

**Typical Composition:** 4–8 members with an interest in event planning, social gatherings, and community engagement. No specific experience required—just enthusiasm!

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Brainstorm and propose social activities for the membership (e.g., regattas, BBQs, fundraisers, mixers).
- Use the **DRMC Activity Proposal & Execution Template** to plan and document all events.
- Coordinate event logistics, including dates, locations, permits, budgets, and volunteer staffing.
- Work with the Communication Squad to promote events to members and the public.
- Ensure all events comply with safety regulations and the club's Code of Conduct.
- Gather feedback after events and complete the Post-Activity Report to capture lessons learned.
- Manage any alcohol service at events in accordance with club policy and provincial regulations.

**Time Commitment:** Highly variable, depending on the number and scale of events planned. Ranges from 2–10 hours per month.

**Reporting Structure:** Reports to the Executive.

### 4.4 SaveTheMarina Squad

**Date of Founding:** November 2025  
**Squad Lead:** [To be appointed by Executive]

**Purpose:** To develop and pursue a bold, long-term vision for the DRMC's future, with a focus on securing external grant funding for major capital projects.

**Typical Composition:** 4–8 members, including individuals with experience in grant writing, project management, government relations, or strategic planning. Dreamers and enthusiasts are also welcome!

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Develop a comprehensive 5-year strategic vision for the marina, including major projects like dredging, breakwater upgrades, and new public amenities.
- Research and identify federal, provincial, and private grant opportunities that align with the club's vision (e.g., harbour safety, climate resilience, Indigenous reconciliation, tourism).
- Draft and submit grant proposals, working with professional grant writers as needed.
- Maintain relationships with key stakeholders, including government officials, partner organizations (e.g., Heron Bay, Eel River Bar First Nation), and local enterprises.
- Track the progress of grant applications and report to the Executive on opportunities and outcomes.
- Coordinate with the FinOps Squad to ensure grant-funded projects have proper financial oversight.

**Time Commitment:** 5–15 hours per month, with peaks during grant application deadlines.

**Reporting Structure:** Reports to the Executive.

### 4.5 Dock Squad

**Date of Founding:** Implied by newsletters (active for winterization and repairs)
**Squad Lead:** [Typically Harbour Master or designate]

**Purpose:** To ensure the safety, functionality, and good repair of all docking facilities, including the docks, finger piers, and associated infrastructure.

**Typical Composition:** 4–8 members with an interest in hands-on maintenance, carpentry, or marine infrastructure. Willingness to perform physical work is essential.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Assist with the annual Spring Setup (installing and connecting docks) and Fall Removal (hauling and storing docks).
- Perform routine inspections of docks, cleats, ladders, and utility connections, reporting any damage or hazards to the Harbour Master.
- Conduct minor repairs as authorized by the Executive or Harbour Master.
- Assist with the installation and maintenance of dock-related equipment (e.g., ladders, signage, fenders).
- Support the Harbour Master with special projects, such as dock reconfiguration or upgrades.
- Ensure the dock area is clean, tidy, and free of hazards.

**Time Commitment:** Highly seasonal. 10–20 hours during Spring Setup and Fall Removal, plus occasional hours throughout the season for inspections and minor repairs.

**Reporting Structure:** Reports to the Harbour Master (or directly to the Executive in the Harbour Master's absence).

## 5. General Member (Volunteer-at-Large)

Not every member needs to join a formal Squad. Members who wish to contribute on an ad-hoc basis can be called upon by any Squad Lead to assist with specific tasks or events.

**How it Works:**
- Squad Leads may put out a call for volunteers for specific, time-limited tasks (e.g., helping with a BBQ, assisting with dock installation, painting a shed).
- Interested members can sign up for these tasks without making a long-term commitment.
- All ad-hoc volunteers are covered by the same safety and conduct rules as Squad members.

**Time Commitment:** As available and interested.
