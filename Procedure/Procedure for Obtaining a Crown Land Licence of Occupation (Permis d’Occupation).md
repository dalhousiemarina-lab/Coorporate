# Procedure for Obtaining a Crown Land Licence of Occupation (Permis d’Occupation)

**Issuing Authority:** Department of Natural Resources and Energy Development (DNRED), New Brunswick

---

## 1. Overview

This document outlines the standard procedure for obtaining a Licence of Occupation (Permis d’occupation) for Crown Land in New Brunswick, Canada. This permit grants legal authorization to occupy and use Crown land on a temporary and non-exclusive basis for purposes such as access roads, utility poles, community events, and similar activities.

| Field | Details |
|-------|--------|
| **Permit Type** | Licence of Occupation (Permis d’occupation) |
| **Jurisdiction** | Crown Land, Province of New Brunswick |
| **Governing Authority** | Department of Natural Resources and Energy Development (DNRED) |
| **Application Form** | CSS-FOL-SNB-60-0043 |
| **Processing Time** | 6 to 12 weeks (may vary based on complexity) |

---

## 2. Application Process

The process consists of five main steps.

### Step 1: Obtain and Complete the Application Form

- **Obtain the Form:** Download or request the *Licence of Occupation Application Form* (CSS-FOL-SNB-60-0043) from the Government of New Brunswick.
- **Provide Required Information:**
  - Applicant contact information (name, address, phone)
  - Applicant status (individual, company, non-profit, etc.)
  - Detailed description of intended use (e.g., road construction, community event, utility pole installation)
  - Approximate period the licence will be required (years, months, days)

### Step 2: Prepare Required Documents and Plans

The following documents must be attached to your application:

| Document | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Detailed Site Plan (Appendix A)** | A sketch or plan showing the location of proposed improvements. Must include: the Property Identification Number (PID) of the subject land, area (hectares) and dimensions (length/width in metres) of the land involved, PIDs of adjacent lands, and location of the installation relative to property boundaries. |
| **Detailed Activity Description** | A clear description of construction, activities, equipment, and materials to be used. |
| **Potential Effects Statement** | A description of all potential effects on adjacent landowners, the environment, or the local area. |
| **Maps and Coordinates** | Include a map and/or aerial photo (available online at GeoNB). For certain projects (e.g., sugar maple corridors, road construction), GPS coordinates or data must be included. |

### Step 3: Calculate and Submit Application Fees

Application fees are **non-refundable** and include HST. Payment may be made by money order, cheque (payable to the **Minister of Finance**), Visa, or Mastercard.

| Type of Use | Application Fee (HST included) |
|-------------|-------------------------------|
| Construct a road | $230.00 |
| Community event | $86.25 |
| Utility pole and anchor | $862.50 + $92.00 per pole |
| All other applications | $862.50 |

### Step 4: Submit the Complete Application Package

Submit your completed application form, all required attachments, and the application fee to:

| Mailing Address | Courier Address (FedEx, Purolator, etc.) |
|-----------------|------------------------------------------|
| Department of Natural Resources and Energy Development<br>Land Use Application Service Centre<br>P.O. Box 6000<br>Fredericton, NB<br>E3B 5H1 | Department of Natural Resources and Energy Development<br>Land Use Application Service Centre<br>1350 Regent St.<br>Fredericton, NB<br>E3C 2G6 |

- **Processing Time:** The review process typically takes **6 to 12 weeks**. Complex applications may require additional time.

### Step 5: Fulfill Approval Requirements

If your application is approved, you will be notified of additional requirements, which may include:

- **Annual Rent:** Licences issued for a duration exceeding **6 months** are subject to an annual rent fee.
- **Liability Insurance:** Minimum **$5,000,000** in liability insurance is required. The policy must list:

  > *“Her Majesty the Queen in Right of the Province of New Brunswick as represented by the Minister of Natural Resources and Energy Development”*

  as an **additional insured**.

- **Other Conditions:** Additional terms and conditions may apply depending on the nature and location of the activity.

---

## 3. Important Notes

- The licence grants **temporary and non-exclusive** use of Crown land.
- All fees are non-refundable, regardless of the application outcome.
- For assistance or to obtain the application form, visit the official DNRED Crown Lands page or contact the Land Use Application Service Centre directly.
