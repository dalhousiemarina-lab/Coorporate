## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this procedure is to ensure that all accidents, near‑misses, property damage, safety concerns, and financial irregularities at the Dalhousie Regional Marina Club (DRMC) are consistently documented, investigated, and addressed. A formal incident reporting process:

- Improves safety for members, guests, and volunteers.
- Reduces the club's liability by demonstrating due diligence.
- Supports insurance claims and helps manage premium increases.
- Creates a culture of continuous improvement and learning.
- Provides a clear paper trail for legal, regulatory, and financial compliance.

This procedure is required by the DRMC Bylaws (Article XX.2) and supplements the Code of Conduct (Section 1.2), which mandates that members report incidents promptly.

---

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to:

- All members, guests, volunteers, and contractors on DRMC property.
- All incidents occurring on DRMC‑owned or leased property, including docks, campground, parking areas, clubhouse, and storage areas.
- Incidents involving DRMC assets, even if they occur elsewhere (e.g., a member's boat damaged while in the club's care).

---

## 3. Definitions

- **Incident:** Any unplanned event that results in or has the potential to result in injury, illness, property damage, environmental harm, financial loss, or a near‑miss.
- **Near‑Miss:** An event that could have resulted in injury, illness, or damage but did not, due to chance or timely intervention.
- **Serious Incident:** An incident involving:
  - Fatality or serious personal injury requiring professional medical attention.
  - Significant property damage (estimated repair cost >$5,000).
  - Environmental spill (fuel, oil, sewage) requiring remediation.
  - Fire, explosion, or sinking.
  - Allegation of theft, fraud, or financial irregularity.
- **Incident Report Form:** The official form (electronic or paper) used to document an incident.
- **Incident Log:** A secure, centralized record of all reported incidents.

---

## 4. Who Should Report?

- **Any person** witnessing or involved in an incident should report it immediately.
- **Members** are responsible for reporting incidents involving their own vessel, site, or guests.
- **Volunteers and Squad members** must report any hazards, damages, or safety concerns they observe during their activities.
- **The Harbour Master** is responsible for reporting incidents related to dock operations, moorage, or the water area.
- **The Executive** must ensure that all serious incidents are properly investigated and that required external notifications (e.g., to insurers, regulators) are made.

---

## 5. Immediate Response (Immediate Action Required)

In the event of an incident, the first priority is always the safety of individuals.

### 5.1 For Medical Emergencies, Fires, or Active Threats
1.  **Call 911** immediately if there is a serious injury, fire, or immediate danger.
2.  **Administer first aid** if trained and it is safe to do so.
3.  **Alert others** in the area to the danger.
4.  **Notify the Harbour Master** or a member of the Executive as soon as possible.

### 5.2 For Environmental Spills (Fuel, Oil, Sewage)
1.  **Contain the spill** if it is safe to do so, using the spill kits located [insert locations, e.g., at the fuel dock, in the maintenance shed].
2.  **Do not wash the spill into the water.**
3.  **Notify the Harbour Master** immediately.
4.  The Harbour Master or Executive will contact the appropriate environmental authorities if required (e.g., Canadian Coast Guard spill reporting line).

### 5.3 For Property Damage or Theft
1.  **Secure the area** to prevent further damage or injury.
2.  **Document the scene** with photos and notes (see Section 6).
3.  **Notify the Harbour Master** or a member of the Executive.
4.  **Do not dispose of damaged property** until authorized by the Executive or an insurer.

## 6. Reporting Procedure (Within 24 Hours)

All incidents, including near‑misses, must be formally reported using the **DRMC Incident Report Form** within 24 hours of the event.

### Step 1: Complete the Incident Report Form
The form can be obtained:
- On the member portal (digital fillable PDF).
- From the Harbour Master (paper copy).
- By email request to [incident@dalhousiemarina.ca](mailto:incident@dalhousiemarina.ca).

The form requires the following information:
- **Date, time, and location** of the incident.
- **Name and contact information** of the person reporting.
- **Names and contact information** of any witnesses.
- **Detailed description** of what happened (who, what, where, when, why, how).
- **Immediate actions taken** (e.g., first aid, spill contained, 911 called).
- **Description of any injuries or damage** (include photos if possible).
- **Whether external authorities** (police, fire, ambulance) were contacted.

### Step 2: Submit the Form
- **Email** the completed form to [incident@dalhousiemarina.ca](mailto:incident@dalhousiemarina.ca).
- **Or deliver** a paper copy to the Harbour Master or a member of the Executive.

### Step 3: Acknowledge Receipt
The Harbour Master or Executive designee shall acknowledge receipt of the incident report within 24 hours.

## 7. Investigation Procedure

### 7.1 Who Investigates?
- **Minor Incidents** (no injury, minor damage): The Harbour Master or relevant Squad Lead shall conduct a brief investigation and document findings.
- **Serious Incidents** (as defined in Section 3): The Executive shall form a small incident review team, which may include the Harbour Master, a member of the FinOps Squad (for financial impacts), and an independent member not involved in the incident.

### 7.2 Investigation Steps
1.  **Gather Facts:** Review the incident report, interview witnesses, inspect the scene, and collect any physical evidence or photos.
2.  **Determine Root Cause:** Identify the underlying factors that led to the incident (e.g., equipment failure, human error, lack of training, unsafe conditions).
3.  **Identify Contributing Factors:** Note any conditions that made the incident worse or increased the risk.
4.  **Document Findings:** Prepare a written investigation summary, including conclusions and recommendations.

### 7.3 Timeline
- Investigations for minor incidents should be completed within **7 days**.
- Investigations for serious incidents should be completed within **30 days**, unless extenuating circumstances require more time.

## 8. Reporting and Follow-Up

### 8.1 Internal Reporting
- **Incident Log:** The Harbour Master or Executive designee shall maintain a secure, centralized Incident Log. This log shall include:
  - Date of incident.
  - Type of incident (injury, damage, near‑miss, environmental, financial).
  - Brief description.
  - Status of investigation (open, closed, pending).
  - Key recommendations.
- **Quarterly Summary:** A anonymized summary of incidents (excluding personal health information and active legal matters) shall be presented to the Executive at each quarterly meeting and may be shared with members to promote safety awareness.

### 8.2 External Reporting
The Executive is responsible for notifying external parties as required:
- **Insurance Provider:** All serious incidents involving property damage, theft, or potential liability must be reported to the club's insurer within the time frame specified in the policy (typically within 24–48 hours). Failure to report promptly can void coverage.
- **WorkSafeNB:** Any injury to a volunteer or worker that requires medical attention beyond first aid must be reported to WorkSafeNB in accordance with provincial regulations.
- **Police:** Theft, vandalism, or any criminal activity must be reported to the local police.
- **Environmental Authorities:** Fuel or chemical spills must be reported to the appropriate federal or provincial authority (e.g., Canadian Coast Guard Spill Reporting Line: 1-800-565-1633).

### 8.3 Member Notification
- For incidents that affect the safety or operations of the marina (e.g., a fuel spill, major dock damage, security breach), the Communication Squad shall issue a timely update to members via the newsletter.
- Member notifications shall be factual and avoid speculation.

## 9. Lessons Learned and Continuous Improvement

The goal of incident reporting is not just to document what went wrong, but to prevent it from happening again.

- After each investigation, the Executive or relevant Squad shall review the recommendations and implement appropriate corrective actions (e.g., repairing equipment, updating procedures, providing training).
- Significant findings and lessons learned shall be incorporated into:
  - Updates to the Standing Operating Procedures (SOPs).
  - Training materials for volunteers and Squad members.
  - Risk assessments for future projects and activities.
- Annually, the Executive shall review the Incident Log to identify patterns and recommend systemic improvements.

---

## 10. Confidentiality

- Incident reports, investigation files, and the Incident Log contain sensitive information. Access shall be restricted to:
  - The Executive.
  - The Harbour Master (for incidents within their scope).
  - The FinOps Squad (for incidents with financial or insurance implications).
  - Legal counsel or insurers as required.
- Personal health information shall be handled in accordance with applicable privacy laws (PIPEDA).
- Information from incident reports shall not be shared publicly in a way that could identify individuals, unless required by law.

---

## 11. Non-Retaliation Policy

The DRMC is committed to a culture of safety and transparency. No member, volunteer, or guest shall be retaliated against for reporting an incident in good faith. Retaliation against a good‑faith reporter is a violation of the Code of Conduct and may result in disciplinary action.
