# DRMC Membership & Voting Made Simple

**Document Version: 3.1 – Effective Date: February 28, 2026**

This document is a plain-language summary of your membership rights. For the complete legal rules, please refer to the official DRMC Bylaws (Article IV).

### The Core Idea: The "Voting Unit"

At DRMC, the right to vote belongs to the **household**, not just one person. We call this a **Voting Unit**.

Think of it as one "membership account" for your family. Each Unit has the potential to cast up to **two votes** on important matters.

- **Who holds the vote?** YOU decide. Each Unit must tell the Secretary who their **Primary Voter** is (and optionally, a **Secondary Voter** to fill in if the Primary can't make it). Only one person from your Unit votes at a time.

---

### Class A Voting Units: Your Base Vote

If your family holds a seasonal agreement for a boat slip, a campsite, or both, you are a **Class A Voting Unit**. Every Class A Unit gets **one (1) Base Vote**.

| Membership Type         | What You Hold                       | Your Base Vote |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------------------- | -------------- |
| **Berth Holder**        | Seasonal boat slip agreement        | **1 Vote**     |
| **Seasonal Camper**     | Seasonal campsite agreement         | **1 Vote**     |
| **Dual Member**         | Both a boat slip AND a campsite     | **1 Vote**     |

**The Rule is Simple:** One Unit = One Base Vote.

---

### The Volunteer Bonus Vote: Earn a Second Vote

We believe those who volunteer to help the marina should have an extra say. That's why every Class A Voting Unit can earn a **second vote**.

- **The Reward:** One (1) additional **Bonus Vote** for your Unit.
- **How to Earn It:** At least one adult in your Unit completes **20 verified volunteer hours** in the last 12 months.
- **The Result:** Your Unit now has **2 total votes**.

### How the "Unit Vote" Works in Practice

This is the most important part. Because the vote belongs to the Unit, you have flexibility:

1.  **Your One Base Vote:** You decide who in your family casts it. Is it you? Your partner? Your adult child living at home? It's your Unit's choice.
2.  **Your Bonus Vote:** This is a *second, separate vote* for your Unit. You can choose the **same person** to cast both votes, OR you can choose a **different person** to cast the bonus vote.

**Example:**
- The *Smith Family Unit* earns a Bonus Vote for their volunteering.
- They tell the Secretary that **Alex** is the Primary Voter (casting the Base Vote).
- They also tell the Secretary that **Jordan** is the designated voter for the Bonus Vote.
- At a meeting, Alex casts the Base Vote, and Jordan casts the Bonus Vote. The Smith family has cast both of their votes.

**The only rule:** You must tell the Secretary in writing who your designated voters are. This keeps our records clear and your votes secure.

---

### Non-Voting Access Categories

These members enjoy our facilities and community but do not have voting rights, as they do not hold a seasonal slip or site agreement.

- **Associate Member:** Social member with no boat slip or campsite.
- **Overnight Guest / Transient:** Short-term visitor (less than 30 days).

---

### Quick Summary

- **Membership is a "Voting Unit" (your household).**
- **Class A Units** (Berth, Camp, Dual) get **1 Base Vote**.
- **Any Class A Unit** can earn **1 Bonus Vote** (with 20 volunteer hours).
- **YOU choose** who in your Unit casts the Base Vote and who casts the Bonus Vote (they can be the same person or two different people).
- Maximum votes per Unit on any single motion: **2**.

*For complete details on voter designation, volunteer hour tracking, or other membership rules, refer to the DRMC Bylaws, Article IV.*
