## Purpose

The purpose of these Standing Operating Procedures (SOPs) is to standardize the day‑to‑day execution of duties for specific officer roles and recurring tasks within the Dalhousie Regional Marina Club (DRMC). While the Bylaws (Article XII) define the existence and general responsibilities of officers, these SOPs provide step‑by‑step instructions to ensure consistency, preserve institutional knowledge, and facilitate smooth transitions when roles change hands.

These procedures are supplementary to the Bylaws and must be followed by all officers and volunteers performing the duties described herein.

---

## 1. Harbour Master SOP

### 1.1 Moorage Assignment Protocol
- **New Member Inquiries:**  
  - Direct all inquiries to the Harbour Master via email (harbourmaster@drmc.ca) or the member portal.  
  - Maintain a digital log of inquiries, including date, name, vessel details, and requested slip/site type.  
  - Within 3 business days, acknowledge receipt and provide information on current availability and waitlist procedures (see Section 1.2).

- **Assigning Available Moorage:**  
  - When a slip or site becomes vacant, review the waitlist (ordered by application date) and offer it to the next eligible Voting Unit.  
  - Contact the member by email and phone; allow 7 calendar days for a response.  
  - If the member declines or does not respond, move to the next person on the list and document the offer.  
  - Once accepted, prepare the moorage agreement and collect the required fees before issuing access credentials.

- **Temporary/Transient Moorage:**  
  - Members seeking temporary changes must submit a request at least 14 days in advance.  
  - Approve only if no permanent member is displaced and the requested period does not exceed 30 consecutive days.  
  - Maintain a calendar of temporary assignments.

### 1.2 Waitlist Management
- The waitlist shall be maintained in a secure spreadsheet or database, sorted by the date and time of application.  
- Each application must include: member name, Voting Unit ID, vessel details, preferred slip size, and contact information.  
- Annually, in January, the Harbour Master shall contact all waitlisted members to confirm their continued interest. Non‑response after two attempts results in removal from the list.  
- The waitlist is confidential and shared only with the Executive as needed.

### 1.3 Dock Inspection Checklist
- **Weekly Inspections:**  
  - Walk all docks and common areas, noting any damage, hazards, or maintenance needs.  
  - Use the digital inspection form (template available in the DRMC drive).  
  - Report urgent safety issues (e.g., broken cleats, loose boards, electrical hazards) immediately to the Facilities Squad and the Executive.

- **Monthly Inspections:**  
  - Check fire extinguishers, first aid kits, and spill kits for expiration and completeness.  
  - Verify that gate locks and security fobs are functioning.  
  - Inspect signage for legibility and visibility.

- **Seasonal/Annual Inspections:**  
  - Before and after winter, conduct a comprehensive structural inspection of all docks, pilings, and utility connections.  
  - Coordinate with the Projects Squad for any major repairs identified.

## 2. Treasurer SOP

### 2.1 Accounts Payable / Receivable Process
- **Invoicing Members:**  
  - Generate annual membership and moorage invoices by January 15 each year, using the current fee schedule approved by the membership.  
  - Send invoices via email with a clear payment deadline (usually March 1).  
  - Record all payments in the accounting software within 3 business days.

- **Paying Bills:**  
  - Receive and verify invoices for goods/services against purchase orders or approvals.  
  - Obtain Executive approval for payments exceeding $500 (as per Bylaws XII.7).  
  - Schedule payments to avoid late fees; use electronic transfers where possible.  
  - Maintain a digital file of all paid invoices and receipts.

### 2.2 Bank Reconciliation Timeline
- Reconcile all bank and credit card accounts monthly, within 15 days of receiving the statement.  
- Investigate and resolve any discrepancies immediately, involving another Executive member if needed.  
- Prepare a brief reconciliation summary for the Executive at each monthly meeting.

### 2.3 Financial Reporting Schedule
- **Monthly:** Provide a cash flow summary and updated budget vs. actuals to the Executive.  
- **Quarterly:** Present a more detailed financial report, including reserve fund balances, at the quarterly general meeting (or Executive meeting if no general meeting).  
- **Annually:** Coordinate with the Governance & Nominating Committee to prepare the year‑end financial statements for the AGM and the external review (if required).  
- Ensure all reports are filed in the club’s official records and shared with members per Bylaws Article VI.

### 2.4 Reserve Fund Tracking
- Maintain a separate ledger for the capital reserve fund.  
- Track contributions and withdrawals, ensuring they comply with the (future) Reserves & Investment Policy.  
- Report reserve balances quarterly.

## 3. Secretary SOP

### 3.1 Meeting Agenda Preparation
- **Executive Meetings:**  
  - Solicit agenda items from all Executive members at least 5 business days before the meeting.  
  - Draft the agenda, including reports from officers, old business, new business, and action items from previous meetings.  
  - Distribute the agenda and any supporting documents at least 48 hours in advance.

- **General Membership Meetings (including AGM):**  
  - Follow the same process but also include time for member questions and any required voting items.  
  - Ensure notice of the meeting is sent to all Voting Units as required by the Bylaws (minimum 14 days for AGM, 7 days for special meetings).

### 3.2 Minute-Taking Standards
- Minutes must record: date, time, location, attendees (noting quorum), and the substance of discussions.  
- Motions must be recorded verbatim, including the mover, seconder, and vote count (if any).  
- Avoid personal opinions or paraphrasing; focus on decisions and action items.  
- Within 7 days, circulate draft minutes to the Executive for review, then finalize and store in the official minutes archive.

### 3.3 Official Correspondence Log
- Maintain a log (spreadsheet or database) of all official correspondence sent or received by the club, including:
  - Date
  - Sender/recipient
  - Subject
  - Action taken (if any)
- This includes emails from the general inbox, letters, and formal notices.
- Ensure all correspondence requiring a response is acknowledged within 5 business days.

### 3.4 Record Keeping
- Organize digital and physical records according to the (future) Document Retention Policy.  
- Key records to maintain:  
  - Incorporation documents (permanent)  
  - Bylaws and amendments (permanent)  
  - Meeting minutes (permanent)  
  - Financial records (7 years)  
  - Membership agreements (duration of membership + 1 year)  
- Regularly back up digital records to a secure cloud service.

## 4. Amendments and Review

These SOPs may be amended by the Executive as needed, provided the changes are consistent with the Bylaws. Any substantive changes should be communicated to the affected officers and noted in the next Executive meeting minutes. A full review of all SOPs shall occur annually in the first quarter.
