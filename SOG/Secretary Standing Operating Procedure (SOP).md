# Secretary Standing Operating Procedure (SOP)

**Review Cycle:** Annually (or as needed)

## Purpose

The purpose of this Standing Operating Procedure (SOP) is to standardize the day‑to‑day execution of duties for the Secretary role within the Dalhousie Regional Marina Club (DRMC). This document provides step‑by‑step instructions to ensure consistency, preserve institutional knowledge, and facilitate smooth transitions when the role changes hands.

This procedure is supplementary to the Bylaws and must be followed by the Secretary and any volunteers performing the duties described herein.

---

## 1. Meeting Agenda Preparation

### 1.1 Executive Meetings
- Solicit agenda items from all Executive members at least 5 business days before the meeting.
- Draft the agenda, including reports from officers, old business, new business, and action items from previous meetings.
- Distribute the agenda and any supporting documents at least 48 hours in advance.

### 1.2 General Membership Meetings (including AGM)
- Follow the same process but also include time for member questions and any required voting items.
- Ensure notice of the meeting is sent to all Voting Units as required by the Bylaws (minimum 14 days for AGM, 7 days for special meetings).

---

## 2. Minute-Taking Standards

- Minutes must record: date, time, location, attendees (noting quorum), and the substance of discussions.
- Motions must be recorded verbatim, including the mover, seconder, and vote count (if any).
- Avoid personal opinions or paraphrasing; focus on decisions and action items.
- Within 7 days, circulate draft minutes to the Executive for review, then finalize and store in the official minutes archive.

---

## 3. Official Correspondence Log

- Maintain a log (spreadsheet or database) of all official correspondence sent or received by the club, including:
  - Date
  - Sender/recipient
  - Subject
  - Action taken (if any)
- This includes emails from the general inbox, letters, and formal notices.
- Ensure all correspondence requiring a response is acknowledged within 5 business days.

---

## 4. Record Keeping

- Organize digital and physical records according to the (future) Document Retention Policy.
- Key records to maintain:
  - Incorporation documents (permanent)
  - Bylaws and amendments (permanent)
  - Meeting minutes (permanent)
  - Financial records (7 years)
  - Membership agreements (duration of membership + 1 year)
- Regularly back up digital records to a secure cloud service.

---

## 5. Amendments and Review

This SOP may be amended by the Executive as needed, provided the changes are consistent with the Bylaws. Any substantive changes should be communicated to the Secretary and noted in the next Executive meeting minutes. A full review of this SOP shall occur annually in the first quarter.
