# Commodore (President) Standing Operating Procedure (SOP)

**Document Version:** 1.0 (Draft) – March 2026  
**Status:** Work in Progress – Not yet approved by the Executive  
**Effective Date:** [To be determined upon approval]  
**Review Cycle:** Annually (or as needed)

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this Standing Operating Procedure (SOP) is to standardize the day‑to‑day execution of duties for the Commodore (President) role within the Dalhousie Regional Marina Club (DRMC). This document provides step‑by‑step instructions to ensure consistency, preserve institutional knowledge, and facilitate smooth transitions when the role changes hands.

This procedure is supplementary to the Bylaws and must be followed by the Commodore and any volunteers performing the duties described herein.

---

## 1. Leadership and Oversight

### 1.1 Executive Leadership
- Preside over all Executive meetings and General Membership meetings, including the Annual General Meeting (AGM).
- Set the meeting tone, ensure adherence to the agenda, and maintain order in accordance with the Bylaws and any adopted rules of order.
- Act as the primary liaison between the Executive and the general membership, ensuring open communication and transparency.

### 1.2 Officer Support and Accountability
- Meet individually with each Executive officer at least quarterly to discuss progress, challenges, and resource needs.
- Monitor the performance of officer duties against their respective SOPs and address any gaps or concerns promptly.
- Facilitate resolution of conflicts between officers or between officers and members, escalating to the Executive only when necessary.

### 1.3 Strategic Direction
- Lead the development and periodic review of the club's strategic plan, in consultation with the Executive and membership.
- Ensure that all club activities, projects, and expenditures align with the strategic priorities approved by the membership.
- Identify emerging opportunities or risks and bring them to the Executive for discussion.

---

## 2. Meeting Management

### 2.1 Meeting Preparation
- Review the draft agenda prepared by the Secretary at least 48 hours before any Executive or General Meeting; suggest adjustments as needed to ensure critical items are addressed.
- Confirm that all required reports (e.g., Treasurer's financial summary, Harbour Master's update) have been submitted and are ready for presentation.
- For General Meetings, ensure that proper notice has been given per Bylaws requirements.

### 2.2 Conducting Meetings
- Start meetings on time and confirm quorum before proceeding.
- Recognize members who wish to speak, ensuring equitable opportunity for input.
- State motions clearly, confirm seconders, and call for votes in accordance with the Bylaws.
- Summarize action items and assignments before adjourning.

### 2.3 Post-Meeting Follow-Up
- Review draft minutes provided by the Secretary within 7 days, verifying accuracy of decisions and action items.
- Follow up with officers on action items assigned during the meeting to ensure timely progress.

---

## 3. External Representation

### 3.1 Community and Government Relations
- Serve as the primary representative of DRMC to external organizations, including municipal government, marina associations, and community groups.
- Maintain a log of external meetings, contacts, and commitments (see Section 5.2).
- Report significant external developments to the Executive in a timely manner.

### 3.2 Media and Public Communications
- Act as the official spokesperson for the club unless otherwise delegated.
- Coordinate with the Executive before issuing public statements on controversial or sensitive matters.
- Review and approve major communications (e.g., newsletters, press releases) prior to distribution.

### 3.3 Stakeholder Engagement
- Foster positive relationships with neighboring marinas, tenants, and regulatory bodies.
- Attend relevant community events and meetings to promote DRMC and advocate for member interests.

---

## 4. Transition and Succession Planning

### 4.1 Knowledge Transfer
- Maintain a Commodore's handover folder containing key contacts, ongoing projects, strategic priorities, and historical context.
- Update this folder throughout the term and review it with the incoming Commodore following each election.

### 4.2 Officer Development
- Identify potential future leaders within the membership and encourage their participation in committees or volunteer roles.
- Support the Governance & Nominating Committee in recruiting qualified candidates for Executive positions.

---

## 5. Official Records and Correspondence

### 5.1 Executive Records
- Maintain a personal file of significant decisions, meeting notes, and correspondence related to the Commodore role.
- Ensure that all official Executive decisions are properly documented in meeting minutes.

### 5.2 External Contact Log
- Maintain a log of external contacts and meetings, including:
  - Date
  - Organization/individual
  - Purpose of contact
  - Follow-up actions required
- Share relevant entries with the Secretary for inclusion in the official correspondence log (as per Secretary SOP Section 3).

---

## 6. Amendments and Review

This SOP may be amended by the Executive as needed, provided the changes are consistent with the Bylaws. Any substantive changes should be communicated to the Commodore and noted in the next Executive meeting minutes. A full review of this SOP shall occur annually in the first quarter.
