## Purpose

The purpose of this Standing Operating Procedure (SOP) is to standardize the day‑to‑day execution of duties for the Treasurer role within the Dalhousie Regional Marina Club (DRMC). This document provides step‑by‑step instructions to ensure consistency, preserve institutional knowledge, and facilitate smooth transitions when the role changes hands.

This procedure is supplementary to the Bylaws and must be followed by the Treasurer and any volunteers performing the duties described herein.

---

## 1. Accounts Payable / Receivable Process

### 1.1 Invoicing Members
- Generate annual membership and moorage invoices by January 15 each year, using the current fee schedule approved by the membership.
- Send invoices via email with a clear payment deadline (usually March 1).
- Record all payments in the accounting software within 3 business days.

### 1.2 Paying Bills
- Receive and verify invoices for goods/services against purchase orders or approvals.
- Obtain Executive approval for payments exceeding $500 (as per Bylaws XII.7).
- Schedule payments to avoid late fees; use electronic transfers where possible.
- Maintain a digital file of all paid invoices and receipts.

---

## 2. Bank Reconciliation Timeline

- Reconcile all bank and credit card accounts monthly, within 15 days of receiving the statement.
- Investigate and resolve any discrepancies immediately, involving another Executive member if needed.
- Prepare a brief reconciliation summary for the Executive at each monthly meeting.

---

## 3. Financial Reporting Schedule

- **Monthly:** Provide a cash flow summary and updated budget vs. actuals to the Executive.
- **Quarterly:** Present a more detailed financial report, including reserve fund balances, at the quarterly general meeting (or Executive meeting if no general meeting).
- **Annually:** Coordinate with the Governance & Nominating Committee to prepare the year‑end financial statements for the AGM and the external review (if required).
- Ensure all reports are filed in the club's official records and shared with members per Bylaws Article VI.

---

## 4. Reserve Fund Tracking

- Maintain a separate ledger for the capital reserve fund.
- Track contributions and withdrawals, ensuring they comply with the (future) Reserves & Investment Policy.
- Report reserve balances quarterly.

---

## 5. Amendments and Review

This SOP may be amended by the Executive as needed, provided the changes are consistent with the Bylaws. Any substantive changes should be communicated to the Treasurer and noted in the next Executive meeting minutes. A full review of this SOP shall occur annually in the first quarter.
