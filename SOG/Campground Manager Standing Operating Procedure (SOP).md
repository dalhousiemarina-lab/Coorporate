# Campground Manager Standing Operating Procedure (SOP)

**Document Version:** 1.0 (Draft) – March 2026  
**Status:** Work in Progress – Not yet approved by the Executive  
**Effective Date:** [To be determined upon approval]  
**Review Cycle:** Annually (or as needed)

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this Standing Operating Procedure (SOP) is to standardize the day‑to‑day execution of duties for the Campground Manager role within the Dalhousie Regional Marina Club (DRMC). This document provides step‑by‑step instructions to ensure consistency, preserve institutional knowledge, and facilitate smooth transitions when the role changes hands.

This procedure is supplementary to the Bylaws and must be followed by the Campground Manager and any volunteers performing the duties described herein.

---

## 1. Campground Assignment Protocol

### 1.1 New Camper Inquiries
- Direct all inquiries to the Campground Manager via email (campground@drmc.ca) or the member portal.
- Maintain a digital log of inquiries, including date, name, party size, requested site type, and preferred dates.
- Within 3 business days, acknowledge receipt and provide information on current availability and booking procedures (see Section 2).

### 1.2 Assigning Available Sites
- When a site becomes available, review the waitlist (if applicable) or accept bookings on a first-come, first-served basis per club policy.
- For seasonal or long-term assignments, prioritize members in good standing per the Bylaws.
- Contact the member by email and phone; allow 7 calendar days for a response.
- If the member declines or does not respond, move to the next person on the list (if applicable) and document the offer.
- Once accepted, prepare the camping agreement and collect the required fees before issuing access credentials or site assignment.

### 1.3 Temporary/Transient Camping
- Members seeking temporary camping arrangements must submit a request at least 14 days in advance.
- Approve only if no permanent camper is displaced and the requested period does not exceed 30 consecutive days.
- Maintain a calendar of temporary assignments and transient bookings.

---

## 2. Waitlist Management

- If a waitlist is maintained for seasonal or high-demand sites, it shall be kept in a secure spreadsheet or database, sorted by the date and time of application.
- Each application must include: member name, Voting Unit ID, party size, preferred site type, and contact information.
- Annually, in January, the Campground Manager shall contact all waitlisted members to confirm their continued interest. Non‑response after two attempts results in removal from the list.
- The waitlist is confidential and shared only with the Executive as needed.

---

## 3. Booking and Reservation System

### 3.1 Reservation Calendar
- Maintain an up-to-date reservation calendar (digital or physical) showing all site assignments, temporary bookings, and maintenance closures.
- Update the calendar immediately upon confirming new bookings or cancellations.
- Ensure the calendar is accessible to other officers (e.g., Harbour Master, Executive) as needed for coordination.

### 3.2 Cancellation Policy
- Follow the club's approved cancellation policy (to be developed or referenced from Facility Regulations).
- Process any refunds in coordination with the Treasurer within 10 business days of cancellation.
- Document cancellations and any associated fees or credits for member accounts.

### 3.3 Check-In and Check-Out Procedures
- Establish a clear check-in process, including:
  - Verification of booking and payment
  - Issuance of access credentials (if applicable)
  - Provision of site rules and emergency information
- Establish a check-out process, including:
  - Site inspection for damage or excessive cleaning needs
  - Return of access credentials
  - Reporting any issues to the Facilities Squad

---

## 4. Site Inspection and Maintenance

### 4.1 Weekly Inspections
- Walk all campground sites and common areas (washrooms, shower facilities, picnic areas, fire pits) weekly during the operating season.
- Note any damage, hazards, or maintenance needs using the digital inspection form (template available in the DRMC drive).
- Report urgent safety issues (e.g., fallen trees, electrical hazards, sewage issues) immediately to the Facilities Squad and the Executive.

### 4.2 Monthly Inspections
- Check fire extinguishers, first aid kits, and emergency equipment for expiration and completeness.
- Verify that gates, locks, and access systems are functioning.
- Inspect signage for legibility, visibility, and compliance with regulations.
- Inspect washroom and shower facilities for cleanliness and functionality; coordinate with Facilities Squad for deep cleaning or repairs as needed.

### 4.3 Seasonal Inspections
- Before opening for the season, conduct a comprehensive inspection of all sites, utility connections, and common areas.
- After closing for the season, conduct a final inspection and coordinate winterization of water systems and facilities.
- Coordinate with the Facilities Squad and Projects Squad for any major repairs or upgrades identified.

---

## 5. Rules Enforcement and Compliance

### 5.1 Campground Rules
- Ensure all campers receive a copy of the campground rules upon check-in (reference DRMC Code of Conduct & Facility Regulations).
- Post rules prominently at the campground entrance and in common areas.

### 5.2 Noise and Conduct
- Monitor campground activities for compliance with quiet hours and conduct rules.
- Address complaints promptly and professionally.
- For serious or repeated violations, issue written warnings and escalate to the Executive as needed, with documentation.

### 5.3 Fire Safety
- Enforce fire safety regulations, including burn bans, fire pit restrictions, and proper extinguishing procedures.
- Ensure fire extinguishers and other fire safety equipment are maintained and accessible.

### 5.4 Guest and Visitor Policy
- Maintain records of guests and visitors as required by club policy.
- Ensure non-members staying in the campground comply with all applicable rules and fee structures.

---

## 6. Financial Administration

### 6.1 Fee Collection
- Collect camping fees in accordance with the approved fee schedule.
- Issue receipts for all payments and forward funds to the Treasurer promptly.
- Maintain a ledger of all camping transactions for reconciliation.

### 6.2 Deposit Tracking
- If security or damage deposits are collected, track them separately and process refunds within the timeline established by club policy.
- Document any deductions for damages, with photographic evidence where possible.

### 6.3 Reporting
- Provide a monthly summary of campground occupancy, revenue, and notable incidents to the Executive.
- Coordinate with the Treasurer to ensure accurate financial reporting.

---

## 7. Emergency Preparedness

### 7.1 Emergency Contacts
- Maintain a current list of emergency contacts (local fire, police, ambulance, utilities, and key club contacts).
- Post emergency contact information prominently in the campground and provide to campers upon check-in.

### 7.2 Emergency Response
- Serve as the first point of contact for campground emergencies during operating hours.
- Follow the club's emergency response plan (to be developed) and coordinate with the Executive and relevant authorities as needed.
- Document all incidents and near-misses, reporting them to the Executive.

### 7.3 Weather and Natural Hazards
- Monitor weather forecasts and issue warnings to campers as needed (e.g., high winds, fire risk, extreme heat).
- Coordinate evacuations if necessary, in consultation with the Executive and local authorities.

---

## 8. Volunteer and Staff Coordination

### 8.1 Volunteer Support
- Recruit and coordinate volunteers to assist with campground operations (e.g., check-in, inspections, cleaning).
- Provide clear instructions and training to volunteers on their duties and club policies.
- Recognize volunteer contributions through appropriate channels.

### 8.2 Campground Host Program
- If applicable, oversee the Campground Host program, including recruitment, training, scheduling, and support.
- Ensure hosts understand their roles, responsibilities, and boundaries.
- Provide regular feedback and address any issues promptly.

---

## 9. Official Records and Reporting

### 9.1 Campground Records
- Maintain the following records:
  - Booking and reservation logs
  - Waitlist (if applicable)
  - Inspection checklists
  - Incident reports
  - Maintenance requests and work orders
  - Financial transaction logs (in coordination with Treasurer)

### 9.2 Record Retention
- Retain records according to the (future) Document Retention Policy.
- Regularly back up digital records to the DRMC cloud service.
- Archive records at the end of each season.

### 9.3 Executive Reporting
- Provide a report at each Executive meeting during the operating season summarizing:
  - Occupancy rates
  - Revenue summary
  - Maintenance issues and resolutions
  - Incidents or enforcement actions
  - Upcoming needs or concerns

---

## 10. Seasonal Operations

### 10.1 Opening Procedures
- Coordinate with the Facilities Squad to ensure all sites and facilities are ready for occupancy by the scheduled opening date.
- Verify that utilities (water, power, sewer) are operational.
- Confirm that all required supplies (e.g., toilet paper, soap, cleaning supplies) are stocked.

### 10.2 Operating Season
- Maintain regular presence in the campground during peak hours.
- Be available to campers for questions, concerns, and emergencies.
- Conduct regular inspections and address issues promptly.

### 10.3 Closing Procedures
- Coordinate with the Facilities Squad to winterize water systems and secure facilities.
- Conduct final inspections and document any end-of-season conditions.
- Process final payments and deposit refunds.
- Prepare a season summary report for the Executive, including recommendations for the following year.

---

## 11. Amendments and Review

This SOP may be amended by the Executive as needed, provided the changes are consistent with the Bylaws. Any substantive changes should be communicated to the Campground Manager and noted in the next Executive meeting minutes. A full review of this SOP shall occur annually in the first quarter.
