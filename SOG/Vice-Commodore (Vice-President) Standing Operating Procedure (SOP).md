# Vice-Commodore (Vice-President) Standing Operating Procedure (SOP)

**Document Version:** 1.0 (Draft) – March 2026  
**Status:** Work in Progress – Not yet approved by the Executive  
**Effective Date:** [To be determined upon approval]  
**Review Cycle:** Annually (or as needed)

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this Standing Operating Procedure (SOP) is to standardize the day‑to‑day execution of duties for the Vice-Commodore (Vice-President) role within the Dalhousie Regional Marina Club (DRMC). This document provides step‑by‑step instructions to ensure consistency, preserve institutional knowledge, and facilitate smooth transitions when the role changes hands.

This procedure is supplementary to the Bylaws and must be followed by the Vice-Commodore and any volunteers performing the duties described herein.

---

## 1. Executive Support and Succession

### 1.1 Support to the Commodore
- Assist the Commodore in the execution of their duties as requested.
- Serve as a sounding board for strategic decisions and operational matters.
- Step in to perform Commodore duties during temporary absences (e.g., vacation, illness) as outlined in Section 2.

### 1.2 Succession Readiness
- Maintain familiarity with the Commodore SOP and related responsibilities to ensure a smooth transition if succession becomes necessary.
- Attend external meetings and events alongside the Commodore to build relationships and develop institutional knowledge.
- Maintain a Vice-Commodore's handover folder containing key contacts, ongoing projects, and role-specific information.

---

## 2. Acting Commodore Duties

### 2.1 Temporary Assumption of Duties
- In the absence of the Commodore, assume the responsibilities of the Commodore for:
  - Presiding over Executive and General Meetings
  - Representing the club at external events
  - Signing documents requiring the Commodore's signature
- Notify the Executive when assuming Commodore duties and upon the Commodore's return.
- Ensure that any significant decisions made while acting as Commodore are communicated to the Commodore promptly.

### 2.2 Vacancy in the Commodore Role
- Should the Commodore position become vacant (resignation, removal, or inability to serve), assume the role on an interim basis until a successor is elected or appointed per the Bylaws.
- Coordinate with the Governance & Nominating Committee to initiate the process of filling the vacancy.

---

## 3. Facilities and Operations Oversight

### 3.1 Facilities Coordination
- Serve as the Executive liaison to the Facilities Squad and Projects Squad.
- Monitor the progress of facility maintenance and capital projects, ensuring they remain on schedule and within budget.
- Escalate significant delays, cost overruns, or safety concerns to the Executive.

### 3.2 Project Management Support
- Assist squad leads in developing project proposals using the DRMC Project Proposal Template.
- Track active projects and provide regular status updates to the Executive.
- Ensure that project documentation is maintained and archived upon completion.

### 3.3 Health and Safety
- Work with the Harbour Master and Facilities Squad to ensure safety inspection findings are addressed in a timely manner.
- Support the development and implementation of any future health and safety policies or emergency response plans.
- Coordinate with relevant officers to ensure all volunteers and members are aware of safety protocols.

---

## 4. Membership and Volunteer Engagement

### 4.1 Volunteer Coordination
- Maintain an up-to-date inventory of volunteer roles and current assignments.
- Work with officers and squad leads to identify volunteer needs and recruit members to fill gaps.
- Recognize volunteer contributions through appropriate channels (e.g., newsletters, annual awards).

### 4.2 Member Onboarding and Orientation
- Coordinate with the Secretary and Harbour Master to ensure new members receive a proper orientation to club facilities, policies, and procedures.
- Develop and maintain a new member welcome package, including key documents and contact information.
- Conduct or arrange for new member orientation sessions as needed.

### 4.3 Member Engagement
- Support the Commodore in fostering a positive and inclusive club culture.
- Assist in planning and promoting social events, work parties, and other member engagement activities.
- Serve as a point of contact for member concerns, directing them to the appropriate officer or escalating to the Commodore as needed.

---

## 5. Committee and Squad Support

### 5.1 Standing Committee Liaison
- Serve as the Executive liaison to the Governance & Nominating Committee.
- Attend committee meetings as appropriate to provide support and ensure alignment with Executive priorities.

### 5.2 Squad Oversight
- Ensure that each squad (e.g., Facilities, Projects, Social) has clear terms of reference and reporting expectations.
- Assist squads in resolving operational challenges and securing necessary resources.
- Report squad activities and accomplishments to the Executive on a regular basis.

---

## 6. Official Records and Reporting

### 6.1 Project and Facilities Records
- Maintain a centralized log of all active and completed projects, including:
  - Project name
  - Squad lead
  - Start date
  - Target completion date
  - Current status
  - Budget information
- Ensure project files are stored in the DRMC digital drive for institutional continuity.

### 6.2 Volunteer Records
- Maintain a log of volunteer roles, assignments, and contact information.
- Coordinate with the Secretary to ensure volunteer records are integrated with the official membership database as needed.

### 6.3 Executive Reporting
- Provide a brief report at each Executive meeting summarizing:
  - Facilities and project updates
  - Volunteer engagement activities
  - Any issues requiring Executive attention

---

## 7. Amendments and Review

This SOP may be amended by the Executive as needed, provided the changes are consistent with the Bylaws. Any substantive changes should be communicated to the Vice-Commodore and noted in the next Executive meeting minutes. A full review of this SOP shall occur annually in the first quarter.
